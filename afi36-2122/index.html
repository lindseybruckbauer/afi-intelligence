    AFI 36-2122: Interservice Officers on Exchange Duty with United States Air Force

    OPR: AFPC/DPM | Effective: 30 June 2025 | Implements: DAFPD 36-21 | Supersedes: DAFI 36-2122, 3 August 2018

    Purpose & Scope

    This instruction governs the Interservice Officer Exchange Program, under which officers from other military services are attached to the U.S. Air Force to gain hands-on experience and understanding of USAF mission, policies, and procedures in areas of shared interest and mutual cooperation. It applies to officers in the Regular Air Force and does not apply to members of the United States Space Force, Air National Guard, or Air Force Reserve. It is not applicable to officers serving in Joint Staff, joint activities, or liaison roles as representatives of their parent military service (§2.1).

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • SAF/MR: Approves new and changed guidance pertaining to this publication (§1.1).
    • AF/A1P (Director of Air Force Military Force Management Policy): Oversees the Interservice Officer Exchange Program; serves as primary POC; maintains key contacts; collaborates with other services' exchange representatives; supports functional managers in developing memorandums of agreement (§1.2).
    • AF/A1M (Director of Manpower, Organization and Resources): Establishes manpower accounts for exchange program administrators when joint offsets are identified or the program is approved by AF/A1P (§1.3).
    • AFPC/DPMO (Operations and Special Duty Career Management): Initiates assignment processes; selects officers for exchange requirements; coordinates with AFDW Manpower for PASCODEs; obtains position descriptions from functional managers (§1.4).
    • AFDW/A1 (Manpower, Personnel and Services): Retrieves required documents and assists functional managers; manages Officer Evaluation System actions; provides MAJCOM-level administrative support including PME nominations, awards/decorations, assignment entitlement requests, and separation/courts-martial actions; updates PASCODEs after validating senior rater ID codes (§1.5).
    • AFDW/A1M (Manpower and Resources): Reviews and recommends revisions to program documents; implements unit manpower document changes; establishes PASCODEs for exchange officers (§1.6).
    • AFDW/FM (Financial Analysis): Establishes financial accounts for exchange programs; determines funding requirements including TDY and professional gear costs; coordinates inter-MAJCOM funding transfers (§1.7).
    • AFDW/XP (Plans and Readiness): Reviews skills, experiences, and perspectives of interservice officers to strategically leverage their expertise (§1.8).
    • AFDW/A1MP (Programs Development Division): Facilitates management of manpower inputs for the SPPBE process (§1.9).
    • Headquarters Air Force Functional Managers: Coordinate with service counterparts; prepare memorandums of agreement and position descriptions; submit requisitions; revalidate position descriptions; notify agencies of program cancellations (§1.10).

    Key Requirements

    1. Proposals to establish new interservice exchange programs must ensure no increase in USAF end strength or Joint Duty Assignment List; USAF offset officers must be identified (§1.10.1). (T-0)
    2. New program proposals must be submitted to the respective MAJCOM Commander with comprehensive justification (§2.2.1). (T-1)
    3. New position proposals in existing programs must include a proposed station report (Attachment 2) and detailed position description (Attachment 3) identifying the designated interservice program POC (§2.2.2). (T-1)
    4. Gaining units must utilize inbound officer information to make necessary arrival arrangements (§2.3). (T-2)
    5. Exchange officers are reported as attached—not assigned—to Air Force units; strength accountability remains with the parent military service (§2.5). (T-2, T-1)
    6. Transfer of exchange officers to a different unit requires prior coordination and approval from AFPC/DPMO (§2.6). (T-1)
    7. Standard tour length is 36 months (including travel/training time); extensions up to 12 additional months require consent of both USAF and parent service (§2.8.1).
    8. Tour length adjustments require a written request through the USAF operational commander, reviewed by the wing commander and forwarded to AFPC/DPMO, then coordinated with the parent service (§2.8.2). (T-1, T-2)
    9. PCS costs to/from and during the Air Force duty station are funded by the parent military service (§2.9.2.1–2.9.2.2). (T-1)
    10. USAF funds TDY travel/per diem costs when performed on behalf of the USAF (§2.9.2.3). (T-1)
    11. All records must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule (opening paragraph).
    12. MAJCOM-level supplements require Human Resource Management Strategic Board approval prior to certification (opening paragraph).

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. Incomplete financial responsibilities section: The text of §2.9.2.3 (TDY funding by USAF) is truncated; the full scope of financial responsibilities—including any provisions beyond TDY—is not fully captured in the available text.
    2. Hostilities recall process undefined: §2.7 states recall of exchange officers during hostilities "should be mutually agreed upon" but provides no defined process, timeline, or escalation authority if the services disagree.
    3. Attachment 2 and Attachment 3 content not included: The instruction references a proposed station report (Attachment 2) and position description format (Attachment 3) as required documents, but their content is not available for review.
    4. Paragraph 1.10 documents cross-referenced but not enumerated: §1.5.1 and §1.6.1 reference "documents identified in paragraph 1.10" without specifying which particular documents, creating potential ambiguity about scope of AFDW responsibilities.
    5. Authority over exchange officers during hostilities: §2.7 states officers "follow the orders and directives issued by that unit" but does not address command authority limitations or UCMJ applicability for non-AF officers, potentially conflicting with parent-service prerogatives.
    6. Scope exclusion for Space Force not explained: The publication excludes USSF members but does not clarify whether a separate instruction governs Space Force interservice exchanges or whether this is an oversight given DAF organizational structure.
