    AFI 36-2135: Joint Officer Management

    OPR: AF/A1P | Effective: 4 November 2020 (Incorporating Change 1, 12 November 2024) | Implements: DAFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel | Supersedes: AFI 36-2135, 26 July 2018

    Purpose & Scope

    AFI 36-2135 establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for the Joint Officer Management program, ensuring a significant number of officers are educated, trained, and experienced in joint matters to enhance joint warfighting capability. It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) officers. It does not apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory.

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Manpower, Personnel and Services): Collaborates with AF/RE and NGB/CF to develop joint officer management personnel policy; develops procedures for Joint Qualification System compliance; supports DoD boards on joint officer management; manages joint qualification designation process (Levels II, III, IV); monitors JQO promotions; reviews and recommends DoD waiver requests to USD(P&R) (§2.1).
    • AF/A1D (Directorate of Force Development): Charges development teams with vectoring quality officers to S-JDA positions to meet promotion objectives and tour lengths; develops policies for officer attendance at Joint and Combined Warfighting Schools; ensures JPME Phase I/II curricula meet CJCSI 1800.01F criteria (§2.2).
    • AF/A1M (Directorate of Manpower, Organization and Resources): Ensures bi-annual updates of Air Force Manpower Programming and Execution System for JDA positions; coordinates updates with AFPC, ARPC, and NGB (§2.3).
    • AF/A1P (Directorate of Military Force Management Policy): Develops Air Force policy for compliance with 10 USC §§661–668, DoDI 1300.19, and CJCSI 1330.05B; provides joint officer management reports through CJCS to SecDef; nominates qualifying officers for Level II and III JQO designation (§2.4).
    • AFPC / ARPC / AF/REG / ANG: Assign officers to S-JDA positions with prescribed minimum tour lengths (§1.3).
    • AF/JAX (Professional Development Office, Office of the Judge Advocate General): Serves as assignment authority for judge advocate officers, implementing and, where necessary, waiving joint officer policies on behalf of AF/JA (§1.5).
    • USD(P&R): Designates officers as joint qualified based on education, training, and experience (§1.2).
    • Development Teams: Vector quality officers to S-JDA ensuring promotion rates are not less than same-grade/competitive-category peers (T-0); consider career milestones and tour completion timelines (§2.2.1).

    Key Requirements

    1. Officers nominated for JQO status must have no quality force indicators (unfavorable information files, control roster, referral OPRs, NJP, courts-martial) in their records. (T-0) (§1.2.1)
    2. Prior to JQO nomination, officers must complete Level III requirements, hold a master's degree, and complete requisite PME (intermediate developmental education for majors; senior developmental education for LtCol/LtCol-selects, RegAF and AFR only). (T-0) (§1.2.2)
    3. Only officers in the grade of major or above may be designated JQO on the Active Duty List or Reserve Active Status List. (§1.2.4)
    4. RegAF officers must be designated JQO before appointment to brigadier general, unless waived. (T-0) (§1.2.5)
    5. RegAF and ANG/AFR general officers assigned to S-JDA serve a minimum of 2 years; all other RegAF officers serve a minimum of 3 years; ANG and AFR personnel serve 2 years. (§1.3)
    6. Critical S-JDA positions must be filled with JQOs; S-JDA positions at LtCol or above must be filled with officers holding appropriate joint qualification levels. (T-0) (§1.3.1)
    7. National Defense University graduates must be placed in joint assignments per DAFI 36-2110, DoDI 1300.19, and CJCSI 1330.05. (§1.3.2)
    8. TDY periods over 30 consecutive days away from the joint organization for service duties are not counted toward the 24-month JDA credit minimum, unless waived by the joint organization. (§1.3.3)
    9. Promotion rates for officers serving/having served on the Joint Staff or OSD staff must, as a group, be not less than rates for same-grade Air Staff or Space Staff officers. (§1.4.1)
    10. Bulk joint tour length waivers are authorized only for officers departing JDA for boarded command positions (G-series orders) or senior developmental education; all other waivers are by exception only with specific justification. (T-0) (§1.6)
    11. Development teams must vector officers to S-JDA such that their promotion rates are not less than same-grade/competitive-category peers. (T-0) (§2.2.1.2)
    12. AF Manpower Programming and Execution System must be updated bi-annually to accurately reflect JDA positions. (T-1) (§2.3.1)

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. Incomplete text for §2.4.4: The document text cuts off mid-section at §2.4.4, leaving potentially significant AF/A1P responsibilities unaddressed in the extracted content.
    2. ANG PME exemption scope unclear: Section 1.2.2 exempts ANG officers from the requisite PME requirement for JQO nomination but does not clarify whether the master's degree requirement is also relaxed or what alternative criteria apply. The "case-by-case" exception language lacks defined approval authority.
    3. Conflicting tour length provisions: Section 1.3 states ANG/AFR personnel serve 2-year tours, while RegAF general officers also serve 2 years but other RegAF serve 3 years. The interaction between component-based and grade-based minimums for AFR general officers could be clearer.
    4. Waiver authority for non-tiered items: Waiver requests for non-tiered compliance items are routed to AFPC/DP3AM, but the specific approval authority (as distinct from the routing destination) is not explicitly named.
    5. Space Force exclusion without transition guidance: The publication explicitly excludes USSF but does not address transitional scenarios for officers who transfer between DAF components or whose joint records originated under Air Force management.
