    AFGM 2026-36-2033: Air Force Guidance Memorandum Reimplementing Warrant Officers

    OPR: AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services) | Effective: 4 February 2026 | Implements: DAFPD 36-20, DAFPD 36-21, DAFPD 36-24, DAFPD 36-25, DAFPD 36-26, DAFPD 36-29, DAFPD 36-30, DAFPD 36-32 | Supersedes: Not specified

    Purpose & Scope

    This AFGM immediately establishes warrant officers (WO) in the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve (AFR), and Air National Guard (ANG). It promulgates personnel policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for managing warrant officers throughout the entire personnel life cycle, including accessions, grade structure, classification, evaluations, promotions, standards, entitlements, and separations (Chapter 1). Compliance is mandatory for all AF and DAF organizations. Where inconsistent with other AF/DAF publications, this memorandum prevails per DAFMAN 90-161. The memorandum becomes void one year from its date or upon publication of a permanent interim change or rewrite, whichever is earlier.

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • SAF/MR (Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Manpower and Reserve Affairs): Signed and issued the AFGM by order of the Secretary of the Air Force.
    • AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services): Serves as the point of contact for policy questions; functions as the de facto OPR for the guidance.
    • AF/RE (Chief of Air Force Reserve): Point of contact for AFR-related WO policy questions (Chapter 1).
    • NGB/CF (Director of the Air National Guard): Point of contact for ANG-related WO policy questions; may further restrict maximum accession age (§4.1.5).
    • AFRS Recruiting Operations Director & 367th Recruiting Group Recruiting Operations Director: May further restrict the maximum accession age based on component needs (§4.1.5).
    • AFRC/A6: Conducts WO selection boards for AFR applicants (§4.1.11).
    • AETC/SG (AETC Office of the Command Surgeon General): Provides concurrence for postpartum members seeking early return to WOTS physical requirements (§4.1.7).
    • AFRS/RSG (Accession Medical Waiver Division): Processes pregnancy-related medical waivers per DAFMAN 48-123 (§4.1.7).
    • The President / Secretary of War (delegated): Authority to appoint warrant officers by warrant (W-1) and chief warrant officers by commission (W-2 through W-5) (§3.2).
    • Unit Commanders: Endorse AF IMT 1288 for AFR WO applicants (§4.1.11); approve DAF Form 357 Family Care Certifications (§4.1.9).
    • Tier Waiver Approval Authorities: Approve waiver requests for tiered compliance items through the chain of command; non-tiered items routed to the AFGM OPR.

    Key Requirements

    1. Citizenship: All WO applicants must be United States citizens (§4.1.1).
    2. Minimum Age: Applicants must be at least 18 years of age for original appointment (T-1) (§4.1.5).
    3. Retirement Eligibility Acknowledgment: Applicants who cannot qualify for retirement before removal from active status must acknowledge this in writing and request a waiver (T-1) (§4.1.5).
    4. Medical Qualification: Applicants must be medically qualified or hold an approved waiver per DAFMAN 48-123 and must obtain an AF Form 422 (valid 12 months for WOTS application) (§4.1.6).
    5. Physical Fitness: All applicants must meet fitness, height, and weight standards per DAFMAN 36-2905 and DoDI 1308.03 (T-0) (§4.1.6.1).
    6. Dress & Appearance: Applicants must meet standards in DAFI 36-2903 (§4.1.6.2).
    7. Drug & Alcohol Certification: All accession applicants must complete AF Form 2030, except those currently holding status in an air component (T-1) (§4.1.10).
    8. Security Clearance: Applicants must hold at least a Secret clearance and initiate Top Secret/SCI procedures per DODM 5200.02_DAFMAN 16-1405; certain specialties require a favorable CI polygraph (§4.1.13).
    9. Family Care Plan: Single, divorced, separated applicants with dependents, or those married to a military/common-law spouse, require a commander-approved DAF Form 357 prior to accession (T-3) (§4.1.9).
    10. Inter-Service Transfer: WOs transferring from another service are originally appointed in the AF without interruption; existing ADSCs and financial obligations carry over (T-0) (§4.1.14–4.1.14.2).
    11. Initial Career Fields: WOs will initially serve only in 17W (Warfighter Communications & IT Systems Operations) and 17Y (Cyber Effects & Warfare Operations) (Chapter 2).
    12. Grade Structure: Five WO grades (W-1 through W-5); W-1 appointed by warrant, W-2 through W-5 by commission (§3.1–3.2).
    13. Pregnancy Policy: Members may apply to WOTS while pregnant but may not complete training while pregnant or for 6 months postpartum (with waiver exceptions requiring physician and AETC/SG approval) (§4.1.7).
    14. Records Management: All records created under this publication must comply with AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule.

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. OPR Designation: The memorandum's OPR is not explicitly identified in the header or certifying block. Chapter 1 lists AF/A1, AF/RE, and NGB/CF as points of contact for questions, but formal OPR assignment is ambiguous.
    2. Appointment Authority Anachronism: Section 3.2 states appointment authority was delegated by the President to the "Secretary of War"—a title abolished in 1947. It is unclear whether this references the Secretary of Defense, Secretary of the Air Force, or is an unrevised carryover from historical statutory language (10 U.S.C.).
    3. Promotion, Evaluation, Separation, Entitlements, and Standards: The AFGM's subject line and implementing directives list DAFPD 36-24 (Evaluations), 36-25 (Promotions), 36-29 (Standards), 36-30 (Entitlements), and 36-32 (Retirements/Separations), yet the extracted text contains no substantive guidance on WO evaluation systems, promotion board procedures/timelines, WO-specific standards, entitlements, or separation/retirement rules. These life-cycle areas remain unaddressed in the available text.
    4. WOTS Curriculum and Training Pipeline: The memorandum references Warrant Officer Training School (WOTS) but provides no detail on training location, duration, curriculum, or graduation requirements.
    5. Non-Prior Service Pathway: Section 4.1.12 states boards are open to non-prior service applicants, but no distinct accession process, training pipeline, or eligibility criteria differentiating them from prior-service applicants are articulated.
    6. Career Progression and Utilization: Beyond initial career field designation (17W/17Y), there is no guidance on WO duty positions, command authority limitations, assignment processes, or career field expansion criteria.
    7. AFR Conditional Release Authority: Section 4.1.14.5 is truncated mid-sentence ("conditional release authority is Deputy Commander, Air Fo…"), leaving the AFR IST conditional release authority incomplete.
