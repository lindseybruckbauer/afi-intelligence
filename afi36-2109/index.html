    AFI 36-2109: Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force, Senior Enlisted Leadership Management and Air Force Enlisted Council

    OPR: AF/CCC | Effective: 7 February 2025 (Administrative Change); base publication 5 January 2021, Incorporating Change 2, 1 February 2023 | Implements: AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Military Personnel | Supersedes: AFI 36-2109, 14 July 2014 | Certified by: SAF/MR

    Purpose & Scope

    This instruction establishes the selection and approval authority for the Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force (CMSAF), Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) positions, and group senior enlisted leader (SEL) positions. It provides guidance on the Air Force Senior Enlisted Leader Council (AFSELC), Air Force Career Field Manager Council (AFCFMC), Air Force Enlisted Council (AFEC), and Senior Enlisted Statesmen Council (SESC). The publication applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR). It does not apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory, and the publication may not be supplemented.

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • Chief of Staff of the Air Force (CSAF): Selects a Chief Master Sergeant to serve as CMSAF and determines CMSAF tenure (§1.1.1).
    • CMSAF: Serves as personal advisor to SecAF and CSAF on enlisted welfare, readiness, morale, utilization, and professional development (§1.2); travels to engage Airmen and families (§1.2.1); testifies before Congress (§1.2.3); consults with sister-service senior enlisted advisors (§1.2.4); serves as waiver authority for non-tiered compliance items (opening paragraph).
    • AF/A1LE (Chiefs' Group): Announces the assignment of the CMSAF position (§1.1.2).
    • AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services): Develops guidance and procedures for CMSAF, senior enlisted leadership management, and AFEC programs in collaboration with AF/RE and NGB/CF (opening paragraph).
    • CCMs (Command Chief Master Sergeants): Hold roles and responsibilities varying by echelon—wing, host wing, tenant unit, deployed, NAF, MAJCOM, joint base (lead/tenant), and joint/combined commands (Chapter 3, §§3.1–3.13).
    • Group SELs: Serve as senior enlisted leaders at group level with roles and responsibilities defined in Chapters 4–5 (§§4.1–4.2, 5.1–5.2).
    • AFSELC: Advises on senior enlisted matters; includes ANG Enlisted Field Advisory Council and AFR Senior Enlisted Leader Council (Chapter 7, §§7.1–7.5).
    • AFCFMC: Added council providing career field manager advisory functions (Chapter 8, §§8.1–8.3).
    • AFEC: Manages Air Force Enlisted Council functions including Outstanding Airmen of the Year (OAY) recognition (Chapter 9, §§9.1–9.5).
    • SESC (Senior Enlisted Statesmen Council): Advisory body of former CMSAFs (Chapter 10, §§10.1–10.3).

    Key Requirements

    1. The CMSAF position is coded Distinguished Visitor Code 4 per DoD Order of Precedence as the senior enlisted advisor to the CSAF. (T-0) (§1.1.3)
    2. Former CMSAFs take precedence before all other retired members of the same grade per DoD Order of Precedence. (T-0) (§1.1.4)
    3. The CMSAF wears distinctive rank insignia separate from CMSgt and is addressed as "Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force" or "Chief." (§1.1.1)
    4. The CMSAF reporting identifier is 9C000; special basic and retired pay rates are set by law. (§1.1.2.1–1.1.2.2)
    5. CCM selection, assignment (RegAF, ANG, AFR), tenure, and joint/combined nominations must follow processes in Chapter 2 (§§2.1–2.7).
    6. Group SEL selection must follow processes in Chapter 4, including ANG-specific funding guidance. (§4.1–4.2, Table 4.1)
    7. Housing designation as Key and Essential is addressed in Chapter 6. (§6.1)
    8. Waiver requests for tiered compliance items route through the chain of command to the appropriate Tier waiver authority; non-tiered items route to the CMSAF. (Opening paragraph)
    9. Each Outstanding Airmen of the Year (OAY) honoree is authorized to bring a spouse, family member, or one person of their choosing. (§9.3)
    10. All records generated under this publication must comply with AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule. (Opening paragraph)

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. CMSAF selection criteria not detailed: While §1.1.1 states the CSAF selects the CMSAF, no eligibility criteria, nomination process, or selection board procedures are specified—leaving the process entirely to CSAF discretion without documented standards.
    2. Housing guidance breadth unclear: Chapter 6 (§6.1) addresses "Key and Essential" housing but only the heading is visible in the extracted text; the scope and criteria for housing entitlements for CCMs and Group SELs cannot be verified from available text.
    3. AFCFMC integration with existing councils: Chapter 8 was added to codify the Career Field Manager Council, but the extracted text does not clarify how AFCFMC recommendations interact with or are adjudicated alongside AFSELC or AFEC recommendations, creating potential overlap.
    4. Space Force exclusion stated twice: The opening paragraph explicitly excludes USSF twice in nearly identical language, suggesting a drafting redundancy but no substantive conflict.
    5. Non-tiered waiver authority ambiguity: The CMSAF is designated as the alternate waiver authority for non-tiered compliance items, but the primary authority for such items is not explicitly named, potentially causing confusion in the waiver routing chain.
