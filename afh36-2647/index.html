    AFH 36-2647: Competency Modeling

    OPR: AETC/A3J | Effective: 8 February 2022 (Incorporating Change 1, 8 July 2025) | Implements: DAFPD 36-26, DAFMAN 36-2689 | Supersedes: AFH 36-2647, 19 April 2019

    Purpose & Scope

    This handbook provides reference material and instructional guidance on how and when competency models are planned, built, and executed in support of the USAF mission (§1.1). It defines the competency-based construct encompassing both foundational competencies (applicable to all Airmen) and occupational competencies (specific to career fields/workforce categories). The publication applies to Air Force military and civilian personnel, Air National Guard, and Air Force Reserve; it does not apply to the United States Space Force. Change 1 (8 July 2025) incorporates revisions to comply with Executive Orders 14151, 14148, and 14168 and updates references for currency.

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • SAF/MR (Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Manpower and Reserve Affairs): Functions as the Air Force's lead proponent for competency modeling and serves as approving official for DAF-level competency modeling policy (§2.1). Also serves as certifying authority for this publication.
    • AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services): Facilitates the Air Force's ability to identify, develop, measure, and track competencies across the service; serves as certifying authority for departmental-level competency modeling policy (§2.2).
    • AETC/CC (Commander, Air Education and Training Command): Develops and executes competency-modeling policies, plans, programs, processes, and other activities necessary to guide, measure, implement, assess, and oversee all aspects of competency modeling for the USAF (§2.3).
    • AETC/A3J (Force Development Competencies Division): Serves as the OPR and collaborates with functional authorities and career field managers to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4).
    • Functional Authorities, Career Field Managers, and Functional Managers: Collaborate with AETC/A3J to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4).
    • SECAF, CSAF, and CMSAF: Have full authority to make changes to foundational competencies (§3.2.2.1).
    • AF Futures: Has delegated authority to identify competencies for inclusion within foundational competencies based on future needs of the USAF (§3.2.2.2).

    Key Requirements

    1. Competency models must describe observable and measurable patterns of knowledge, skills, abilities, and other characteristics (KSAOs) required for mission success (§1.1).
    2. Occupational competency models must be applicable to all components (RegAF, AFR, ANG) within a functional community (§1.2).
    3. Foundational competencies are universally applicable to all Airmen (officer, enlisted, and civilian) and serve as the underpinning construct of force development (§3.1.2.1).
    4. Occupational competency model development must follow a distinct process with continued involvement of career field subject matter experts (§3.2.4.1).
    5. Foundational competency development must follow a validated scientific methodology (four-phase approach involving review, behavioral event interviews, survey validation, and psychometric reduction) (§3.2.1.1–3.2.1.4).
    6. Foundational competencies are organized into four categories: Developing Self, Developing Others, Developing Ideas, and Developing Organizations (§3.2.3).
    7. Supplements to this publication at any level must be reviewed by the OPR prior to certification, approval, and implementation.
    8. All records generated from processes in this publication must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed of per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule.
    9. Revisions must comply with Executive Orders 14151, 14148, and 14168 (Summary of Changes).

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. Deleted attachments without explanation of replacement: Attachments 3 through 7 are marked "(DELETED)" with no indication of whether their content was relocated, superseded, or eliminated, potentially leaving gaps in previously provided reference material.
    2. Unclear enforcement mechanism: As a handbook (AFH) rather than an instruction (AFI), the publication provides guidance and reference material but does not clearly delineate mandatory compliance obligations versus recommended practices, which may create ambiguity about enforceability.
    3. AF Futures delegation scope undefined: Section 3.2.2.2 states AF Futures has "delegated authority" to identify competencies for foundational models but does not specify the source, scope, or limitations of that delegation.
    4. Space Force exclusion without alternative: The publication explicitly excludes the USSF but does not reference a corresponding Space Force competency modeling publication, leaving it unclear whether equivalent guidance exists for Guardians.
    5. Limited detail on occupational model governance: While §2.4 assigns collaboration responsibilities, the document (within the parsed text) does not clearly define approval authority for new or modified occupational competency models or dispute resolution when functional authorities and AETC/A3J disagree.
