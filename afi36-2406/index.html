    AFI 36-2406: Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems

    OPR: AF/A1PPP | Effective: 22 August 2025 (Incorporating Change 1, 27 May 2026) | Implements: DAFPD 36-24, Military Evaluations | Supersedes: AFI 36-2406, 6 August 2024

    Purpose & Scope

    AFI 36-2406 provides guidance and procedures for implementing the United States Air Force Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems, including how to prepare, submit, and manage evaluation forms (§1.1). It covers the performance feedback process, officer performance evaluations (Officer ALQ Evaluations), and enlisted ALQ evaluations/Enlisted Performance Briefs (EPBs). The publication applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard; it does not apply to the United States Space Force (USSF). Compliance is mandatory. The instruction was developed collaboratively between AF/A1, AF/RE, and NGB/CF.

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • SAF/MR: Certifying authority for the publication.
    • AF/A1PPP (Promotions and Evaluations Policy Branch): Office of primary responsibility; approval authority (with AF/A1P concurrence) for non-tiered waiver requests and locally created versions of prescribed forms.
    • AFPC/DPPSP (Promotions, Evaluations and Recognitions Policy Branch): Approval authority for waiver requests on non-tiered items (with AF/A1P concurrence); approval authority for supplements initiated at MAJCOM level or below; approval authority for non-rated periods of supervision for RegAF members; sole authority to correct digitally signed evaluations after transmission to AFPC (§1.13, Table rules).
    • ARPC/PB (Promotion Board Secretariat): Receives published supplements; ARPC/DPTSE serves as approval authority for ARC members regarding non-rated periods of supervision. ARPC/PB may approve changes up to one duty day prior to central selection boards in extreme circumstances.
    • AFPC MLR Office: Approves exceptions to maintain integrity in the Officer Evaluation System and ensure fair consideration; may approve changes up to one duty day prior to central selection boards in extreme circumstances.
    • AF/REP (Air Force Reserve Directorate of Personnel) and NGB/A1P (National Guard Bureau Force Management Division): Concurrence authorities for non-tiered waiver requests applicable to Reserve and ANG members, respectively.
    • Wing Commander (or Deputy Wing Commander equivalent): Final approval/disapproval authority when a lower approval authority recommends disapproval of a request (delegable no lower than deputy wing commander or equivalent).
    • CSAF: Sole authority to approve exceptions to certain policies; HAF Deputy Chief of Staff, Personnel, may exercise similar authority for general officer management levels.
    • Raters/Evaluators: Required to meet evaluator requirements (§1.5), conduct performance feedback (Ch. 2), prepare evaluations IAW prescribed timelines and formats, and provide mandatory comments where required (§1.9).
    • Higher Level Reviewers (HLRs): Review evaluations above the rater level; authorized to use stratification statements originating between rater and HLR (§3.15.6.7.2); responsibilities detailed in §3.14 and §4.13.
    • Referral Reviewers: May grant extensions to ratees for rebuttals (e.g., when Area Defense Counsel unavailable or rebuttal exceeds 10 pages) (§1.11).
    • AFELM/CC (on G-series orders): Authorized to sign ALQ evaluations in lieu of the forced distributor when the forced distributor signs the MEL in joint agencies (§4.12.5.4).
    • ERAB (Evaluation Review Appeal Board): Authorized to modify evaluations that differ from applicant's request, including directing corrections in lieu of voiding reports (§10.2.4).
    • Ratees: Acknowledge evaluations (§3.18); may grant access to completed feedback forms at their discretion (§2.9.3).

    Key Requirements

    1. Mandatory Compliance: Compliance with this publication is mandatory; requirements are tiered (T-0 through T-3) with waiver requests routed through the chain of command to the appropriate tier waiver approval authority (Preamble).
    2. Physical Fitness Assessment Documentation: Physical fitness assessment scores must be documented on officer and enlisted evaluations (Change 1, Summary of Changes).
    3. Performance Feedback Sessions: Documented performance feedback assessments are required per Table 2.1 timelines; failure to conduct or document feedback is addressed at §2.10. The updated feedback system uses the newly prescribed AF Form 717, Performance Objectives and Feedback Form.
    4. Evaluation Preparation and Submission: Evaluations must be prepared, processed, and submitted IAW Chapter 1 procedures, including proper FROM/THRU dates, Static Close-out Dates (SCODs), and rater supervision day requirements (§§3.10–3.12, 4.6–4.8).
    5. Referral Evaluations: Evaluations containing adverse or derogatory information must follow the referral process, including providing the ratee opportunity to respond; rebuttals are limited to 10 pages (§1.11, Figures 1.1–1.2).
    6. Mandatory Comments: Evaluators must include mandatory comments as specified in §1.9; waiver of mandatory comments related to criminal convictions requires written finding by the approval authority that comments are not in the best interests of the Air Force and would unduly harm the ratee (§1.8.1.1.1.3.1).
    7. Prohibited Considerations and Comments: Evaluators must avoid prohibited considerations and comments per §1.12 (general) and §3.16 (officer-specific).
    8. Stratifications: Officer stratification quotas and peer groups must conform to guidance in §3.15 and Table 3.4; Joint and DAF SNCO stratifications follow updated guidance (Change 1).
    9. Rating Chain and Evaluator Requirements: Rating chain deviations must be documented and approved (§1.7); single evaluator requirements updated for enlisted members (Change 1); multiple general officers are now authorized to sign evaluations.
    10. Supplements: Field agencies (MAJCOM, NAF, Wing, FOA) will not publish supplements that change basic policies or duplicate instruction text; supplements require AF/A1PP and AFPC/DPPSP approval before publication.
    11. Records Management: All records generated must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed IAW the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule.
    12. Privacy Act Compliance: Collection and maintenance of evaluation information is authorized by DoDI 5400.11; applicable SORNs (F036 AF PC A and F036 AFPC T) must be observed.

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. Approval authority variability (§1.4.11): The text states "the documentation and/or approval authority required will vary depending on the nature of the circumstances" without providing a definitive matrix, creating potential ambiguity about which authority applies in edge cases.
    2. "Extreme circumstances" undefined: Both AFPC MLR and ARPC/PB may approve late changes to evaluations "in extreme circumstances and on a case-by-case basis," but neither the criteria nor examples of what constitutes "extreme circumstances" are provided in the extracted text.
    3. Delegation limits for waiver disapproval: The wing commander or equivalent is the final approval/disapproval authority when a lower authority recommends disapproval, delegable no lower than deputy wing commander—but the instruction does not fully clarify what happens if no wing-level equivalent exists in non-standard organizational structures (e.g., certain DRUs or joint billets).
    4. USSF exclusion scope: The instruction explicitly excludes the USSF but does not address how evaluations are handled for USAF members serving in USSF-led organizations or cross-component assignments, which may require coordination with USSF-specific guidance not referenced here.
    5. Deleted tables (Tables 2.3–2.5): Three tables in Chapter 2 are marked "DELETED" without replacement or cross-reference to where superseding guidance resides, potentially leaving a gap in procedural detail for performance feedback preparation.
