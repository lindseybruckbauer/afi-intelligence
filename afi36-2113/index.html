    AFI 36-2113: THE FIRST SERGEANT

    OPR: HQ USAF/CCC | Effective: 11 June 2020 | Implements: AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel | Supersedes: AFI 36-2113, 18 Nov 2014

    Purpose & Scope

    This AFI provides guidance for the management of Air Force first sergeants, establishing policy for the First Sergeant Program including eligibility, selection, classification, training, tenure, assignment, withdrawal, additional duty first sergeants, first sergeant councils, the first sergeant device, and manpower/utilization. It applies to Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) personnel. The Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services develops policy for the program in collaboration with the Chief of Air Force Reserve and the Director of the Air National Guard. Compliance is mandatory, and tiered waiver authorities (T-0 through T-3) apply to wing/unit-level requirements (Chapter 1; preamble).

    Authority & Responsibilities

    • USAF First Sergeant Special Duty Manager (SDM) (§2.1): Serves as the approval authority for RegAF requests to serve beyond four years in the 8F000 SDI (§2.1.13); serves as the approval authority for RegAF applications for members currently serving in another SDI (§2.1.14).
    • AFPC/DP2W (AEF Operations and Readiness Division) (§2.2): Supports first sergeant operations and readiness functions.
    • AFPC First Sergeant Special Duty Assignment Functional Area Manager (FAM) (§2.3): Manages first sergeant special duty assignment functions.
    • AFPC/DP2OSS (§6.8.3): May waive assignment codes to facilitate needs of the Air Force.
    • Air Force Executive Talent Management Office – The Chief's Group (§2.4): Provides talent management support for the first sergeant program.
    • HQ Air Education and Training Command, Manpower and Personnel (§2.5): Supports training and manpower requirements for first sergeants.
    • USAF First Sergeant Academy Commandant (§2.6): Oversees first sergeant training and academy operations.
    • RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) (§2.7): Supports first sergeant management at the MAJCOM/DRU level.
    • RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command First Sergeant (§2.8): Serves as the MAJCOM/DRU-level first sergeant advisor.
    • RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Headquarters First Sergeant (§2.9): Performs first sergeant duties at headquarters level.
    • ANG Component Functional Manager (§2.10): Manages ANG-specific first sergeant program requirements.
    • AFR Component Functional Manager (§2.11): Manages AFR-specific first sergeant program requirements.
    • Host Wing CCM (§2.12): Provides oversight of first sergeant management at the wing level; recommends over-grade requests during rotation under applicable FY manning policy, with approval by WG/CC or equivalent (§6.4.7).
    • First Sergeant (§2.13): Works directly for and derives authority from the unit commander; serves as the dedicated focal point for readiness, health, morale, welfare, and quality of life; builds and maintains a mission-ready force; ensures the force understands commander's policies, goals, and objectives; responds to unit member needs 24/7; leads principally outside the operational chain of command (§§1.1, 2.13).
    • Unit Commander (§1.1.1, §1.3.1): The first sergeant works directly for and derives authority from the unit commander; the commander-first sergeant relationship is a key advisory partnership.
    • Tier Waiver Approval Authorities (preamble): Approve waiver requests for tiered compliance items routed through the chain of command; requestor's commander approves non-tiered items.

    Key Requirements

    1. First sergeants must meet minimum eligibility criteria and maintain qualifications to hold the 8F000 Special Duty Identifier (SDI) (§§3.1–3.3).
    2. RegAF, ANG, and AFR personnel must follow component-specific nomination and application processes for initial selection (§§4.1–4.5).
    3. Selected personnel must attend the USAF First Sergeant Academy prior to or upon assumption of duties (§§5.2–5.3).
    4. First sergeants must complete an installation orientation upon assignment (§5.4) and engage in continuous development (§5.5).
    5. RegAF first sergeants serve a standard 4-year tenure; extensions beyond four years require SDM approval (§§2.1.13, 6.1–6.2).
    6. ANG and AFR tenure and assignment follow component-specific procedures (§§6.3–6.4).
    7. Early withdrawal (voluntary or involuntary) from the 8F000 SDI must follow prescribed procedures, including use of sample memoranda (§6.7; Figures 6.1–6.2).
    8. Return to the 8F000 SDI after release must meet re-entry criteria (§6.6).
    9. Additional duty first sergeants must be appointed in accordance with Chapter 7 philosophy and full-time requirements (§§7.1–7.2).
    10. First sergeant councils must operate within established structure guidelines and may not function as private organizations (§§8.1–8.3).
    11. The first sergeant diamond device is authorized only for personnel properly serving in the first sergeant duty position (§9.1).
    12. Manpower authorizations, organization of assignment, and utilization standards must be followed; first sergeants are rated by the unit commander (§§10.1–10.7).
    13. First sergeants must maintain professional relationships and avoid favoritism or the appearance of impropriety per AFI 36-2909 (§1.2).
    14. Family association restrictions apply to first sergeant assignments (§10.8).
    15. All supplements to this publication must be routed to the OPR for coordination and approved by the Human Resource Management Strategic Board per AFI 36-8101 (preamble).

    Gaps & Ambiguities

    1. Authority of HQ USAF/CCC vs. AF/A1: The publication is both OPR'd and certified by HQ USAF/CCC, yet the preamble states the Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services (AF/A1) develops policy. The delineation of policy development authority versus program execution authority between these offices is not explicitly clarified.
    2. Over-grade approval authority (§6.4.7): The text states over-grade requests require "a recommendation by the CCM, approval authority, WG/CC or equivalent," but the comma-separated list makes it ambiguous whether all three endorsements are required or whether "approval authority" and "WG/CC or equivalent" are the same entity.
    3. Additional Duty First Sergeant governance (Chapter 7): The table of contents indicates limited coverage (two sections), and the document title implies comprehensive governance of all first sergeants, yet detailed standards, training requirements, and oversight mechanisms for additional duty first sergeants appear to receive significantly less treatment than primary-duty first sergeants.
    4. Security Clearance requirements (§10.5): The topic is listed but the extracted text does not elaborate on the specific clearance level required or the process for adjudication, leaving potential ambiguity for units with sensitive missions.
    5. Rater designation (§10.7): While stated that the commander rates the first sergeant, the instruction does not address potential conflicts when a first sergeant supports multiple units or is assigned under a group-level construct.
