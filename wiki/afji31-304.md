# AFJI 31-304: Enemy Prisoners of War, Retained Personnel, Civilian Internees and Other Detainees

**OPR:** Army Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations and Plans (DCSOPS) | **Effective:** 1 October 1997 (Certified Current 15 May 2025) | **Implements:** DoD Directive 2310.1 (August 1994); DoD Directive 5100.77 | **Supersedes:** AR 190-8 (1 June 1982); rescinds AR 190-57 (4 March 1987)

## Purpose & Scope

This multi-service regulation (AR 190-8 / OPNAVINST 3461.6 / AFJI 31-304 / MCO 3461.1) provides policy, procedures, and responsibilities for the administration, treatment, employment, and compensation of enemy prisoners of war (EPW), retained personnel (RP), civilian internees (CI), and other detainees (OD) in the custody of U.S. Armed Forces, including persons held during military operations other than war (para 1-1). It implements international law—principally the four 1949 Geneva Conventions—and applies to the Army, Navy, Air Force, and Marine Corps, including Reserve components when lawfully ordered to active duty under Title 10 U.S.C. In the event of conflicts between this regulation and the Geneva Conventions, the Geneva Conventions take precedence (para 1-1b(4)).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretaries of the Military Departments:** Develop internal policies consistent with this regulation; ensure Geneva Convention training per DoDD 5100.77; ensure prompt reporting and investigation of suspected law-of-war violations; conduct periodic program reviews (para 1-4a).
- **Secretary of the Army (SA):** Serves as DoD Executive Agent for administering the EPW, CI, and RP Program; plans and develops policy in coordination with ASD-ISA (para 1-4b).
- **Army DCSOPS:** Primary HQDA staff responsibility for EPW/CI/RP programs; develops and disseminates policy guidance on treatment, care, accountability, legal status, and processing; provides HQDA staff supervision for the National Prisoner of War Information Center (NPWIC); assigns internment serial number (ISN) blocks (para 1-4c).
- **Combatant Commanders / Task Force Commanders / Joint Task Force Commanders:** Bear highlighted responsibilities for EPW/CI/RP operations (para 1-4g, referenced in Summary of Change).
- **Proponent (DCSOPS):** Authority to approve exceptions to this regulation consistent with controlling law; may delegate approval authority in writing to a division chief at the grade of colonel or civilian equivalent.
- **EPW Command (with medical advice):** Authorized to transfer injured, sick, and wounded EPW to other commands (para referenced in authority statements).
- **National Prisoner of War Information Center (NPWIC):** Central hub for EPW/CI/RP information and ISN management (para 1-7).
- **Branch PWIC:** Receives ISN block assignments and supports theater-level accountability (para 1-8).

## Key Requirements

1. **Compliance is mandatory** for all services and their Reserve components when on active duty under Title 10 U.S.C.
2. All EPW must be tagged with DD Form 2745 (EPW Capture Tag) upon capture (para 2-1b).
3. Tribunals must be conducted in accordance with established procedures to determine the legal status of detainees (para 1-6).
4. Suspected or alleged violations of the law of war must be promptly reported and investigated per DoDD 5100.77 (para 1-4a(3)).
5. Appropriate training on the principles of the Geneva Conventions must be provided to all service members (para 1-4a(2)).
6. EPW held aboard ship must be handled per established shipboard policy (para 2-1b).
7. OCONUS evacuation of EPW must follow updated evacuation policy (para 2-3).
8. Health and Comfort Packs may be used as a temporary substitution for Advance of Pay during short-term operations (para 3-4h).
9. Employment of EPW must comply with restrictions on authorized/unauthorized work, working conditions, and compensation rates (Chapter 4, paras 4-1 through 4-22).
10. Civilian internees must be afforded general protections, safety programs, and proper internment facility administration (Chapters 5–7).
11. Contract employment of EPW must follow updated rules and procedures (para 4-22).
12. Army supplementation of this regulation is prohibited without prior HQDA (DAMO-ODL) approval; Navy, Marine Corps, and Air Force supplementation is authorized but not required.
13. Each EPW is authorized up to 55 pounds of personal effects and property upon transfer (referenced in authority statements); certain additional items are excluded from this weight limit.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **OPR clarity for Air Force:** While DCSOPS is identified as the overall proponent, the Air Force-specific OPR and office of responsibility (referenced only as "HQ USAF/SPO" for supplementation) is not clearly delineated for program execution, potentially creating ambiguity in Air Force-internal accountability.
2. **Combatant/JTF Commander responsibilities:** The Summary of Change highlights these responsibilities (para 1-4g), but the extracted text does not provide the full enumeration, leaving the scope of their obligations partially undefined in the available text.
3. **Tribunal procedures:** While para 1-6 establishes procedures for conducting tribunals, the extracted text does not include the substantive details, making it difficult to assess completeness or alignment with subsequent DoD policy developments on detainee status determination.
4. **Operations other than war:** The regulation states it covers persons held during MOOTW (para 1-1a), but the structural focus is heavily oriented toward conventional EPW/CI scenarios; guidance specific to non-traditional detention contexts appears limited based on the available text.
5. **Potential datedness:** Despite being certified current through 15 May 2025, the regulation predates significant post-2001 detainee policy developments (e.g., DoDD 2310.01E, 2006 Detainee Treatment Act), raising questions about sufficiency for contemporary operations.
