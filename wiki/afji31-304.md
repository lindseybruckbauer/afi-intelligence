# AFJI 31-304: Enemy Prisoners of War, Retained Personnel, Civilian Internees and Other Detainees

**OPR:** Army Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations and Plans (DCSOPS) | **Effective:** 1 October 1997 (Certified Current 15 May 2025) | **Implements:** DoD Directive 2310.1 (August 1994); DoD Directive 5100.77 | **Supersedes:** AR 190-8 (1 June 1982); rescinds AR 190-57 (4 March 1987)

## Purpose & Scope
This multi-service regulation (AR 190-8 / OPNAVINST 3461.6 / AFJI 31-304 / MCO 3461.1) provides policy, procedures, and responsibilities for the administration, treatment, employment, and compensation of Enemy Prisoners of War (EPW), Retained Personnel (RP), Civilian Internees (CI), and Other Detainees (OD) in U.S. Armed Forces custody, including during military operations other than war. It implements international law—principally the four 1949 Geneva Conventions—and applies to the Army, Navy, Air Force, and Marine Corps, including their Reserve components when lawfully ordered to active duty under Title 10 U.S.C. In any conflict between this regulation and the Geneva Conventions, the Geneva Conventions take precedence (para 1-1b(4)).

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **Secretaries of the Military Departments:** Develop internal policies consistent with this regulation; ensure Geneva Convention training per DoD Directive 5100.77; ensure prompt reporting/investigation of suspected law-of-war violations; conduct periodic program and training reviews (para 1-4a).
- **Secretary of the Army (SA):** Serves as DoD Executive Agent for the EPW, CI, and RP Program; plans and develops policy in coordination with ASD-ISA (para 1-4b).
- **Army DCSOPS:** Primary HQDA staff responsibility for EPW/CI/RP programs; develops and disseminates policy guidance on treatment, care, accountability, legal status, and processing; provides staff supervision for the National Prisoner of War Information Center (NPWIC); develops ISN assignment plans; provides reports and technical advice to OSD, JCS, military departments, and unified commands (para 1-4c).
- **Combatant Commanders / Task Force Commanders / Joint Task Force Commanders:** Highlighted responsibilities for EPW/CI/RP operations (para 1-4g).
- **Proponent (DCSOPS):** Authority to approve exceptions to this regulation consistent with controlling law; may delegate approval authority in writing to a division chief at colonel grade or civilian equivalent.
- **EPW Command (with medical advice):** Authorized to transfer injured, sick, and wounded EPW to other commands (para referenced in authority statements).
- **National Prisoner of War Information Center (NPWIC):** Central accounting and information hub for EPW/RP/CI data (para 1-7).
- **Branch PWIC:** Theater-level prisoner of war information center receiving ISN blocks from NPWIC (para 1-8).

## Key Requirements
1. **Compliance is mandatory** for all services; the regulation has the force of a multi-service directive (header statement).
2. All detained persons (EPW, RP, CI, OD) must be treated in accordance with the 1949 Geneva Conventions; Geneva Convention provisions override this regulation where conflicts exist (para 1-1b(4)).
3. Suspected or alleged violations of the law of war must be **promptly reported and investigated** per DoD Directive 5100.77 (para 1-4a(3), 1-4c(2)).
4. **Tribunals** must be conducted in accordance with established procedures to determine the legal status of detainees (para 1-6).
5. A **DD Form 2745** (EPW Capture Tag) must be used upon capture of enemy prisoners of war (para 2-1b).
6. EPW held aboard ship must follow specific policies established in the regulation (para 2-1b).
7. OCONUS evacuation of EPW/RP must follow updated evacuation policy (para 2-3).
8. EPW internment facilities must be established and operated in compliance with detailed standards covering housing, medical care, discipline, correspondence, religious activities, employment, and compensation (Chapters 3–4).
9. Civilian Internee internment facilities must meet parallel standards including administrative processing, personal effects handling, medical care, communications, discipline, and release procedures (Chapters 5–7).
10. EPW employment must comply with restrictions on authorized/unauthorized work, working conditions, compensation rates, and contract employment rules (Chapter 4, including para 4-22).
11. **Health and Comfort Packs** may be used as a temporary substitute for Advance of Pay for short-term operations (para 3-4h).
12. **Public Affairs** policy governing EPW/CI/OD matters must be followed (para 1-9).
13. Army supplementation is prohibited without HQDA (DAMO-ODL) approval; Navy, Marine Corps, and Air Force supplementation is authorized but not required, with copies furnished to respective headquarters.

## Gaps & Ambiguities
1. **OPR for Air Force unclear:** While the Army DCSOPS is clearly identified as the overall proponent, the specific Air Force Office of Primary Responsibility is not identified in the available text. The Air Force supplement routing address (HQ USAF/SPO) is listed but its role beyond receiving supplements is not defined.
2. **Other Detainee (OD) category under-defined:** The regulation references "Other Detainees" alongside EPW, RP, and CI throughout the purpose statement and responsibility sections, but the extracted text does not provide a clear definition, dedicated chapter, or distinct procedural guidance for ODs comparable to the treatment given EPW (Chapters 2–4) and CI (Chapters 5–7).
3. **Tribunal procedures referenced but not detailed in extracted text:** Paragraph 1-6 is cited as establishing procedures for conducting tribunals to determine detainee status, but the substance of those procedures is not available in the parsed text, making it impossible to assess completeness.
4. **Delegation of exception authority:** The proponent may delegate exception approval to a colonel-grade division chief, but the regulation does not specify criteria or limits bounding such exceptions beyond "consistent with controlling law and regulation," which could create inconsistent application across services.
5. **Reserve component applicability boundary:** The regulation applies to Reserve components "when lawfully ordered to active duty under Title 10," but does not address training scenarios or Title 32 activations where personnel might encounter detainee situations.
