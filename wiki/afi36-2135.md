# AFI 36-2135: Joint Officer Management

**OPR:** AF/A1P | **Effective:** 4 November 2020 (Incorporating Change 1, 12 November 2024) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2135, 26 July 2018

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 36-2135 establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for the Joint Officer Management program, ensuring a significant number of Air Force officers are educated, trained, and experienced in joint matters to enhance joint warfighting capability. It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) officers. It does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory; tiered waiver authorities (T-0 through T-3) apply as identified, with non-tiered waiver requests routed through the applicable MAJCOM to AFPC/DP3AM.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Collaborates with AF/RE and NGB/CF to develop joint officer management personnel policy; maintains procedures for Joint Qualification System compliance; supports USD(P&R) boards; manages joint qualification designation (Levels II–IV); monitors JQO promotions; reviews and recommends DoD program waivers; develops joint duty credit screening procedures (§§2.1.1–2.1.9).
- **AF/A1D (Directorate of Force Development):** Charges development teams with vectoring quality officers to S-JDA positions to meet promotion objectives and tour length requirements; ensures officers are designated to attend Joint and Combined Warfighting Schools; ensures JPME Phase I/II curricula meet CJCSI 1800.01F criteria (§§2.2.1–2.2.3).
- **AF/A1M (Directorate of Manpower, Organization and Resources):** Updates the Air Force Manpower Programming and Execution System bi-annually to reflect JDA positions; coordinates updates with AFPC, ARPC, and NGB (§§2.3.1–2.3.2).
- **AF/A1P (Directorate of Military Force Management Policy):** Develops Air Force policy for compliance with 10 USC §§661–668, DoDI 1300.19, and CJCSI 1330.05B; provides joint officer management reports to CJCS/SecDef; nominates officers for Level II and III JQO designation (§§2.4.1–2.4.3).
- **USD(P&R):** Designates officers as joint qualified based on education, training, and experience achievements (§1.2).
- **AFPC / ARPC / AF/REG / ANG:** Assign officers to S-JDA positions for prescribed tour lengths (§1.3).
- **AF/JAX (Professional Development Office, TJAG):** Serves as assignment authority for judge advocate officers, including authority to implement and waive policies in this instruction (§1.5).
- **Development Teams:** Vector quality officers to S-JDAs ensuring promotion rates, tour length requirements, and career milestone considerations are met (§§2.2.1.1–2.2.1.5).

## Key Requirements
1. Officers nominated for JQO status must have no quality force indicators (UIFs, control roster, referral OPRs, NJP, courts-martial) in their records. (T-0) (§1.2.1)
2. Prior to JQO nomination, officers must complete Level III requirements, hold a master's degree, and complete requisite PME—intermediate developmental education for majors; senior developmental education for Lt Cols/Lt Col-selects (RegAF and AFR; not required for ANG). (T-0) (§1.2.2)
3. Only officers in the grade of major or above may be designated JQO on the Active Duty List or Reserve Active Status List. (§1.2.4)
4. RegAF officers must be designated JQO before appointment to brigadier general, unless waived. (T-0) (§1.2.5)
5. RegAF/ANG/AFR general officers must serve a minimum 2-year joint tour; all other RegAF officers a minimum 3-year tour; ANG/AFR personnel a minimum 2-year tour. (§1.3)
6. Critical S-JDAs will be filled with JQOs; S-JDA positions designated Lt Col or above will be filled with officers holding the appropriate joint qualification level. (T-0) (§1.3.1)
7. National Defense University graduates must be placed in joint assignments per DAFI 36-2110, DoDI 1300.19, and CJCSI 1330.05. (§1.3.2)
8. Promotion rates for officers serving/having served on the Joint Staff or OSD staff must, as a group, be not less than rates for same-grade Air Staff or Space Staff officers. (§1.4.1)
9. JQOs in grade major or above must, as a group, be promoted at a rate not less than same-grade/competitive-category officers overall. (§1.4.2)
10. Bulk joint tour length waivers are authorized only for officers departing JDAs for boarded command positions (G-series orders) or senior developmental education; all others are by exception with detailed justification. (T-0) (§§1.6.1–1.6.2)
11. AF Manpower Programming and Execution System must be updated bi-annually to reflect JDA positions accurately. (T-1) (§2.3.1)

## Gaps & Ambiguities
1. **ANG tour length inconsistency (§1.3):** General officer minimum tour is stated as "not less than 2 years" for all components, yet a separate sentence states "ANG and AFR personnel are assigned for 2 years" without distinguishing between general officers and all others, creating ambiguity about whether ANG/AFR non-GOs also serve 2-year (rather than 3-year) tours.
2. **Space Force exclusion scope:** The publication explicitly excludes USSF but references the Office of the Chief of Space Operations (Space Staff) as a promotion-rate comparator (§1.4.1), leaving unclear how data coordination occurs across separate services.
3. **AF/A1P responsibilities (§2.4):** The extracted text cuts off at §2.4.4, suggesting additional responsibilities exist but are not available for review—full scope of AF/A1P duties is incomplete.
4. **Waiver authority for JQO prerequisite (§1.2.5):** The text states the BG JQO requirement may be "waived" but does not specify the waiver approval authority, deferring implicitly to DoDI 1300.19 without explicit Air Force-level guidance.
5. **Case-by-case exceptions (§1.2.2):** Criteria for exceptions to master's degree and PME requirements are undefined beyond "case-by-case basis," with no designated approval authority specified in the available text.
