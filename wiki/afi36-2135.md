# AFI 36-2135: JOINT OFFICER MANAGEMENT

**OPR:** AF/A1P | **Effective:** 4 November 2020 (Incorporating Change 1, 12 November 2024) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2135, 26 July 2018

## Purpose & Scope

AFI 36-2135 establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for the Department of the Air Force Joint Officer Management program, ensuring a significant number of officers are educated, trained, and experienced in joint matters to enhance joint warfighting capability. It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) officers. This publication **does not apply** to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory, and tiered waiver authorities (T-0 through T-3) apply as indicated throughout (§1, opening paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Collaborates with AF/RE and NGB/CF to develop joint officer management personnel policy; maintains procedures for statutory/DoD Joint Qualification System compliance; supports ASD(M&RA) boards; ensures CJCS representation on promotion boards; manages joint qualification designation (Levels II–IV); monitors JQO promotions; reviews and recommends DoD JOM program waivers to USD(P&R); develops procedures for joint duty credit requests (§2.1).
- **AF/A1D (Directorate of Force Development):** Charges development teams with vectoring quality officers to S-JDA positions to meet promotion objectives and tour length requirements; ensures sufficient JQOs fill critical JDA positions; develops policies for Joint and Combined Warfighting School attendance; ensures JPME Phase I/II curricula meet CJCSI 1800.01F criteria (§2.2).
- **AF/A1M (Directorate of Manpower, Organization and Resources):** Updates the Air Force Manpower Programming and Execution System bi-annually to reflect JDA positions; coordinates updates with AFPC, ARPC, and NGB (§2.3).
- **AF/A1P (Directorate of Military Force Management Policy):** Develops Air Force policy for compliance with 10 USC §§661–668, DoDI 1300.19, and CJCSI 1330.05B; provides joint officer management reports through CJCS to SecDef; nominates officers for Level II and III JQO designation (§2.4).
- **AFPC / ARPC / AF/REG / ANG:** Assign officers to S-JDA positions for required tour lengths (§1.3).
- **AF/JAX (Professional Development Office, Office of the Judge Advocate General):** Serves as assignment authority for judge advocates regarding joint duty, including authority to implement and waive policies in this instruction to the extent practicable, pursuant to 10 USC §§806 and 9037 (§1.5).
- **USD(P&R):** Designates officers as joint qualified based on achievement of education, training, and experience criteria (§1.2).
- **Development Teams:** Vector quality officers to S-JDA such that promotion rates meet or exceed rates for officers in the same grade/competitive category; consider career milestones and tour completion timelines (§2.2.1–2.2.1.5).

## Key Requirements

1. Officers nominated for JQO status must have **no quality force indicators** (UIFs, control roster, referral OPRs, NJP, courts-martial) in their records. (T-0) (§1.2.1)
2. Prior to JQO nomination, officers must complete Level III requirements, hold a **master's degree**, and complete requisite **PME** (IDE for majors; SDE for Lt Cols/Lt Col-selects, RegAF and AFR only; not required for ANG). (T-0) (§1.2.2)
3. Only officers in the grade of **major or above** on the Active Duty List or Reserve Active Status List may be designated JQO. (§1.2.4)
4. Officers must be designated JQO **before appointment to brigadier general** (RegAF only), unless waived. (T-0) (§1.2.5)
5. **Joint tour lengths:** RegAF/AFR/ANG general officers serve not less than 2 years; all other RegAF officers not less than 3 years; ANG and AFR personnel serve 2 years. (§1.3)
6. **Critical S-JDAs** must be filled with JQOs; S-JDA positions designated Lt Col or above must be filled with officers holding appropriate joint qualification levels. (T-0) (§1.3.1)
7. TDY periods exceeding 30 consecutive days away from the joint organization for service duties are **not counted** toward the 24-month JDA credit minimum unless waived by the joint organization. (§1.3.3)
8. **Joint tour length waivers:** Bulk waivers are authorized only for officers departing JDA for board-selected command (G-series orders) or senior developmental education. All other waivers are by exception only and require specific justification of unique qualifications and mission impact. (T-0) (§1.6)
9. Promotion rates for officers serving/having served on the Joint Staff or OSD staff must, as a group, be **not less than** rates of same-grade/competitive-category officers on the Air Staff or Space Staff. (§1.4.1)
10. Development teams must vector officers to S-JDA positions such that promotion rates are not less than rates for officers of the same grade and competitive category. (T-0) (§2.2.1.2)

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Incomplete Section 2.4:** The extracted text cuts off at §2.4.4, leaving AF/A1P's full set of responsibilities unknown. Additional duties assigned to AF/A1P and any subsequent roles (§2.5+) are not captured.

**(b) Inconsistent ANG/AFR tour lengths:** §1.3 states general officers serve "not less than 2 years" and "all others…not less than 3 years," then separately states "ANG and AFR personnel are assigned for 2 years" without distinguishing between general officers and other grades for the reserve components, creating potential ambiguity about whether the 3-year rule ever applies to ANG/AFR non-GOs.

**(c) Space Force exclusion without transition guidance:** The publication explicitly excludes USSF but does not address officers who transfer between USAF and USSF mid-joint tour or how existing joint qualifications carry over, despite referencing the Office of the Chief of Space Operations in promotion comparison metrics (§1.4.1).

**(d) Waiver authority for non-tiered items:** Waiver requests for non-tiered compliance items are routed to AFPC/DP3AM, but no specific approval authority level is identified for those items, unlike the clearly defined T-0 through T-3 tier structure.

**(e) Case-by-case exceptions (§1.2.2):** The instruction permits exceptions to master's degree and PME requirements on a "case-by-case basis" but does not specify the approval authority or criteria for granting such exceptions beyond a general reference to §1.4 promotion objectives.
