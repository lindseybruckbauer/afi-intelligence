# AFI 36-2135: JOINT OFFICER MANAGEMENT

**OPR:** AF/A1P | **Effective:** 4 November 2020 (Incorporating Change 1, 12 November 2024) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2135, 26 July 2018

## Purpose & Scope

AFI 36-2135 establishes policy and assigns responsibilities for the joint officer management program, ensuring a significant number of Air Force officers are educated, trained, and experienced in joint matters to enhance joint warfighting capability. It governs joint qualification designation, joint duty assignments, joint tour length requirements, joint promotion objectives, and associated waivers. The publication applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) officers. It does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory (§1.1, opening paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Collaborates with AF/RE and NGB/CF to develop joint officer management personnel policy; develops procedures for Joint Qualification System compliance; supports USD(P&R) boards; manages joint qualification designation (Levels II–IV); monitors JQO promotions; reviews and recommends DoD waiver requests (§2.1).
- **AF/A1D (Directorate of Force Development):** Charges development teams with vectoring quality officers to S-JDA positions to meet promotion objectives and tour length requirements (T-0/T-1); develops policies for Joint and Combined Warfighting School attendance; ensures JPME Phase I/II curricula meet CJCSI standards (§2.2).
- **AF/A1M (Directorate of Manpower, Organization and Resources):** Ensures Air Force Manpower Programming and Execution System is updated bi-annually to reflect JDA positions; coordinates updates with AFPC, ARPC, and NGB (§2.3).
- **AF/A1P (Directorate of Military Force Management Policy):** Develops Air Force joint officer management policy for Title 10 and DoD compliance; provides joint officer management reports through CJCS to SecDef; nominates qualifying officers for Level II and III JQO designation (§2.4).
- **AFPC / ARPC / AF/REG / ANG:** Assign officers to S-JDA positions for prescribed tour lengths (§1.3).
- **AF/JAX (Professional Development Office, under AF/JA):** Serves as assignment authority for judge advocate officers in the joint officer management program; may waive policies in this instruction to the extent practicable (§1.5).
- **USD(P&R):** Designates officers as joint qualified based on achievement of education, training, and experience requirements (§1.2).
- **Development Teams:** Vector quality officers to S-JDA positions ensuring promotion rates meet statutory objectives and joint tour requirements are satisfied (§2.2.1–2.2.1.5).

## Key Requirements

1. **Joint Qualification Prerequisites (T-0):** Officers nominated for JQO must have no quality force indicators (UIFs, control roster, referral OPRs, NJP, courts-martial) in their records (§1.2.1).
2. **JQO Nomination Requirements (T-0):** Officers must complete Level III requirements, hold a master's degree, and complete requisite PME (IDE for majors; SDE for Lt Cols/Lt Col-selects, RegAF and AFR only—not required for ANG) prior to JQO nomination (§1.2.2).
3. **Brigadier General Prerequisite (T-0):** RegAF officers must be designated JQO before appointment to brigadier general, unless waived (§1.2.5).
4. **Joint Tour Lengths:** RegAF/AFR/ANG general officers serve not less than 2 years; all other RegAF officers serve not less than 3 years; ANG and AFR personnel serve 2 years (§1.3).
5. **Critical S-JDA Fill (T-0):** Critical S-JDAs must be filled with JQOs; S-JDA positions designated Lt Col or above must be filled with appropriately joint-qualified officers (§1.3.1).
6. **NDU Graduate Placement:** National Defense University graduates are placed in joint assignments per DAFI 36-2110 and DoDI 1300.19/CJCSI 1330.05 (§1.3.2).
7. **Joint Promotion Objectives (T-0, RegAF):** Officers serving/having served on the Joint Staff or OSD staff must, as a group, be promoted at a rate not less than comparable Air Staff/Space Staff officers; JQOs must be promoted at a rate not less than all officers of the same grade/competitive category (§1.4).
8. **Joint Tour Length Waivers (T-0):** Bulk waivers authorized only for boarded command positions (G-series orders) or senior developmental education departures; all other waivers are by exception with specific justification (§1.6).
9. **Development Team Vectoring (T-0/T-1):** Teams must vector quality officers to S-JDA such that promotion rates meet or exceed those of same-grade/competitive-category peers and must consider career milestones and tour completion timelines (§2.2.1.1–2.2.1.5).
10. **Manpower System Accuracy (T-1):** AF/A1M must update the Manpower Programming and Execution System bi-annually and coordinate with AFPC, ARPC, and NGB (§2.3).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Incomplete section text:** Section 2.4.4 is cut off in the extracted text, leaving AF/A1P's full scope of responsibilities potentially incomplete. Additional responsibilities for AF/A1P and any subsequent offices (§2.5+) are unknown from the provided text.

**(b) USSF exclusion without transition guidance:** The publication explicitly excludes the U.S. Space Force but does not address how joint-qualified officers transferring between the Air Force and Space Force are managed or whether joint credit/qualification carries over.

**(c) ANG PME exception lacks criteria:** ANG officers are exempted from the IDE/SDE requirement for JQO nomination (§1.2.2), but no alternative standard or rationale is specified, creating ambiguity about the threshold for ANG JQO quality.

**(d) Conflicting tour length statements:** Section 1.3 states "all others are to be assigned for not less than 3 years" for RegAF, then separately states "ANG and AFR personnel are assigned for 2 years." The AFR general officer minimum (2 years) versus AFR non-GO minimum (2 years) appears consistent, but the distinction between RegAF non-GO (3 years) and AFR non-GO (2 years) could create inequitable joint credit accumulation without explicit justification.

**(e) Waiver authority for non-tiered items:** The opening paragraph routes non-tiered waiver requests to AFPC/DP3AM, but the instruction does not clearly delineate which compliance items are non-tiered versus tiered beyond those explicitly marked with T-numbers.
