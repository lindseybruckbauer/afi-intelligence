# AFI 36-2112: MATERIEL MANAGEMENT ENLISTED CAREER BROADENING PROGRAM

**OPR:** AF/A4LR | **Effective:** 12 September 2018 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-26, Total Force Development | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This manual provides instruction on the management and administration of the Materiel Management Enlisted Career Broadening Program (MMECBP), a 3-year developmental program that cross-utilizes training, knowledge, experience, and perspectives to promote enterprise-level understanding of the Air Force supply chain. It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF) personnel and all agencies that nominate or select enlisted Airmen (AFSC 2S0X1, Materiel Management) for the program, encompassing rotational assignments at AFMC Supply Chain Management Groups and DLA Major Subordinate Commands (§§1.1–1.2). This publication may not be supplemented at any level.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **AF/A4LR (Air Force Logistics Readiness Branch):** Provides policy and program messaging oversight (§2.1).
- **2S0X1 Materiel Management Career Field Manager (CFM):** Overall OPR for the program; actively promotes the program by providing oversight, direction, and execution of the strategic plan as prescribed by AF/A4L; presides as chair of the Functional Advisory Council and the selection panel (§§1.4, 2.1.1, 2.2.1, 3.1.3).
- **Air Force Materiel Management Functional Advisory Council:** Orchestrates developmental opportunities for the Materiel Management functional community; MAJCOM 2S0 Functional CMSgts serve as voting members; MAJCOM Functional Manager serves as voting member and secretary (§2.2).
- **AFPC Materiel Management Enlisted Assignments Functional Manager:** Provides functional assignment oversight and guidance to ensure program success (§2.3).
- **Organization Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Ensures volunteers meet mandatory requirements, approves packages, and forwards nominations through MAJCOM A4Rs (§3.1.2).
- **MAJCOM Functional Manager:** Validates and uploads nomination packages to SharePoint pending selection panel review (§3.1.2).
- **SCMG and MSC Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Responsible for program administration duties as outlined in §4.3.
- **Program Manager:** Carries out program manager responsibilities as outlined in §4.4.

## Key Requirements

1. Applicants must possess the rank of Technical Sergeant or be selected for promotion to Technical Sergeant (§3.2.1). **(T-1)**
2. Applicants must have less than 14 years time-in-service as of 1 October of the reporting year; this requirement is **non-waiverable** (§3.2.2). **(T-1)**
3. Applicants must hold a 2S071 control AFSC; **non-waiverable** (§3.2.3). **(T-1)**
4. Applicants must be eligible for permanent change of station per AFI 36-2110; **non-waiverable** (§3.2.4). **(T-1)**
5. CONUS members must have a minimum of four years time on station before entering the program; time-on-station waivers or DEROS curtailments will not be considered (§3.2.5). **(T-1)**
6. Applicants must hold at minimum a SECRET security clearance (§3.2.6). **(T-1)**
7. Applicants must have zero negative quality force indicators (e.g., control roster, UIF, PT failures within 12 months, referral EPRs within 5 years) (§3.2.7). **(T-1)**
8. Applicants must not be currently assigned to a developmental special duty (§3.2.8). **(T-1)**
9. Applicants must be recommended by their squadron Commander or Director (or equivalent) (§3.2.9). **(T-1)**
10. AF/A4LR will solicit nominations after TSgt promotion release each year; selection panel convenes in October; AF/A4L notifies selectees no later than 30 days after selection (§§3.1.1, 3.1.3). **(T-1)**
11. Volunteer packages must include rank-ordered location preferences for DLA and AFMC assignments (§3.3.1). **(T-1)**
12. Records created under this publication must be maintained per AFM 33-363 and disposed of per AF Records Disposition Schedule (Preamble).
