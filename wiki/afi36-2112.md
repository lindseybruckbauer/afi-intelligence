# AFI 36-2112: MATERIEL MANAGEMENT ENLISTED CAREER BROADENING PROGRAM

**OPR:** AF/A4LR | **Effective:** 12 September 2018 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-26, Total Force Development | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This manual provides instruction on the management and administration of the Materiel Management Enlisted Career Broadening Program (MMECBP), a 3-year developmental program that cross-utilizes training, knowledge, experience, and perspectives to promote enterprise-level understanding of the Air Force supply chain (§1.1). It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF) personnel and all agencies that nominate or select enlisted Airmen in AFSC 2S0X1 (Materiel Management) for the program (§1.2). The publication may not be supplemented at any level. Compliance is mandatory.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **2S0X1 Materiel Management Career Field Manager (CFM):** Overall OPR for the program; actively promotes the program by providing oversight, direction, and execution of the strategic plan prescribed by AF/A4L; chairs the selection panel and presides as chair of the Functional Advisory Council (§1.4, §2.1.1, §2.2.1, §3.1.3).
- **AF/A4LR (Air Force Logistics Readiness Branch):** Provides policy and program messaging oversight; sends annual nomination announcements to MAJCOM A4R agencies after TSgt promotion release (§2.1, §3.1.1).
- **Air Force Materiel Management Functional Advisory Council:** Orchestrates developmental opportunities for the Materiel Management functional community; MAJCOM 2S0 Functional CMSgts serve as voting members; MAJCOM Functional Manager serves as voting member and secretary (§2.2).
- **AFPC Materiel Management Enlisted Assignments Functional Manager:** Provides functional assignment oversight and guidance to ensure program success (§2.3).
- **Organization Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Ensures volunteers meet mandatory requirements, approves packages, and forwards nominations electronically through MAJCOM A4Rs (§3.1.2).
- **MAJCOM Functional Manager:** Validates and uploads nomination packages to SharePoint pending selection panel review (§3.1.2).
- **SCMG and MSC Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Responsibilities outlined in §4.3 (specific duties not fully captured in extracted text).
- **Program Manager:** Responsibilities outlined in §4.4 (specific duties not fully captured in extracted text).

## Key Requirements

1. Nominees must possess the rank of Technical Sergeant or be selected for promotion to TSgt (§3.2.1). **(T-1)**
2. Nominees must have less than 14 years time-in-service as of 1 October of the reporting year; this requirement is non-waiverable (§3.2.2). **(T-1)**
3. Nominees must hold a 2S071 control AFSC; non-waiverable (§3.2.3). **(T-1)**
4. Nominees must be PCS-eligible per AFI 36-2110; non-waiverable (§3.2.4). **(T-1)**
5. CONUS members must have a minimum of four years time on station before entering the program (§3.2.5). **(T-1)**
6. Nominees must possess at minimum a SECRET security clearance (§3.2.6). **(T-1)**
7. Nominees must have zero negative quality force indicators (e.g., control roster, UIF, PT failures within 12 months, referral EPRs within 5 years) (§3.2.7). **(T-1)**
8. Nominees must not be currently assigned to a developmental special duty (§3.2.8). **(T-1)**
9. Nominees must be recommended by their squadron Commander or Director (or equivalent) (§3.2.9). **(T-1)**
10. Volunteer packages must include rank-ordered location preferences for DLA and AFMC assignments (§3.3.1). **(T-1)**
11. The selection panel convenes in October each year; AF/A4L must notify selected individuals no later than 30 days after selection (§3.1.3). **(T-1)**
12. Rotational assignments are at AFMC Supply Chain Management Groups (SCMGs) or DLA Major Subordinate Commands (MSCs)—DLA positions at Columbus OH, Richmond VA, and Philadelphia PA (§3.3.1.1).
13. Program participants must complete Acquisition Professional Development Program certifications as applicable (§4.2).
14. Upon completion, members incur an Active Duty Service Commitment (§7.5) and receive Special Experience Indicators (§7.3).
