# AFI 36-2112: MATERIEL MANAGEMENT ENLISTED CAREER BROADENING PROGRAM

**OPR:** AF/A4LR | **Effective:** 12 September 2018 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-26, Total Force Development | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope
This manual provides instruction on the management and administration of the Materiel Management Enlisted Career Broadening Program (MMECBP), a 3-year developmental program that cross-utilizes training, knowledge, experience, and perspectives to promote enterprise-level understanding of the Air Force supply chain (§1.1). It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF) personnel and all agencies that nominate or select enlisted Airmen in AFSC 2S0X1 (Materiel Management) for the program (§1.2). The publication may not be supplemented at any level. Compliance is mandatory.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **2S0X1 Materiel Management Career Field Manager (CFM):** Overall OPR for the program; actively promotes the program by providing oversight, direction, and execution of the strategic plan as prescribed by AF/A4L; presides as chair of the Functional Advisory Council and chairs the selection panel (§1.4, §2.1.1, §2.2.1, §3.1.3).
- **AF/A4LR (Air Force Logistics Readiness Branch):** Provides policy and program messaging oversight; sends annual nomination announcements to MAJCOM A4R agencies after TSgt promotion release (§2.1, §3.1.1).
- **Air Force Materiel Management Functional Advisory Council:** Orchestrates developmental opportunities for the Materiel Management functional community; MAJCOM 2S0 Functional CMSgts serve as voting members; MAJCOM Functional Manager serves as voting member and secretary (§2.2).
- **AFPC Materiel Management Enlisted Assignments Functional Manager:** Provides functional assignment oversight and guidance to ensure program success (§2.3).
- **Organization Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Ensures volunteers meet mandatory requirements, approves packages, and forwards nominations through MAJCOM A4Rs (§3.1.2).
- **MAJCOM Functional Manager:** Validates and uploads nomination packages to the Materiel Management SharePoint pending selection panel review (§3.1.2).
- **SCMG and MSC Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Responsibilities outlined in program administration (§4.3).
- **Program Manager:** Responsibilities for program administration (§4.4).

## Key Requirements
1. Applicants must hold the rank of Technical Sergeant or be selected for promotion to TSgt (§3.2.1, T-1).
2. Applicants must have less than 14 years time-in-service as of 1 October of the reporting year; this is non-waiverable (§3.2.2, T-1).
3. Applicants must hold a 2S071 control AFSC; non-waiverable (§3.2.3, T-1).
4. Applicants must be eligible for permanent change of station per AFI 36-2110; non-waiverable (§3.2.4, T-1).
5. CONUS members must have a minimum of four years time on station before entering the program (§3.2.5, T-1). Time-on-station waivers or DEROS curtailments are not considered (T-1).
6. Applicants must possess, at minimum, a SECRET security clearance (§3.2.6, T-1).
7. Applicants must have zero negative quality force indicators (e.g., control roster, UIF, PT failures within 12 months, referral EPRs within 5 years) (§3.2.7, T-1).
8. Applicants must not be currently assigned to a developmental special duty (§3.2.8, T-1).
9. Applicants must be recommended by their squadron Commander or Director (or equivalent) (§3.2.9, T-1).
10. Volunteers submit electronic packages including rank-ordered location preferences for DLA and AFMC assignments (§3.3.1, T-1).
11. The selection panel convenes in October each year; AF/A4L notifies selected individuals no later than 30 days after selection (§3.1.3, T-1).
12. Career broadeners rotate through designated groups/directorates on a 3-year rotational training plan at AFMC SCMGs or DLA MSCs (§1.1, Ch. 5).
13. Program completion results in a Special Experience Indicator and incurs an Active Duty Service Commitment (§7.3, §7.5).
