# AFI 36-2112: MATERIEL MANAGEMENT ENLISTED CAREER BROADENING PROGRAM

**OPR:** AF/A4LR | **Effective:** 12 September 2018 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-26, Total Force Development | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope
This manual provides instruction on the management and administration of the Materiel Management Enlisted Career Broadening Program (MMECBP), a 3-year developmental program that cross-utilizes training, knowledge, experience, and perspectives to promote enterprise-level understanding of the Air Force supply chain (§1.1). It applies to all Regular Air Force (RegAF) personnel and all agencies that nominate or select enlisted Airmen for the program. The program is limited to AFSC 2S0X1 (Materiel Management) enlisted members and involves rotational assignments at AFMC Supply Chain Management Groups (SCMGs) or DLA Major Subordinate Commands (MSCs) (§1.1–1.2). This publication may not be supplemented at any level.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **2S0X1 Materiel Management Career Field Manager (CFM):** Overall OPR for the program; actively promotes the program by providing oversight, direction, and execution of the strategic plan prescribed by AF/A4L; chairs the selection panel and presides as chair of the Functional Advisory Council (§1.4, §2.1.1, §2.2.1, §3.1.3).
- **AF/A4LR (Air Force Logistics Readiness Branch):** Provides policy and program messaging oversight; sends annual nomination announcements to MAJCOM A4R agencies after TSgt promotion release (§2.1, §3.1.1).
- **Air Force Materiel Management Functional Advisory Council:** Orchestrates developmental opportunities for the Materiel Management functional community; MAJCOM 2S0 Functional CMSgts serve as voting members; Materiel Management MAJCOM Functional Manager serves as voting member and secretary (§2.2).
- **AFPC Materiel Management Enlisted Assignments Functional Manager:** Provides functional assignment oversight and guidance to ensure program success (§2.3).
- **Organization Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Ensures volunteers meet mandatory requirements, approves packages, and forwards nominations electronically through MAJCOM A4Rs (§3.1.2).
- **MAJCOM Functional Manager:** Validates and uploads nomination packages to SharePoint pending selection panel review (§3.1.2).
- **SCMG and MSC Commander or Director (or equivalent):** Responsibilities outlined in §4.3 (full details not in extracted text).
- **Program Manager:** Responsibilities outlined in §4.4 (full details not in extracted text).

## Key Requirements
1. Applicants must hold the rank of Technical Sergeant or be selected for promotion to TSgt (§3.2.1). (T-1)
2. Applicants must have less than 14 years time-in-service as of 1 October of the reporting year; this is non-waiverable (§3.2.2). (T-1)
3. Applicants must possess a 2S071 control AFSC; non-waiverable (§3.2.3). (T-1)
4. Applicants must be eligible for permanent change of station per AFI 36-2110; non-waiverable (§3.2.4). (T-1)
5. CONUS members must have a minimum of four years time on station before entering the program (§3.2.5). (T-1)
6. Applicants must possess at minimum a SECRET security clearance (§3.2.6). (T-1)
7. Applicants must have zero negative quality force indicators (e.g., control roster, UIF, PT failures within 12 months, referral EPRs within 5 years) (§3.2.7). (T-1)
8. Applicants must not be currently assigned to a developmental special duty (§3.2.8). (T-1)
9. Applicants must be recommended by their squadron Commander or Director (or equivalent) (§3.2.9). (T-1)
10. Volunteer packages must be submitted electronically through the organization Commander/Director and routed through MAJCOM A4Rs (§3.1.2). (T-1)
11. The selection panel convenes in October each year; AF/A4L notifies selectees no later than 30 days after selection (§3.1.3). (T-1)
12. Time-on-station waivers and DEROS curtailments will not be considered for this program (§3.2.5). (T-1)
13. Compliance with Acquisition Professional Development Program certifications is required per §4.2 (details not fully in extracted text).
