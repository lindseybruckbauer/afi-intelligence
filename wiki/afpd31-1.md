# AFPD 31-1: Integrated Defense

**OPR:** AF/A4SP | **Effective:** 21 June 2018 | **Implements:** DoDD 3025.13, DoDI 3224.03, DoDI 5200.08, DoDI 5100.76, DoDD 5200.31E, DoD S-5210.41-M, DoDD 5210.56, DTM 09-012 | **Supersedes:** AFPD 31-1, 28 October 2011

## Purpose & Scope

This Air Force Policy Directive establishes the framework for how the Air Force formulates and applies Integrated Defense—the incorporation of multidisciplinary active and passive, offensive and defensive capabilities employed to mitigate risks and defeat adversary threats to Air Force operations within the base boundary and base security zone (§2.3). Compliance is mandatory for all military and civilian Air Force personnel, members of the Air Force Reserve and Air National Guard, and other individuals or organizations bound by agreement or obligation with the Department of the Air Force (preamble).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Commanders at all levels:** Execute assigned missions in the installation's integrated defense plan; organize, train, and equip forces for integrated defense operations; align mission assurance activities with integrated defense (§3.1).
- **Installation Commanders:** Identify risks and develop risk management strategies to produce effects-based, integrated defense plans ensuring unhindered AF, Joint, and Coalition missions (§2.2). Nuclear-capable installation commanders must plan and execute nuclear weapon security per DoD S-5210.41-M (§2.10).
- **AF/A1:** Provides guidance on Security Forces manpower determination/validation; provides policy for integrating education and training requirements into accessions, PME, and ancillary training (§3.2).
- **AF/A2:** Provides operational, planning, programming, and budgeting guidance for intelligence support to integrated defense; supports intelligence preparation of the operational environment and collection management for ISR assets within the base security zone (§3.3).
- **AF/A3:** Approves Protection Level of Air Force assets; oversees homeland defense and civil support doctrine per AFPD 10-8 (§3.4).
- **AF/A4:** Establishes policy and guidance for training, organizing, and equipping personnel for Integrated Defense; ensures sustainment and readiness through planning, programming, and budgeting; establishes implementation guidance; integrates AF policy for protection against all threats and hazards (§3.5.1–3.5.5).
- **AF/A4S:** Retains strategy development, policy/guidance, resource advocacy, external agency engagement, and career field management; manages integrated defense risk management methodology; establishes policy for AF Law and Order programs; designates DoD program manager for military working dog training; determines minimum system security standards; develops policy for integrated defense technology and equipment (§3.5.6–3.5.19).
- **AF/A4C:** Provides field expertise for structural, environmental, Fire Emergency Services, EOD, Emergency Management, and engineer areas; implements non-medical Counter-CBRN passive defense and consequence management programs; develops engineer-related force protection training standards and equipment requirements (§3.5.20–3.5.22).
- **AF/A10:** Oversees Counter-CBRN doctrine, policy, and operational standards per AFPD 10-26 (§3.6).
- **AF/SG:** Develops medical-related force protection and integrated defense training standards and equipment requirements (§3.7).

## Key Requirements

1. Installation commanders must publish desired Integrated Defense effects (Anticipate, Deter, Detect, Assess, Warn, Defeat, Delay, Defend, Recover) in a commander's intent and mission statement (§2.6).
2. Integrated Defense courses of action must comply with applicable laws, Status of Forces Agreements, Rules of Engagement, and Law of Armed Conflict (§2.7).
3. Information synchronization for integrated defense decisions must be executed through the information fusion cell per AF policy, guidance, and TTPs (§2.8).
4. Communications with and dispatch of integrated defense forces must be conducted from the Base Defense Operations Center; command and control follows the AF Incident Management System per AFMAN 10-2502 (§2.9).
5. Nuclear-capable installations must plan and execute nuclear weapon security per DoD S-5210.41-M and AFMAN 31-108, Volumes 1–3 (§2.10).
6. Installations must synchronize integrated defense efforts with friendly forces operating within the base security zone (§2.7).
7. AF/A4S must provide DoD Physical Security Review Board with incident information on conventional arms, ammunition, and explosives (§3.5.11).
8. All records created under this directive must be maintained per AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per AF RDS (preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **AF/SG responsibilities truncated:** Section 3.7 is cut off mid-sentence, leaving the full scope of Surgeon General integrated defense responsibilities undefined.
2. **AFIMSC authorities unclear:** The Summary of Changes references updates to authorities transferred to the Air Force Installation and Mission Support Center, but the extracted text does not contain explicit AFIMSC role assignments, making it difficult to determine what AFIMSC is authorized or required to do.
3. **Base Security Zone definition absent:** The directive references operations within the "base security zone" multiple times (§§2.3, 2.7, 3.3.3) but does not define its boundaries or how it is established.
4. **Integrated Defense Figure 1.1 referenced but not rendered:** The capabilities (means) available to installation commanders are described as depicted in Figure 1.1, but the figure content is not available in text form, leaving the enumeration of specific capabilities unclear.
5. **Potential overlap with AF/A3 and AF/A4:** Both offices have roles touching force protection doctrine and operational standards (§§3.4, 3.5), with limited delineation of where AF/A3 oversight ends and AF/A4 authority begins outside of asset protection levels.
