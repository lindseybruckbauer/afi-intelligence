# AFPD 31-1: Integrated Defense

**OPR:** AF/A4SP | **Effective:** 21 June 2018 | **Implements:** DoD Directive 3025.13, DoDI 3224.03, DoDI 5200.08, DoDI 5100.76, DoDD 5200.31E, DoD S-5210.41-M, DoDD 5210.56, DTM 09-012 | **Supersedes:** AFPD 31-1, 28 October 2011

## Purpose & Scope

This Air Force Policy Directive establishes the framework for how the Air Force formulates and applies Integrated Defense—the incorporation of multidisciplinary active and passive, offensive and defensive capabilities employed to mitigate risks and defeat adversary threats to Air Force operations within the base boundary and base security zone (§2.3). Compliance is mandatory and applies to all military and civilian Air Force personnel, members of the Air Force Reserve and Air National Guard, and other individuals or organizations bound by agreement or obligation with the Department of the Air Force (introductory paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Commanders at all levels:** Execute assigned missions in the installation's integrated defense plan; organize, train, and equip forces for integrated defense operations; align mission assurance activities with integrated defense (§3.1).
- **Installation Commanders:** Identify risks and develop risk management strategies to produce effects-based, integrated defense plans ensuring unhindered Air Force, Joint, and Coalition missions (§2.2). Commanders of nuclear-capable installations must plan and execute nuclear weapon security per DoD S-5210.41-M (§2.10).
- **AF/A1:** Provides guidance on Security Forces manpower determination/validation; provides policy for integrating education and training requirements into accessions, PME, and ancillary training (§3.2).
- **AF/A2:** Provides operational, planning, programming, and budgeting guidance for intelligence support to integrated defense; supports intelligence preparation of the operational environment and ISR collection management within the base security zone (§3.3).
- **AF/A3:** Approves Protection Level of Air Force assets; oversees homeland defense and civil support doctrine/policy per AFPD 10-8 (§3.4).
- **AF/A4:** Establishes policy and guidance for training, organizing, and equipping personnel for Integrated Defense; ensures sustainment and readiness through planning, programming, and budgeting; integrates policy for protection against all threats and hazards (§3.5.1–3.5.5).
- **AF/A4S:** Retains strategy development, policy/guidance, resource advocacy, external agency engagement, and career field management; manages integrated defense risk management methodology; establishes policy for Law and Order programs; designates DoD program manager for military working dog training; directs arming policy for integrated defense forces; determines minimum system security standards; develops integrated defense technology/equipment policy (§3.5.6–3.5.19).
- **AF/A4C:** Provides engineer expertise for structural, environmental, Fire Emergency Services, EOD, and Emergency Management areas; implements non-medical Counter-CBRN passive defense and consequence management programs; develops engineer-related force protection training standards and equipment requirements (§3.5.20–3.5.22).
- **AF/A10:** Oversees Counter-CBRN doctrine, policy, and operational standards per AFPD 10-26 (§3.6).
- **AF/SG:** Develops medical-related force protection and integrated defense training standards and equipment requirements (§3.7).

## Key Requirements

1. Installation commanders must develop effects-based, integrated defense plans addressing mission accomplishment, threats, vulnerabilities, and risks (§2.2).
2. Integrated defense operations must comply with applicable laws, Status of Forces Agreements, Rules of Engagement, and Law of Armed Conflict (§2.7).
3. Commanders of nuclear-capable installations must plan and execute nuclear weapon security operations IAW DoD S-5210.41-M and AFMAN 31-108 (§2.10).
4. Information synchronization for integrated defense decisions must be executed through the information fusion cell IAW Air Force policy and TTP (§2.8).
5. Communications with and dispatch of integrated defense forces must be conducted from the Base Defense Operations Center (§2.9).
6. Command and control of integrated defense forces must follow the Air Force Incident Management System per AFMAN 10-2502 (§2.9).
7. Installations must synchronize integrated defense efforts with friendly forces operating within the base security zone (§2.7).
8. Nine Integrated Defense Desired Effects must be addressed: Anticipate, Deter, Detect, Assess, Warn, Defeat, Delay, Defend, and Recover (§2.6).
9. All records created under this publication must be maintained IAW AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per AF RDS (introductory paragraph).
