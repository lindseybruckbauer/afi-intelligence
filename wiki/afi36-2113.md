# AFI 36-2113: THE FIRST SERGEANT

**OPR:** HQ USAF/CCC | **Effective:** 11 June 2020 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2113, 18 November 2014

## Purpose & Scope

This AFI provides guidance for the management of the Air Force First Sergeant Program, including selection, classification, training, tenure, assignment, duties, and organizational structure of first sergeants. It applies to Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) personnel. The instruction covers the full lifecycle of first sergeant duty—from eligibility and application through tenure, withdrawal, return to career field, and the use of additional duty first sergeants. It also addresses first sergeant councils, the first sergeant device, special duty incentives, and manpower/utilization standards. (Chapters 1–10)

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services:** Develops policy for the Air Force First Sergeant Program in collaboration with the Chief of Air Force Reserve and the Director of the Air National Guard. (Preamble)
- **USAF First Sergeant Special Duty Manager (SDM):** Oversees program management for the first sergeant special duty. (§2.1) Serves as the approval authority for RegAF requests to serve beyond four years in the 8F000 SDI (§2.1.14) and for RegAF applications for members currently serving in another SDI (§2.1.15).
- **AFPC/DP2W (AEF Operations and Readiness Division):** Supports assignment and readiness functions related to first sergeants. (§2.2)
- **AFPC First Sergeant Special Duty Assignment Functional Area Manager (FAM):** Manages functional area assignment actions. (§2.3) AFPC/DP2OSS may waive assignment codes to facilitate needs of the Air Force. (§6.8.3)
- **Air Force Executive Talent Management Office – The Chief's Group:** Supports first sergeant talent management. (§2.4)
- **HQ AETC, Manpower and Personnel:** Responsible for training pipeline and academy oversight coordination. (§2.5)
- **USAF First Sergeant Academy Commandant:** Manages academy curriculum and attendance. (§2.6)
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM):** Exercises oversight of first sergeant management at the MAJCOM/DRU level; provides recommendations on over-grade requests during rotation. (§2.7, §6.4.7)
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command First Sergeant:** Serves as the MAJCOM/DRU-level functional representative for first sergeants. (§2.8)
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Headquarters First Sergeant:** Performs first sergeant duties at the headquarters level. (§2.9)
- **ANG Component Functional Manager:** Manages ANG-specific first sergeant program functions. (§2.10)
- **AFR Component Functional Manager:** Manages AFR-specific first sergeant program functions. (§2.11)
- **Host Wing CCM:** Oversees first sergeant functions at the wing level. (§2.12)
- **First Sergeant:** Works directly for and derives authority from the unit commander; serves as the dedicated focal point for readiness, health, morale, welfare, and quality of life; builds and maintains a mission-ready force; ensures the force understands commander's policies, goals, and objectives; responds to unit member needs 24/7; leads principally outside the operational chain of command. (§1.1, §2.13)
- **Unit Commander:** The first sergeant's rating authority; the source of the first sergeant's authority within the unit. (§1.1.1, §10.7)
- **WG/CC or equivalent:** Serves as approval authority for over-grade requests during AFR rotation, in conjunction with CCM recommendation and FY manning policy. (§6.4.7)
- **Tier Waiver Approval Authority / Requestor's Commander:** Submit waiver requests through the chain of command to the appropriate Tier waiver authority, or to the requestor's commander for non-tiered compliance items. (Preamble)

## Key Requirements

1. **Compliance is mandatory.** All Regular Air Force, ANG, and AFR personnel are subject to this instruction. Compliance obligations carry Tier designations (T-0 through T-3) per AFI 33-360. (Preamble)
2. **Eligibility and Minimum Qualifications:** Applicants must meet stated criteria and minimum qualifications before nomination or selection as a first sergeant. (§3.1, §3.2)
3. **Maintaining Qualifications:** Selected first sergeants must maintain qualification standards throughout their tenure. (§3.3)
4. **Nomination and Selection:** RegAF, ANG, and AFR each follow distinct nomination and application processes. (§§4.1–4.5)
5. **AFSC Award and Academy Attendance:** First sergeants must attend the USAF First Sergeant Academy; the 8F000 Special Duty Identifier (SDI) is awarded upon classification and training completion. (§§5.1–5.3)
6. **Installation Orientation:** First sergeants must complete an installation orientation upon assignment. (§5.4)
7. **Continuous Development:** First sergeants are required to pursue continuous professional development. (§5.5)
8. **Tenure:** RegAF first sergeants serve a 4-year tenure; extensions beyond four years require SDM approval. (§§6.1–6.2, §2.1.14)
9. **Early Withdrawal:** Voluntary and involuntary withdrawal from the 8F000 SDI must follow prescribed procedures, including use of sample memoranda. (§6.7, Figures 6.1–6.2)
10. **Additional Duty First Sergeants:** Units without an authorized first sergeant position may use full-time additional duty first sergeants under prescribed guidance. (§7.1–7.2)
11. **First Sergeant Councils:** First sergeant councils must adhere to prescribed structure and may not operate as private organizations unless in compliance with applicable rules. (§§8.1–8.3)
12. **Manpower and Utilization Standards:** First sergeant positions must conform to manpower authorizations; duties, rater designation, office symbol, security clearance, accommodations, and family association rules are specified. (§§10.1–10.8)
13. **Records Management:** All records created under this instruction must be maintained per AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per the AF Records Information Management System. (Preamble)
14. **Supplements:** Any supplement at any level must be routed to the OPR for coordination and approved by the Human Resource Management Strategic Board per AFI 36-8101. (Preamble)

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Authority of CCM vs. SDM on tenure extensions (ARC):** The instruction specifies the SDM as approval authority for RegAF beyond-4-year requests (§2.1.14), but the corresponding ANG and AFR approval authorities for extended tenure are not visible in the extracted text, creating potential ambiguity for ARC members.
2. **Over-grade approval chain (§6.4.7):** The over-grade approval process references "recommendation by the CCM, approval authority, WG/CC or equivalent," but the relationship between these actors—whether sequential or alternative—is unclear from the extracted language.
3. **Scope of AFPC code waivers (§6.8.3):** The statement that "AFPC/DP2OSS may waive codes to facilitate needs of the Air Force" lacks specificity regarding which assignment codes may be waived and under what conditions, potentially allowing broad discretion.
4. **Incomplete visibility into Chapter content:** The extracted text provides only Chapter 1 substantive content and the table of contents for remaining chapters; detailed compliance requirements in Chapters 2–10 (e.g., specific Tier-level obligations, detailed duties at §10.3, security clearance requirements at §10.5) could not be fully assessed from the provided extract.
5. **First Sergeant not a replacement for the chain of command (§1.1.4):** The instruction states this principle but does not delineate specific boundaries or scenarios where role overlap with superintendents or other SNCOs might create confusion.
