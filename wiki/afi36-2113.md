# AFI 36-2113: THE FIRST SERGEANT

**OPR:** HQ USAF/CCC | **Effective:** 11 June 2020 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2113, 18 November 2014

## Purpose & Scope

This AFI provides guidance for the management of the Air Force First Sergeant Program, including selection, classification, training, tenure, assignment, withdrawal, manpower utilization, and the duties and responsibilities of first sergeants and their functional managers. It applies to Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) personnel. The publication is mandatory-compliance and may be supplemented at any level with OPR coordination and Human Resource Management Strategic Board approval per AFI 36-8101 (§Preamble).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **USAF First Sergeant Special Duty Manager (SDM) (§2.1):** Serves as the approval authority for RegAF requests to serve beyond four years in the 8F000 Special Duty Identifier (SDI) (§2.1.13); serves as the approval authority for RegAF applications from members currently serving in another SDI (§2.1.14).
- **AFPC AEF Operations and Readiness Division (AFPC/DP2W) (§2.2):** Responsible for first sergeant deployment and readiness operations.
- **AFPC First Sergeant Special Duty Assignment Functional Area Manager (FAM) (§2.3):** Manages special duty assignment processes for first sergeants.
- **Air Force Executive Talent Management Office – The Chief's Group (§2.4):** Provides talent management support for first sergeant positions.
- **HQ Air Education and Training Command, Manpower and Personnel (§2.5):** Supports training and manpower aspects of the first sergeant program.
- **USAF First Sergeant Academy Commandant (§2.6):** Oversees the First Sergeant Academy curriculum and operations.
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) (§2.7):** Provides oversight of first sergeant management at the MAJCOM/DRU level.
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command First Sergeant (§2.8):** Serves as the MAJCOM/DRU-level first sergeant advisor.
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Headquarters First Sergeant (§2.9):** Performs first sergeant duties at the headquarters level.
- **ANG Component Functional Manager (§2.10):** Manages ANG-specific first sergeant program requirements.
- **AFR Component Functional Manager (§2.11):** Manages AFR-specific first sergeant program requirements.
- **Host Wing Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) (§2.12):** Provides installation-level oversight of first sergeant management.
- **First Sergeant (§2.13):** Works directly for and derives authority from the unit commander (§1.1.1); serves as the dedicated focal point for readiness, health, morale, welfare, and quality of life within the unit; responds to unit member needs 24/7 (§1.1.2); leads principally outside the operational chain of command (§1.1.3); is not a replacement for the chain of command (§1.1.4).
- **Unit Commander (§1.3.1):** Provides authority to the first sergeant; maintains a close professional advisory relationship with the first sergeant.
- **WG/CC or equivalent (§6.4.7):** Serves as approval authority for over-grade requests during AFR rotation, with CCM recommendation.
- **AFPC/DP2OSS (§6.8.3):** May waive assignment codes to facilitate needs of the Air Force.
- **Tier Waiver Approval Authorities (§Preamble):** Approve waiver requests for tiered compliance items (T-0 through T-3) submitted through the chain of command.

## Key Requirements

1. **Mandatory Compliance:** Compliance with this publication is mandatory for all RegAF, ANG, and AFR personnel (§Preamble).
2. **Eligibility & Minimum Qualifications:** Personnel must meet eligibility criteria and minimum qualifications outlined in §3.1–3.2, and must maintain those qualifications throughout their tenure (§3.3).
3. **Selection Process:** RegAF (§4.1, §4.3), ANG (§4.4), and AFR (§4.5) each follow distinct nomination and application procedures for first sergeant selection.
4. **AFSC Award & Academy Attendance:** First sergeants must receive the 8F000 AFSC (§5.1) and attend the USAF First Sergeant Academy (§5.2–5.3).
5. **Installation Orientation:** First sergeants must complete an installation orientation upon assignment (§5.4; sample checklist at Attachment 2).
6. **Continuous Development:** First sergeants are required to engage in continuous professional development (§5.5); additional duty first sergeant symposiums support this requirement (§5.6).
7. **Tenure:** RegAF first sergeants serve a standard 4-year tenure in the 8F000 SDI; extensions beyond four years require SDM approval (§2.1.13, §6.1–6.2). ANG and AFR tenure rules are in §6.3 and §6.4 respectively.
8. **Withdrawal Procedures:** Both voluntary and involuntary SDI withdrawal processes are prescribed, with required memorandum formats (§6.5–6.7, Figures 6.1–6.2).
9. **Additional Duty First Sergeants:** Full-time additional duty first sergeants are governed by §7.1–7.2, subject to specific philosophy and utilization rules.
10. **Professionalism & Relationships:** First sergeants must maintain professional relationships per AFI 36-2909, avoid favoritism, and be mindful of the appearance of impropriety (§1.2). They must not serve as a replacement for the chain of command (§1.1.4).
11. **First Sergeant Councils:** Installation-level first sergeant councils are structured per §8.1–8.2; they are distinguished from private organizations (§8.3).
12. **Manpower & Utilization:** Manpower standards, organization of assignment, duties, accommodations, security clearance, office symbol, rater designation, and family association rules are prescribed in §10.1–10.8.
13. **Records Management:** All records created under this AFI must be maintained per AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per the AF Records Information Management System (§Preamble).
14. **Supplements:** All supplements to this publication must be routed to the OPR for coordination and approved by the Human Resource Management Strategic Board per AFI 36-8101 (§Preamble).
15. **Tiered Waiver Compliance:** Requirements annotated with Tier numbers (T-0 through T-3) require waiver requests through the chain of command to the designated approval authority; non-tiered items may be waived by the requestor's commander (§Preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Authority of the SDM vs. CCM:** The SDM (§2.1) holds approval authority for beyond-4-year tenure and cross-SDI applications, while the CCM (§2.7, §2.12) holds MAJCOM/installation-level oversight. The boundary between SDM functional authority and CCM advisory/oversight authority is not crisply delineated in the extracted text, which could create friction in contested decisions.
2. **Additional Duty First Sergeant Selection & Training Standards:** Chapter 7 addresses philosophy and full-time additional duty first sergeants, but the extracted text does not clarify whether additional duty first sergeants must meet identical eligibility criteria (Chapter 3) or complete the same academy training (Chapter 5) as primary first sergeants. This could lead to inconsistent application across units.
3. **First Sergeant Council Governance:** Section 8.3 distinguishes councils from private organizations, but the extracted text does not detail the council's formal authority, charter requirements, or relationship to the CCM—potentially leaving governance ambiguous at installations.
4. **Over-Grade Approval During AFR Rotation (§6.4.7):** The approval authority is "WG/CC or equivalent," but "equivalent" is not defined, which may cause inconsistency in reserve component execution.
5. **AFPC/DP2OSS Code Waiver Authority (§6.8.3):** The scope and conditions under which assignment codes may be waived to "facilitate needs of the Air Force" are not bounded, leaving broad discretion without stated criteria or documentation requirements.
