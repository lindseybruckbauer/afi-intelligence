# AFI 36-2113: THE FIRST SERGEANT

**OPR:** HQ USAF/CCC | **Effective:** 11 June 2020 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2113, 18 November 2014

## Purpose & Scope

This AFI provides guidance for the management of the Air Force First Sergeant Program, including the roles, responsibilities, eligibility, selection, classification, training, tenure, assignment, and utilization of first sergeants. It applies to Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR) personnel. The publication is mandatory for compliance and may be supplemented at any level, subject to coordination with the OPR and approval by the Human Resource Management Strategic Board per AFI 36-8101 (§1.1, opening paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services:** Develops policy for the First Sergeant Program in collaboration with the Chief of Air Force Reserve and the Director of the Air National Guard (opening paragraph).
- **USAF First Sergeant Special Duty Manager (SDM):** Oversees first sergeant program management at the Air Staff level; serves as the approval authority for RegAF requests to serve beyond four years in the 8F000 SDI (§2.1, §2.1.14); serves as the approval authority for RegAF applications for members currently serving in another SDI (§2.1.15).
- **AFPC/DP2W (AEF Operations and Readiness Division):** Supports first sergeant assignment and readiness operations (§2.2).
- **AFPC First Sergeant Special Duty Assignment Functional Area Manager (FAM):** Manages functional area assignments for first sergeants (§2.3). AFPC/DP2OSS may waive codes to facilitate needs of the Air Force (§6.8.3).
- **Air Force Executive Talent Management Office – The Chief's Group:** Supports first sergeant talent management (§2.4).
- **HQ AETC, Manpower and Personnel:** Supports training and education functions (§2.5).
- **USAF First Sergeant Academy Commandant:** Oversees first sergeant academy curriculum and operations (§2.6).
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM):** Provides oversight of first sergeant matters at the MAJCOM/DRU level (§2.7).
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Command First Sergeant:** Serves as the MAJCOM/DRU-level first sergeant advisor (§2.8).
- **RegAF MAJCOM/DRU Headquarters First Sergeant:** Performs first sergeant duties at the headquarters level (§2.9).
- **ANG Component Functional Manager:** Manages ANG-specific first sergeant program functions (§2.10).
- **AFR Component Functional Manager:** Manages AFR-specific first sergeant program functions (§2.11).
- **Host Wing Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM):** Provides wing-level oversight; recommends approval for over-grade requests during rotation alongside the WG/CC or equivalent (§2.12, §6.4.7).
- **First Sergeant:** Works directly for and derives authority from the unit commander; serves as a dedicated focal point for readiness, health, morale, welfare, and quality of life; builds and maintains a mission-ready force; ensures the force understands the commander's policies, goals, and objectives; responds to unit member needs 24/7; leads principally outside the operational chain of command; is not a replacement for the chain of command (§1.1, §2.13).
- **Unit Commander:** The first sergeant serves as a key advisor to the commander; the commander is the source of the first sergeant's authority (§1.1.1, §1.3.1).
- **Tier Waiver Approval Authorities:** Waiver requests for tiered compliance items (T-0 through T-3) are submitted through the chain of command to the appropriate tier authority; non-tiered items go to the requestor's commander (opening paragraph).

## Key Requirements

1. **Compliance is mandatory.** All personnel subject to this AFI must comply; waivers for tiered requirements (T-0 through T-3) must be routed through the chain of command to the appropriate approval authority (opening paragraph; see AFI 33-360).
2. **Eligibility & Minimum Qualifications.** Personnel must meet established criteria and minimum qualifications to serve as a first sergeant and must maintain those qualifications throughout their tour (§3.1–§3.3).
3. **Selection Process.** RegAF, ANG, and AFR each follow distinct nomination and application processes for initial first sergeant selection (§4.1–§4.5).
4. **AFSC Award & Academy Attendance.** First sergeants must attend the USAF First Sergeant Academy; AFSC 8F000 is awarded upon completion of training requirements (§5.1–§5.3).
5. **Installation Orientation.** Newly assigned first sergeants must complete an installation orientation (§5.4; Attachment 2 provides a sample checklist).
6. **Continuous Development.** First sergeants are required to pursue ongoing professional development (§5.5).
7. **Tenure.** RegAF first sergeants serve a four-year tenure; service beyond four years in the 8F000 SDI requires approval by the SDM (§6.1–§6.2, §2.1.14).
8. **Release & Return to 8F000 SDI.** Specific procedures govern release from and return to the first sergeant SDI (§6.5–§6.6).
9. **Early Withdrawal.** Voluntary and involuntary withdrawal procedures are prescribed, including required memorandum formats (§6.7, Figures 6.1–6.2).
10. **Additional Duty First Sergeants.** Units without an authorized full-time first sergeant position may appoint additional duty first sergeants under prescribed guidelines (§7.1–§7.2).
11. **First Sergeant Councils.** Installations establish first sergeant councils to foster communication and mutual support (§8.1–§8.3); councils are private organizations (§8.3).
12. **First Sergeant Device & Special Duty Incentives.** Wear of the first sergeant diamond device and eligibility for special duty incentive pay are governed by Chapter 9 (§9.1–§9.2).
13. **Manpower & Utilization.** First sergeant manpower requirements, organizational assignment, duty scope, accommodations, security clearance, office symbol, rater designation, and family association rules are specified (§10.1–§10.8).
14. **Professionalism.** First sergeants must maintain professional relationships with all unit members, avoid favoritism or the appearance of impropriety, and adhere to AFI 36-2909 (§1.2).
15. **Records Management.** All records created under this AFI must be maintained per AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per the Air Force Records Information Management System (opening paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **SDM Authority Scope.** The SDM is identified as the approval authority for beyond-four-year tenure (§2.1.14) and cross-SDI applications (§2.1.15), but the extracted text does not fully detail the criteria or standards the SDM uses in adjudicating these decisions, potentially leaving discretion undefined.
2. **Over-Grade Request Authority.** Section 6.4.7 states over-grade requests during rotation require a CCM recommendation and approval by the "WG/CC or equivalent," but the phrase "approval authority" preceding WG/CC is ambiguous—it is unclear whether the WG/CC *is* the approval authority or whether a separate approval authority exists alongside the WG/CC.
3. **AFPC Code Waiver Authority.** Section 6.8.3 grants AFPC/DP2OSS authority to waive codes to "facilitate needs of the Air Force" without specifying the scope, limitations, or documentation requirements for such waivers.
4. **Relationship with Superintendents.** Section 1.1.3 notes that first sergeants are seldom the ranking SNCO in the unit and must "balance communication with the organization's leaders," but the document (as extracted) does not provide detailed guidance on delineating responsibilities between the first sergeant and the unit superintendent, a historically ambiguous boundary.
5. **Implements Statement.** The publication header did not specify the implementing directive, though the body text identifies AFPD 36-21; this inconsistency could cause confusion in publication tracking.
