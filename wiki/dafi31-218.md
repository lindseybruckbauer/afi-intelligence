# DAFI 31-218: Motor Vehicle Supervision

**OPR:** AF/A4 (DCS/Logistics, Engineering & Force Protection) | **Effective:** 10 December 2021 | **Implements:** Joint Publication AR 190-5/OPNAV 11200.5D/AFI 31-218/MCO 5110.1D/DLAR 5720.1 (22 May 2006) | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope
This DAF Guidance Memorandum (DAFGM 2021-01) re-designates AFI 31-218, Motor Vehicle Supervision, as a Department of the Air Force Instruction and makes immediate, mandatory changes to two provisions concerning personal protective equipment (PPE) requirements and traffic violation descriptions. The memorandum applies to all MAJCOMs, FOAs, and DRUs under Distribution C. Compliance is mandatory, and where inconsistent with other DAF publications, this memorandum prevails per DAFI 33-360.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **Secretary of the Air Force:** Orders the re-designation of AFI 31-218 as a DAF Instruction and authorizes the guidance memorandum changes.
- **AF/A4 (DCS/Logistics, Engineering & Force Protection — Lt Gen Warren D. Berry):** Issues the DAFGM and directs compliance across the force.
- **MAJCOMs/FOAs/DRUs:** Required to comply with the memorandum's changes immediately upon issuance.
- **All personnel creating records under this publication:** Must maintain records per AFI 33-322 and dispose of them per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule in the Air Force Records Management System.

## Key Requirements
1. **PPE Reference Update (§4-2c(1)(c)):** The paragraph is amended to direct users to DoDI 6055.04, *DoD Motor Vehicle and Traffic Safety*, Paragraph 3.3.d for a comprehensive list of required Personal Protective Equipment, replacing previous PPE guidance within the AFI itself.
2. **Traffic Violation Table Update (Table 5-2, Violation #22):** The words "and/or reflectorized vest" are deleted from the violation description, removing reflectorized vests from the associated requirement or violation criteria.
3. **Publication Re-designation:** AFI 31-218 is formally re-designated as DAFI 31-218, making it a Department of the Air Force-level instruction applicable to both Air Force and Space Force.
4. **Sunset Clause:** The DAFGM becomes void after one year from 10 December 2021 or upon publication of an interim change or rewrite of DAFI 31-218, whichever occurs first.

## Gaps & Ambiguities
- **(a) Unclear authority assignment:** The original OPR for the base DAFI 31-218 is not identified within the DAFGM text; only the issuing office (AF/A4) is stated. It is unclear which office holds day-to-day OPR responsibilities for implementation at installation level.
- **(b) Topics not fully addressed:** The DAFGM modifies only two discrete provisions. The full scope of motor vehicle supervision—traffic enforcement, licensing, registration, impoundment, and other topics implied by the title—is not addressed in this document, as it relies entirely on the underlying joint regulation (AR 190-5 et al.) and the base AFI/DAFI 31-218 text, neither of which is substantively reproduced here.
- **(c) Potential conflicts:** The removal of "reflectorized vest" language from Table 5-2, Violation #22, while simultaneously redirecting PPE requirements to DoDI 6055.04, Paragraph 3.3.d, could create ambiguity if DoDI 6055.04 still requires reflectorized vests—personnel may be uncertain whether the vest requirement is eliminated or merely relocated to the DoD-level reference.
- **(c) Sunset clause risk:** The DAFGM expired by 10 December 2022 unless an interim change or rewrite was published; if no successor action was taken, the status of these changes becomes unclear.
