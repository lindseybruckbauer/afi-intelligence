# DAFI 31-218: Motor Vehicle Supervision

**OPR:** AF/A4 (DCS/Logistics, Engineering & Force Protection) | **Effective:** 10 December 2021 | **Implements:** Joint publication AR 190-5/OPNAV 11200.5D/AFI 31-218/MCO 5110.1D/DLAR 5720.1 (22 May 2006) | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope
This DAF Guidance Memorandum (DAFGM2021-01) re-designates AFI 31-218 as a Department of the Air Force Instruction and makes immediate, mandatory changes to DAFI 31-218, Motor Vehicle Supervision. The publication governs motor vehicle traffic supervision on DAF installations and applies to all MAJCOMs, FOAs, and DRUs (Distribution C). Where inconsistent with other DAF publications, this memorandum prevails per DAFI 33-360. The memorandum becomes void one year from 10 December 2021 or upon publication of an interim change or rewrite of DAFI 31-218, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **Secretary of the Air Force:** Orders the re-designation of AFI 31-218 as a DAF Instruction and authorizes the guidance memorandum changes.
- **AF/A4 (DCS/Logistics, Engineering & Force Protection):** Issues the DAFGM; signed by Lt Gen Warren D. Berry.
- **MAJCOMs/FOAs/DRUs:** Required to comply with the memorandum's mandatory provisions.

## Key Requirements
1. **Re-designation compliance:** AFI 31-218 is re-designated as DAFI 31-218; all references and usage must reflect the DAF Instruction designation.
2. **PPE reference update (§4-2c(1)(c)):** Personnel must refer to DoDI 6055.04, *DoD Motor Vehicle and Traffic Safety*, Paragraph 3.3.d for the comprehensive list of required Personal Protective Equipment, replacing previous language in the instruction.
3. **Reflectorized vest requirement removed (Table 5-2, Violation #22):** The words "and/or reflectorized vest" are deleted from the violation description, eliminating the reflectorized vest as an element of that traffic violation.
4. **Records management:** All records created under this publication must be maintained per AFI 33-322 and disposed of per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule in the Air Force Records Management System.
5. **Precedence:** Where this memorandum conflicts with other DAF publications, this memorandum prevails per DAFI 33-360.

## Gaps & Ambiguities
- **(a) Unclear authority assignment:** The OPR is not explicitly labeled in the document header; AF/A4 is inferred from the "FROM" line and signatory block but not formally designated as OPR.
- **(b) Limited substantive content:** The title "Motor Vehicle Supervision" implies comprehensive traffic supervision policy (enforcement procedures, licensing, speed limits, impoundment, etc.), but this two-page DAFGM addresses only two narrow changes (PPE reference and reflectorized vest deletion). The underlying joint regulation (AR 190-5 et al., dated 2006) contains the full policy but is not reproduced here.
- **(c) Expiration concern:** The memorandum becomes void after one year (by December 2022). If no interim change or rewrite was published, a compliance gap would exist for the two changes directed.
- **(d) PPE specifics deferred:** By pointing to DoDI 6055.04, Paragraph 3.3.d for the "comprehensive list" of required PPE, the DAF instruction itself no longer contains an organic PPE requirement list, creating dependency on an external DoD publication that could be revised independently.
