# AFI 31-213: Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards and Off-Installation Liaison and Operations

**OPR:** Unknown | **Effective:** 27 July 2006 (Certified Current: 23 April 2026) | **Implements:** Not specified in header | **Supersedes:** Not specified in header

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 31-213 is a joint-service publication (also designated Army Regulation 190–24, OPNAVINST 1620.2A, MCO 1620.2D, and COMDTINST 1620.1E) that governs the establishment and operation of Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards (AFDCBs) and addresses off-installation liaison and operations conducted by military police and law enforcement elements. It applies to personnel and installations across the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard, providing a unified framework for coordinating with civilian authorities to maintain good order and discipline in areas surrounding military installations.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards (AFDCBs):** Serve as the joint-service mechanism to advise commanders on off-installation conditions that may adversely affect the health, safety, morale, and welfare of military personnel.
- **Installation Commanders:** Presumed responsible for establishing or participating in AFDCBs and implementing off-limits or other control measures based on board recommendations.
- **Military Police / Law Enforcement:** Presumed responsible for conducting off-installation liaison and operations in coordination with civilian law enforcement agencies.

## Key Requirements
1. Establishment and operation of Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards in areas with significant military population, per joint-service policy.
2. Coordination among the five service branches (Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard) for disciplinary control measures affecting off-installation areas.
3. Conduct of off-installation liaison with civilian law enforcement and local authorities.
4. Implementation of off-limits designations or other control actions recommended by AFDCBs to protect service members.
