# AFI 36-1301: Management of Acquisition Key Leadership Positions

**OPR:** SAF/AQH | **Effective:** 23 July 2019 | **Implements:** HAFMD 1-10 | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-1301, 19 October 2015

## Purpose & Scope

This AFI implements HAFMD 1-10 and establishes authority for the Defense Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics Workforce Education, Training, and Career Development Program as delegated to SAF/AQ (the Service Acquisition Executive). It provides direction on managing, coding, and tracking acquisition key leadership positions (KLPs) and applies to all Air Force personnel who manage, administer, or support KLPs, all members assigned to KLPs, and all agencies that select members for KLPs. The publication applies to HQ Air Force Reserve Command (but not AFRC units) and the Air National Guard (§1.2, Applicability paragraph). Compliance is mandatory.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/AQ (Service Acquisition Executive):** Designates key leadership positions (§1.3); approves selection of PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs and Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.4.4); sets tenure for those same positions (§1.6.1); approves position and tenure waivers for PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs/Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1); may approve early release from tenure agreements (§1.7.2).
- **SAF/AQH (Director, Acquisition Career Management):** Assists the Service Acquisition Executive on KLP policy and management (§1.3.1); receives staffed recommendations for KLP designation (§1.4.2); receives copies of approved waivers from commands (§1.7.1).
- **Program Executive Officers:** Recommend program KLPs for SAE designation based on MDAP requirements and portfolio needs (§1.4).
- **Major Commands (MAJCOMs):** Recommend KLP designations to the SAE (§1.4); ensure KLPs are correctly coded in manpower and personnel systems (§1.4.7, T-3); request position waivers when candidates do not meet qualification requirements (§1.5.2).
- **Hiring Authority (for non-§1.4.4 KLPs):** Retains final selection approval authority for all other KLPs not reserved to the SAE (§1.4.5).
- **Appropriate Senior Level Official within the Command:** Approves position and tenure waivers for all KLPs not reserved to the SAE (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1); provides a copy of approved waivers to SAF/AQH.
- **Gaining Organization:** Ensures civilian hires sign a tenure agreement (DD Form 2889) reflecting the required tenure period (§1.6).
- **Individuals in KLPs:** Must meet qualification requirements prior to assignment (§1.5.2); must remain in position for the tenure period set by the SAE (§1.6); must comply with acquisition workforce management requirements per AFI 63-101/20-101, Chapter 10 (§1.5.6).

## Key Requirements

1. **KLP Designation:** Key leadership positions are designated solely by the Service Acquisition Executive (§1.3).
2. **Mandatory KLPs:** PEOs, Deputy PEOs, Senior Contracting Officials (Air Staff/MAJCOM level), Senior Center Contracting Officials, and other SAE-designated positions are mandatory KLPs (§1.3.2). For ACAT I/Business Cat I programs, PM, Deputy PM, Chief Engineer, Product Support Manager, Chief Developmental Tester, and Program Lead BFM are also mandatory (§1.3.3). ACAT II PMs are mandatory KLPs (§1.3.5).
3. **Acquisition Corps Membership:** All KLP candidates must be Acquisition Corps members (§1.5.1, T-1).
4. **Qualification Requirements:** Candidates must meet DoD position qualification requirements per DoDI 5000.66 upon selection—no 6-month grace period (§1.5.2, T-0; Summary of Changes).
5. **Five Selection Factors:** Candidates are evaluated on education, training, experience, competencies (executive leadership, program execution, technical management, business management), and currency (§1.5.4).
6. **Position Coding:** Positions must be aligned to a single function per DoD/AF coding guidance (§1.4.3, T-0). Owning units must identify the KLP in the civilian position description (§1.4.7, T-3).
7. **Tenure Agreements:** Civilian KLP incumbents must sign DD Form 2889; military members sign an active duty service commitment agreement (§1.6, T-1).
8. **ACAT I PM Tenure Periods:** Divided into Program Definition (Analysis of Alternatives through Milestone B) and Program Execution (post-Milestone B through IOC); outside those periods, tenure is 4 years or through the nearest milestone (§1.6.2).
9. **Waivers:** Position waivers required when candidates do not meet qualifications; tenure waivers required for early release. SAE approves waivers for senior KLPs; command senior officials approve all others and provide copies to SAF/AQH (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1).
10. **Waiver Tiering:** Requests for waivers of tiered requirements must be submitted through the chain of command to the appropriate tier waiver authority (Applicability paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignment:**
- The instruction states the "appropriate senior level official within the command" approves waivers for non-SAE-reserved KLPs (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1) but does not define the specific grade, title, or position level required, leaving discretion open to interpretation across commands.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed:**
- The document text cuts off at §1.6.2.3, so the full tenure waiver process (§1.7), detailed responsibilities of assignment/employment teams, and Attachment 2 (five-factor evaluation criteria) are not fully visible in the provided extract. Guidance on removal or reassignment from KLPs beyond waiver release is not apparent.
- Conditional KLPs (§1.3.4) are designated "as necessary when the function is required," but no criteria or process for determining when a function is required are specified.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- The instruction removes the prior 6-month grace period for meeting qualifications, requiring compliance at selection. This could create tension with DoDI 5000.66 provisions if DoD-level guidance permits any transition period for newly selected individuals.
- Additional KLP designation requests are limited to GS-15/O-6 and above (§1.4.8), potentially excluding senior GS-14 positions that may carry equivalent program responsibility in practice.
