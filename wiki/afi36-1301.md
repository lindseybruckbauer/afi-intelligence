# AFI 36-1301: Management of Acquisition Key Leadership Positions

**OPR:** SAF/AQH | **Effective:** 23 July 2019 | **Implements:** HAF MD 1-10 | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-1301, 19 October 2015

## Purpose & Scope

This AFI establishes authority and provides direction for managing, coding, and tracking acquisition key leadership positions (KLPs) within the Air Force, as delegated to the Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Acquisition, Technology and Logistics (SAF/AQ) in the role of Service Acquisition Executive (SAE). It applies to all Air Force personnel who manage, administer, support, are assigned to, or select members for key leadership positions. The publication applies to Headquarters Air Force Reserve Command (but not AFRC units) and the Air National Guard (§1.2, applicability paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Service Acquisition Executive (SAF/AQ):** Designates key leadership positions (§1.3); approves selection of PEOs, DPEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs and DPMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.4.4); sets tenure for those same positions (§1.6.1); approves position and tenure waivers for PEOs, DPEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs/DPMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.7); may approve early release from tenure agreements (§1.7.2.1).
- **SAF/AQH (Director, Acquisition Career Management):** Assists the SAE on KLP policy and management (§1.3.1); serves as coordination authority for supplements to this AFI; receives copies of approved waivers from commands.
- **Program Executive Officers:** Recommend program KLP designations to the SAE (§1.4); assess Major Defense Acquisition Program requirements and portfolio needs for KLP identification (§1.4.1–1.4.2).
- **Major Commands:** Recommend KLP designations (§1.4); ensure KLPs are correctly coded in manpower and personnel systems (§1.4.7, T-3); request SAE designation of additional KLPs at GS-15/O-6 and above (§1.4.8).
- **Hiring Authority:** Retains final selection approval for all KLPs not reserved to the SAE under §1.4.4 (§1.4.5).
- **Appropriate Senior Level Official within the Command:** Approves position and tenure waivers for all KLPs not reserved to the SAE (§1.7).
- **Gaining Organization:** Ensures civilian hires sign a tenure agreement (DD Form 2889) reflecting the required tenure period (§1.6).
- **KLP Incumbents:** Must comply with Air Force Acquisition Workforce Management requirements per AFI 63-101/20-101, Chapter 10 (§1.5.6); must remain in position for the designated tenure period (§1.6).

## Key Requirements

1. All candidates for KLPs **must be Acquisition Corps members** (§1.5.1, T-1).
2. Candidates **must meet DoD position qualification requirements** prescribed in DoDI 5000.66 **upon selection**—the previous 6-month grace period has been removed (§1.5.2, T-0; Summary of Changes).
3. Positions **must be aligned to a single function** per DoD and AF coding guidance (§1.4.3, T-0).
4. Owning units **must identify the position as a KLP in the civilian position description** (§1.4.7, T-3).
5. Civilian hires **must sign a tenure agreement** (DD Form 2889); military members sign an active duty service commitment agreement (§1.6, T-1).
6. ACAT I PM tenure is structured around two distinct periods: **Program Definition** (Analysis of Alternatives through Milestone B) and **Program Execution** (post-Milestone B through Initial Operational Capability) (§1.6.2). Outside these periods, tenure is **4 years or through the nearest milestone** (§1.6.2.3).
7. Members who do not meet qualification requirements upon selection **must have a position waiver and training plan** at time of nomination (Summary of Changes; §1.5.2).
8. Mandatory KLPs include PEOs, DPEOs, Senior Contracting Officials (Air Staff/MAJCOM level), Senior Center Contracting Officials, ACAT I/Business Cat I program leads (PM, DPM, Chief Engineer, Product Support Manager, Chief Developmental Tester, Business Financial Manager), and ACAT II PMs (§1.3.2–1.3.5).
9. KLP selection for PEO, DPEO, ACAT I/Business Cat I PM/DPM, and ACAT II PM requires **SAE approval** considering program factors, individual qualifications, and functional requirements (§1.4.4).
10. All supplements to this AFI **must be routed to SAF/AQH** for coordination prior to certification and approval (applicability paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Tenure for non-ACAT I positions:** While the SAE "will set" tenure for PEOs, DPEOs, and ACAT I/II PMs (§1.6.1), the instruction does not specify default tenure periods or guidance for all other KLPs, leaving tenure determination for positions such as Chief Engineers, Product Support Managers, and Senior Contracting Officials unclear.
2. **"Appropriate senior level official within the command":** The authority to approve waivers for non-SAE-reserved KLPs is assigned to this role (§1.7), but the instruction does not define the specific position or grade level that constitutes this authority, potentially leading to inconsistent application across commands.
3. **Functional lead KLP designation criteria:** Section 1.3.4 states functional leads (e.g., Contracting Officer, Cost Estimator) are designated "as necessary when the function is required based on the phase or type of acquisition program," but provides no criteria or process for determining when a function is "required," leaving this to undefined judgment.
4. **AFRC applicability scope:** The instruction applies to HQ AFRC but explicitly excludes AFRC units, yet does not clarify how KLP management responsibilities (e.g., coding, waiver processing) are handled when AFRC units support acquisition programs.
5. **Text truncation:** The document text ends mid-sentence at §1.6.2.3 (describing milestones), and Attachment 2 (summarizing the five selection factors) is referenced but not included in the parsed text, so completeness of tenure rules and qualification factor details cannot be fully assessed.
