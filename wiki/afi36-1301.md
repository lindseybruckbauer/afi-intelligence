# AFI 36-1301: Management of Acquisition Key Leadership Positions

**OPR:** SAF/AQH | **Effective:** 23 July 2019 | **Implements:** HAF MD 1-10 | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-1301, 19 October 2015

## Purpose & Scope

This AFI establishes authority and provides direction for managing, coding, and tracking acquisition key leadership positions (KLPs) within the Air Force, implementing the Defense Acquisition, Technology, and Logistics Workforce Education, Training, and Career Development Program as delegated to SAF/AQ (§1.2). It applies to all Air Force personnel who manage, administer, or support KLPs; all members assigned to KLPs; and all agencies that select members for KLPs. It applies to HQ Air Force Reserve Command (but not AFRC units) and the Air National Guard (Introductory paragraph). Compliance is mandatory.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/AQ (Service Acquisition Executive):** Designates key leadership positions (§1.3); approves selection of PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs and Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.4.4); sets tenure for those same positions (§1.6.1); approves position and tenure waivers for PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs/Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1); may approve early release from tenure agreements (§1.7.2.1).
- **SAF/AQH (Director, Acquisition Career Management):** Assists the SAE on KLP policy and management (§1.3.1); serves as coordination authority for all supplements to the AFI (Introductory paragraph); receives copies of approved waivers from commands (§1.7.1).
- **Program Executive Officers:** Recommend program KLP designations to the SAE based on portfolio needs (§1.4); staff recommendations through SAF/AQH (§1.4.2); may request position waivers for candidates who do not meet qualification requirements (§1.5.2).
- **Major Commands (MAJCOMs):** Recommend KLP designations to the SAE (§1.4); ensure KLPs are correctly coded in manpower and personnel systems (§1.4.7, T-3); may request SAE designation of additional KLPs at GS-15/O-6 and above (§1.4.8); may request position waivers (§1.5.2).
- **Appropriate Senior Level Official within the Command:** Approves position and tenure waivers for all KLPs not reserved to the SAE (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1); provides copies of approved waivers to SAF/AQH.
- **Hiring Authority:** Retains final selection approval authority for KLPs not identified in §1.4.4 (§1.4.5).
- **Gaining Organizations:** Must ensure civilian hires sign a tenure agreement (DD Form 2889) reflecting the tenure period (§1.6).
- **KLP Incumbents:** Must comply with acquisition workforce management requirements per AFI 63-101/20-101, Chapter 10 (§1.5.6); must remain in position for the designated tenure period (§1.6).
- **Owning Units:** Must identify the position as a KLP in the civilian position description (§1.4.7, T-3).

## Key Requirements

1. **KLP candidates must be Acquisition Corps members** prior to assignment (§1.5.1, T-1).
2. **Candidates must meet DoD position qualification requirements** per DoDI 5000.66 upon selection—not after; the former 6-month grace period has been removed (§1.5.2, T-0; Summary of Changes).
3. **Positions must be aligned to a single function** per DoD and AF coding guidance (§1.4.3, T-0).
4. **Civilian KLP selectees must sign a tenure agreement** (DD Form 2889); military members sign an active duty service commitment agreement (§1.6, T-1).
5. **ACAT I PM tenure** is structured around two distinct periods: Program Definition (Analysis of Alternatives through Milestone B approval) and Program Execution (post-Milestone B through Initial Operational Capability). Outside these periods, tenure is 4 years or through the nearest milestone (§1.6.2).
6. **Position and tenure waivers** for PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs/Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs require SAE approval; all other KLP waivers are approved by the appropriate senior command official, with copies provided to SAF/AQH (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1).
7. **MAJCOMs must correctly code KLPs** in manpower and personnel systems, and owning units must annotate KLP designation in civilian position descriptions (§1.4.7, T-3).
8. **Five selection factors** must be evaluated for KLP candidates: education, training, experience, competencies (executive leadership, program execution, technical management, business management), and currency (§1.5.4; Attachment 2).
9. **Mandatory KLPs** include PEOs, Deputy PEOs, Senior Contracting Officials (Air Staff/MAJCOM), Senior Center Contracting Officials, and positions identified by the SAE (§1.3.2). For ACAT I/Business Cat I programs, PMs, Deputy PMs, Chief Engineers, Product Support Managers, Chief Developmental Testers, and Business Financial Managers are also mandatory KLPs (§1.3.3).
10. **Supplements** to this AFI may be issued at any level but must be routed to SAF/AQH for coordination prior to certification and approval (Introductory paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignment:**
- The term "appropriate senior level official within the command" for non-SAE waiver approvals (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1) is not precisely defined—no specific grade, position, or organizational level is specified, which could lead to inconsistent waiver authority across commands.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed:**
- The document title and §1.2 reference "managing" KLPs broadly, but the extracted text provides limited detail on tracking mechanisms, reporting requirements, or metrics for KLP management beyond coding in manpower systems.
- Attachment 2 (referenced in §1.5.4) summarizing the five selection factors is not included in the extracted text, leaving the specific evaluation criteria undefined in this summary.
- Tenure details for non-ACAT I positions (e.g., PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT II PMs) are stated as set by the SAE (§1.6.1), but no default periods or guidelines are provided, potentially leading to inconsistency.
- The text is truncated at §1.6.2.3, so later chapters (likely addressing waiver procedures in detail, removal actions, and responsibilities of assignment teams referenced in §1.2) are not fully available for analysis.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- None identified from the available text.
