# AFI 36-1301: Management of Acquisition Key Leadership Positions

**OPR:** SAF/AQH | **Effective:** 23 July 2019 | **Implements:** HAF Mission Directive 1-10; DoDI 5000.66 | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-1301, 19 October 2015

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction establishes authority and provides direction for managing, coding, and tracking acquisition key leadership positions (KLPs) within the Air Force. It applies to all Air Force personnel who manage, administer, or support KLPs, all members assigned to KLPs, and all agencies that select members for KLPs. It applies to Headquarters Air Force Reserve Command (but not AFRC units) and the Air National Guard. Compliance is mandatory. (§1.2, Preamble)

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Service Acquisition Executive (SAF/AQ):** Designates key leadership positions (§1.3); approves selection of PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs and Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.4.4); sets tenure for those same positions (§1.6.1); approves position and tenure waivers for PEOs, Deputy PEOs, ACAT I/Business Cat I PMs/Deputy PMs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1); may approve early release from tenure agreements (§1.7.2).
- **SAF/AQH (Director, Acquisition Career Management):** Assists the SAE on KLP policy and management (§1.3.1); serves as routing destination for supplements, waiver copies, and KLP designation recommendations (Preamble, §1.4.2, §1.7.1).
- **Program Executive Officers:** Recommend program KLP designations to the SAE (§1.4); assess MDAP requirements and portfolio needs for KLP nominations (§1.4.1).
- **Major Commands:** Recommend KLP designations to the SAE (§1.4); ensure KLPs are correctly coded in manpower and personnel systems (§1.4.7, T-3); may request additional KLP designations for GS-15/O-6 and above positions (§1.4.8).
- **Hiring Authority:** Retains final selection approval for all KLPs not reserved to the SAE under §1.4.4 (§1.4.5).
- **Appropriate Senior Level Official (Command):** Approves position and tenure waivers for KLPs not reserved to the SAE (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1); provides copies of approved waivers to SAF/AQH.
- **Gaining Organization:** Ensures civilian hires sign tenure agreements (DD Form 2889) reflecting the SAE-determined tenure period (§1.6).
- **Owning Units:** Identify KLPs in civilian position descriptions (§1.4.7, T-3).
- **KLP Candidates/Incumbents:** Must be Acquisition Corps members (§1.5.1, T-1); must meet DoD position qualification requirements upon selection (§1.5.2, T-0); must comply with acquisition workforce management requirements per AFI 63-101/20-101 (§1.5.6); must serve the tenure period or obtain a waiver (§1.6).

## Key Requirements

1. All candidates for KLPs must be Acquisition Corps members. (§1.5.1, T-1)
2. Candidates must meet DoD position qualification requirements prescribed in DoDI 5000.66 upon selection—the prior 6-month grace period has been eliminated. (§1.5.2, T-0)
3. If candidates do not meet qualification requirements, a position waiver and training plan must be provided at the time of nomination. (Summary of Changes; §1.5.2)
4. Positions must be aligned to a single function per DoD and AF coding guidance. (§1.4.3, T-0)
5. Civilian hires must sign a tenure agreement (DD Form 2889); military members sign an active duty service commitment agreement. (§1.6, T-1)
6. ACAT I PM tenure is structured around two distinct periods: Program Definition (between Analysis of Alternatives and 6 months prior to RFP release through Milestone B) and Program Execution (post-Milestone B through Initial Operational Capability). Outside these periods, tenure is 4 years or through the nearest milestone. (§1.6.2)
7. MAJCOMs must ensure KLPs are correctly coded in manpower and personnel systems and identified in civilian position descriptions. (§1.4.7, T-3)
8. Five factors—education, training, experience, competencies (executive leadership, program execution, technical management, business management), and currency—are required evaluation criteria for KLP candidate selection. (§1.5.4, Attachment 2)
9. Position and tenure waivers for SAE-reserved positions (PEO, Deputy PEO, ACAT I/Business Cat I PM/Deputy PM, ACAT II PM) require SAE approval; all other KLP waivers are approved by the appropriate senior command official with a copy to SAF/AQH. (§1.7.1, §1.7.2.1)
10. All supplements to this AFI must be routed to SAF/AQH for coordination prior to certification and approval. (Preamble)

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Tenure details for non-ACAT I PMs partially unclear:** The document specifies detailed tenure periods for ACAT I PMs (§1.6.2) and states the SAE sets tenure for PEOs, Deputy PEOs, and ACAT II PMs (§1.6.1), but the text is truncated before fully addressing tenure standards for other KLP categories (e.g., functional leads, senior contracting officials). It is unclear whether a default tenure period applies.
2. **"Appropriate senior level official" undefined:** For non-SAE-reserved KLP waivers, the approving authority is described only as "the appropriate senior level official within the command" (§1.7.1), without specifying a minimum grade or title, potentially leading to inconsistent application across commands.
3. **Waiver training plan requirements lack specificity:** While the Summary of Changes mandates a training plan accompany waivers at nomination, no section in the extracted text prescribes the content, format, or approval timeline for such plans.
4. **Functional lead KLP designation conditionality:** Section 1.3.4 states certain functional leads "will be designated as key leadership positions… as necessary when the function is required based on the phase or type of acquisition program," but provides no criteria or process for determining when the function is "required," leaving this to interpretation.
5. **AFRC and ANG applicability scope:** The instruction applies to HQ AFRC but not AFRC units, and applies to the ANG. The rationale for excluding AFRC units while including ANG is not explained and could create coverage gaps for Reserve acquisition personnel assigned to KLPs at the unit level.
