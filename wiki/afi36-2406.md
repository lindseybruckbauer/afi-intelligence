# AFI 36-2406: Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems

**OPR:** AF/A1PPP | **Effective:** 22 August 2025 (Incorporating Change 1, 27 May 2026) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-24, Military Evaluations | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2406, 6 August 2024

## Purpose & Scope

AFI 36-2406 provides guidance and procedures for implementing the USAF Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems, including how to prepare, submit, and manage evaluation forms. It governs the performance feedback process, officer performance evaluations (Officer ALQ Evaluations), enlisted ALQ evaluations/Enlisted Performance Briefs (EPBs), referral evaluations, stratifications, rating chain requirements, and the evaluation appeals process. The instruction applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard; it does **not** apply to the United States Space Force (USSF). Compliance is mandatory (§1.1, preamble).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority for the publication.
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Co-developed the instruction; AF/A1PP and AF/A1PPP approve policy deviations, supplements, and locally created forms (preamble, §1.13).
- **AFPC/DPPSP (Promotions, Evaluations and Recognitions Policy Branch):** Approval authority for non-tiered waiver requests (with AF/A1P concurrence); approves supplements and locally created forms; sole authority to correct digitally signed evaluations after transmission to AFPC (preamble, Table rules).
- **AFPC MLR Office:** Approves exceptions to maintain integrity in the Officer Evaluation System and ensure fair consideration for all affected officers; may approve changes up to one duty day prior to a central selection board in extreme circumstances (extracted authorities).
- **ARPC/PB (Air Reserve Personnel Center Promotion Board Secretariat):** Receives published supplements; serves as approval authority for ARC evaluation changes, including case-by-case exceptions up to one duty day prior to central selection boards (extracted authorities).
- **ARPC/DPTSE:** Approval authority for ARC members requesting consideration for non-rated periods of supervision (§1.4.11.3).
- **CSAF:** Only authority to approve exceptions to general officer evaluation management; HAF Deputy Chief of Staff, Personnel may exercise similar authority in general officer management cases (extracted authorities).
- **Wing Commander (or Deputy Wing Commander equivalent):** Final approval/disapproval authority when a lower-level approval authority recommends disapproval of a request (extracted authorities).
- **Raters/Evaluators:** Must meet evaluator requirements (§1.5); comply with mandatory considerations (§1.8) and mandatory comments (§1.9); conduct performance feedback sessions (Chapter 2); prepare evaluations per Chapters 3–4.
- **Higher Level Reviewers (HLRs):** Review and endorse evaluations; authorized to use stratification statements from intermediate officials (§3.14, §3.15.6.7.2).
- **Referral Reviewers:** May grant extensions when ratees need additional time for rebuttals, e.g., due to non-availability of Area Defense Counsel or rebuttal return for exceeding 10 pages (§1.11).
- **AFELM/CC (Joint Agencies):** Authorized to sign ALQ evaluations in lieu of the forced distributor when the forced distributor signs the MEL (§4.12.5.4).
- **ERAB (Evaluation Review Appeals Board):** Authorized to modify evaluations differing from the applicant's request, including directing corrections in lieu of voiding (§10.2.4).
- **Ratees:** May grant access to completed feedback forms at their discretion (§2.9.3); must acknowledge evaluations (§3.18).
- **Field Agencies (MAJCOMs, NAFs, Wings, FOAs):** May not publish supplements that change basic policies/procedures or duplicate text without AF/A1PP and AFPC/DPPSP approval (preamble).

## Key Requirements

1. **Mandatory compliance** with tiered requirements (T-0 through T-3); waiver requests must be routed through the chain of command to the appropriate tier waiver approval authority (preamble).
2. **Physical fitness assessment scores** must be documented on officer and enlisted evaluations per AFGM 2026-01 implementation (Summary of Changes).
3. **Performance feedback sessions** must be conducted and documented per the timelines in Table 2.1; failure to conduct or document a session is addressed at §2.10.
4. **Evaluators must meet minimum supervision day thresholds** as specified in §1.5, §3.12, and §4.8 to render evaluations.
5. **Referral evaluations** must follow the procedures in §1.11, including providing the ratee an opportunity to acknowledge and rebut; referral memoranda must conform to the prescribed format (Figures 1.1–1.2).
6. **Mandatory comments** are required under §1.9; waivers require a written finding by the approval authority that the mandatory comments are not in the best interests of the Air Force and would unduly harm the ratee (§1.8.1.1.1.3.1).
7. **Prohibited considerations and comments** must be avoided per §1.12 (general) and §3.16 (officer-specific).
8. **Stratifications** must conform to authorized peer groups (Table 3.4) and updated quotas (§3.15); Joint and DAF SNCO stratification guidance updated per Change 1.
9. **Records management:** All records generated must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (preamble).
10. **Privacy Act compliance:** Collection and maintenance of evaluation data is authorized by DoDI 5400.11; applicable SORNs are F036 AF PC A and F036 AFPC T (preamble).
11. **Supplements** initiated at MAJCOM level or below require AF/A1PP and AFPC/DPPSP approval before publication; published copies must be sent to AF/A1PP, AFPC/DPPSP, and ARPC/PB (preamble).
12. **Corrections to digitally signed evaluations** after transmission may only be made by AFPC or ARPC (Table rules, extracted authorities).
13. **Performance objectives must align** with major performance areas and competencies with Airman Leadership Qualities (ALQs) (Summary of Changes).
14. **AF Form 717, Performance Objectives and Feedback Form**, is prescribed and must be used per the updated feedback system guidance (Summary of Changes, Chapter 2).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignments:**
- The approval authority for waiver requests on non-tiered items involves AFPC/DPPSP "with concurrence of AF/A1P (or AF/REP and NGB/A1P as applicable)," but the instruction does not clearly define what happens when concurrence is not obtained or how disagreements between these offices are resolved.
- The phrase "the documentation and/or approval authority required will vary depending on the nature of the circumstances" (extracted authority) is vague and does not specify which circumstances trigger which authority level.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed in extracted text:**
- The instruction's title and table of contents reference Chapters 5–10+ (302 pages total, only ~14,000 characters parsed), meaning significant content on enlisted evaluation details (Chapter 4 continuation), LOEs, MLR procedures, appeals (Chapter 10), and potentially commander-directed evaluations is not visible in the extracted text.
- The updated feedback system referenced by the prescription of AF Form 717 is described in the Summary of Changes but the detailed procedural guidance (presumably in Chapter 2 and associated tables) is only partially visible.
- Tables 2.3–2.5 are marked "DELETED" with no explanation of where the content migrated or whether it was superseded.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- The dual-track exception authority for evaluation changes near central selection boards (AFPC MLR for active duty, ARPC/PB for reserves) uses identical "extreme circumstances, case-by-case" language without defining what constitutes "extreme circumstances," creating risk of inconsistent application across components.
- The authorization for multiple general officers to sign evaluations (Summary of Changes) may create ambiguity about which signature carries precedence or serves as the final evaluator of record if not fully delineated in the unparsed sections.
