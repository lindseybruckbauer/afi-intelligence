# AFI 36-2406: Officer and Enlisted Evaluation Systems

**OPR:** AF/A1PPP | **Effective:** 22 August 2025 (Incorporating Change 1, 27 May 2026) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-24, Military Evaluations | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2406, 6 August 2024

## Purpose & Scope

AFI 36-2406 provides guidance and procedures for implementing the United States Air Force Officer and Enlisted Evaluation Systems, including how to prepare, submit, and manage evaluation forms. It applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard; it does **not** apply to the United States Space Force (USSF). Compliance is mandatory. The instruction was developed collaboratively between AF/A1, AF/RE, and NGB/CF. Change 1 incorporates AFGM 2026-01, requiring documentation of physical fitness assessment scores on evaluations, prescribing AF Form 717 (Performance Objectives and Feedback Form), authorizing multiple general officers to sign evaluations, clarifying stratification quotas, and updating single evaluator requirements, among other changes.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority for the publication.
- **AF/A1PPP (Promotions and Evaluations Policy Branch):** Office of primary responsibility; must approve locally created versions of prescribed forms and field agency supplements.
- **AFPC/DPPSP (Promotions, Evaluations and Recognitions Policy Branch):** Approval authority for non-tiered waiver requests (with AF/A1P concurrence); approval authority for RegAF non-rated period of supervision course requests; authorized to make corrections to digitally signed evaluations after transmission; processes recommended changes via DAF Form 847.
- **ARPC/PB (Promotion Board Secretariat):** Receives published supplements; serves as approval authority for ARC member non-rated period considerations (via ARPC/DPTSE); may approve changes up to one duty day prior to central selection boards in extreme circumstances.
- **AF/A1PP (Military Force Policy Division):** Must approve MAJCOM-level or below supplements before publication.
- **AFPC MLR Office:** Approves exceptions to maintain integrity of the Officer Evaluation System; may approve changes up to one duty day prior to central selection boards in extreme circumstances.
- **CSAF:** Sole authority to approve exceptions to general officer management-level evaluation policies; HAF Deputy Chief of Staff, Personnel, may exercise similar authority for general officer management cases.
- **Wing Commander (or equivalent, delegable to deputy):** Final approval/disapproval authority when a lower approval authority recommends disapproval of a request (§1.4.11.1.2).
- **Raters / Higher Level Reviewers (HLRs):** Responsible for preparing, reviewing, and signing evaluations per rating chain requirements; authorized to use stratification statements from intermediate officials between rater and HLR (§3.15.6.7.2).
- **Referral Reviewer:** May grant extensions for ratee rebuttals (e.g., when Area Defense Counsel is unavailable or rebuttal exceeds 10 pages).
- **AFELM/CC (Joint agencies, on G-series orders):** Authorized to sign ALQ evaluations in lieu of the forced distributor when the forced distributor signs the MEL (§4.12.5.4).
- **Evaluation Review Appeals Board (ERAB):** Authorized to modify evaluations that differ from an applicant's request, including directing corrections rather than voiding reports (§10.2.4).
- **Ratees:** May grant access to completed feedback forms at their discretion (§2.9.3); responsible for acknowledging evaluations (§3.18).
- **Field Agencies (MAJCOMs, NAFs, Wings, FOAs):** Shall not publish supplements that change basic policies/procedures or duplicate instruction text; supplements require AF/A1PP and AFPC/DPPSP approval.

## Key Requirements

1. **Mandatory compliance** — All evaluation actions must adhere to tiered compliance requirements (T-0 through T-3); waiver requests must be routed through the chain of command to the appropriate tier authority (Preamble).
2. **Physical fitness scores** must be documented on officer and enlisted evaluations per AFGM 2026-01 (Summary of Changes).
3. **Performance feedback sessions** must be conducted and documented per Chapter 2 timelines (Tables 2.1, 2.2); failure to conduct or document feedback is addressed at §2.10.
4. **Evaluator qualifications** must meet requirements at §1.5; single evaluator authorizations have specific criteria for enlisted members and deployed commander LOEs (Summary of Changes; §4.12).
5. **Referral evaluations** must follow procedures at §1.11, including providing the ratee opportunity for rebuttal; referral memorandum format is prescribed (Figures 1.1–1.2).
6. **Mandatory comments** are required for specific circumstances including criminal convictions (§1.9, §1.8.1.1.1.3.1); waiver of mandatory criminal-conviction comments requires a written finding that inclusion is not in the best interests of the Air Force and would unduly harm the ratee.
7. **Stratifications** must conform to authorized peer groups (Table 3.4) and updated officer stratification quotas (§3.15); joint and DAF SNCO stratifications follow updated guidance (Summary of Changes).
8. **Performance objectives** must align with major performance areas, and competencies must align with Airman Leadership Qualities (ALQs), documented via AF Form 717 (Summary of Changes; Chapter 2).
9. **Evaluation close-out dates** and "FROM"/"THRU" dates must comply with static close-out date tables (Tables 3.2, 3.3) and enlisted-specific rules (§§4.6–4.7); extensions require approval per §3.17.
10. **Records management** — All records generated must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (Preamble).
11. **Privacy Act compliance** — Collection and maintenance of evaluation data is authorized by DoDI 5400.11 under applicable SORNs (F036 AF PC A and F036 AFPC T) (Preamble).
12. **Post-transmission corrections** — Once a digitally signed evaluation is transmitted to AFPC or ARPC, only AFPC or ARPC is authorized to make corrections (Table/Rule, Minor Errors).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignments:**
- The preamble states non-tiered waiver approval rests with AFPC/DPPSP "with concurrence of AF/A1P (or AF/REP and NGB/A1P as applicable)," but the instruction does not clearly delineate which specific non-tiered items fall under Reserve or Guard concurrence versus AF/A1P concurrence, potentially causing routing confusion.
- The statement "the documentation and/or approval authority required will vary depending on the nature of the circumstances" (authority extract) is vague and does not specify criteria for determining which authority applies.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed in extracted text:**
- Chapter 5 and beyond (Chapters 6–10+) are referenced in the table of contents structure but their substantive text is not included in the extract; topics such as senior enlisted/officer-specific evaluation nuances, appeals processes (Chapter 10 is only partially visible), and wartime provisions (§1.15) cannot be assessed for completeness.
- The updated feedback system (AF Form 717) is prescribed but detailed procedural guidance for its use is only partially visible in Chapter 2; Tables 2.3–2.5 are marked "DELETED" with no replacement content visible.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- The CSAF is identified as the sole exception authority for general officer evaluations, yet the HAF Deputy Chief of Staff, Personnel "may exercise similar authority" — the boundary between these two authorities is not precisely defined, which could create ambiguity in practice.
- The ERAB's authority to modify evaluations beyond the applicant's request (§10.2.4) may create tension with due-process expectations if the scope of permissible modifications is not further bounded elsewhere in the full text.
