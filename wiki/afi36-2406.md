# AFI 36-2406: Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems

**OPR:** AF/A1PPP | **Effective:** 22 August 2025 (Incorporating Change 1, 27 May 2026) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-24, Military Evaluations | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2406, 6 August 2024

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction provides guidance and procedures for implementing the USAF Officer and Enlisted Evaluations Systems, including how to prepare, submit, and manage evaluation forms. It covers the performance feedback process, officer ALQ evaluations (including Officer Performance Briefs), enlisted ALQ evaluations (including Enlisted Performance Briefs), stratifications, referral evaluations, and the evaluation appeals process. The publication applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard; it does **not** apply to the United States Space Force (USSF). Compliance is mandatory (§1.1, opening paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority for the publication.
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Co-developed the instruction; AF/A1PP and AF/A1PPP serve as the policy offices for evaluations and must approve supplements and locally created forms.
- **AF/A1PPP (Promotions and Evaluations Policy Branch):** Office of primary responsibility; must concur on field-level supplements, locally created forms, and policy deviations.
- **AFPC/DPPSP (Promotions, Evaluations and Recognitions Policy Branch):** Approval authority for non-tiered waiver requests (with AF/A1P concurrence); approval authority for RegAF non-rated period supervision requests; receives recommended changes via DAF Form 847; sole authority (along with ARPC) to correct digitally signed evaluations after transmission (§1.13, Table corrections rule).
- **ARPC/PB (Air Reserve Personnel Center Promotion Board Secretariat):** Receives published supplements; may approve changes to evaluation records up to one duty day prior to central selection boards in extreme circumstances.
- **ARPC/DPTSE:** Approval authority for ARC members requesting non-rated periods of supervision consideration (§1.4.11.3).
- **AFPC MLR Office:** Approves exceptions to maintain integrity in the Officer Evaluation System and ensure fair consideration; may approve changes up to one duty day prior to central selection boards in extreme circumstances.
- **CSAF:** Sole authority to approve exceptions to certain evaluation policies; HAF Deputy Chief of Staff, Personnel, may exercise similar authority for general officer management levels.
- **Wing Commander (or equivalent, delegable to deputy):** Final approval/disapproval authority when a lower-level approval authority recommends disapproval of a request (§1.4.11.1.2).
- **AF/RE (Chief of Air Force Reserve) and NGB/CF (Director of Air National Guard):** Co-developed the instruction; AF/REP and NGB/A1P provide concurrence on non-tiered waivers for their respective components.
- **Raters, Additional Raters, and Higher Level Reviewers (HLRs):** Prepare, endorse, and review evaluations per rating chain requirements; HLRs provide stratification and oversight (§1.5, §1.6, §3.14, §4.12–4.13).
- **Referral Reviewer:** May grant extensions when the ratee needs additional time for rebuttal (e.g., non-availability of Area Defense Counsel or rebuttal exceeding 10 pages) (§1.11).
- **AFELM/CC (on G-series orders):** Authorized to sign ALQ evaluations in lieu of the forced distributor in joint agencies when the forced distributor signs the MEL (§4.12.5.4).
- **ERAB (Evaluation Review Appeals Board):** Authorized to modify evaluations that differ from the applicant's request, including directing corrections in lieu of voiding reports (§10.2.4).
- **Ratee:** May grant access to completed performance feedback forms at their discretion (§2.9.3); must acknowledge evaluations (§3.18).

## Key Requirements

1. **Mandatory compliance:** All evaluations must comply with this AFI; tier waiver designations (T-0 through T-3) identify the appropriate waiver approval authority. Waiver requests must be submitted through the chain of command (opening paragraph).
2. **Supplements restriction:** Field agencies (MAJCOM, NAF, Wing, FOA) will not publish supplements that change basic policies/procedures or duplicate text; MAJCOM-level and below supplements require AF/A1PP and AFPC/DPPSP approval before publication.
3. **Physical fitness documentation:** Physical fitness assessment scores must be documented on officer and enlisted evaluations per AFGM 2026-01 (Summary of Changes).
4. **Performance feedback:** Documented performance feedback sessions are required per the timelines in Table 2.1; performance objectives must align with major performance areas and competencies with Airman Leadership Qualities (ALQs) (Ch. 2, Summary of Changes).
5. **Referral evaluations:** Evaluations containing adverse or derogatory information must follow the referral process, including providing the ratee opportunity for written rebuttal (§1.11; Figures 1.1–1.2).
6. **Stratification rules:** Officer stratification quotas apply as clarified in the change; authorized stratification peer groups are defined in Table 3.4. Joint and DAF SNCO stratification guidance was updated (§3.15, Summary of Changes).
7. **Evaluator requirements:** Evaluators must meet supervisory day thresholds (§1.5, §3.12, §4.8); single evaluator requirements were updated for enlisted members and deployed commander LOEs (Summary of Changes).
8. **Prohibited considerations and comments:** Evaluators must not include certain categories of information as specified in §1.12 and §3.16.
9. **Mandatory comments:** Specific mandatory comments (e.g., for criminal convictions) are required; a waiver requires a written finding by the approval authority that such comments are not in the best interests of the Air Force and would unduly harm the ratee (§1.8.1.1.1.3.1, §1.9).
10. **Records management:** All records generated must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule.
11. **Privacy Act compliance:** Collection/maintenance of evaluation information is authorized by DoDI 5400.11; applicable SORNs (F036 AF PC A; F036 AFPC T) must be observed.
12. **Post-transmission corrections:** Once a digitally signed evaluation has been transmitted to AFPC or ARPC, only AFPC or ARPC is authorized to make corrections.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignment:**
- The phrase "the documentation and/or approval authority required will vary depending on the nature of the circumstances" (§1.4 area) provides no specificity on which circumstances trigger which authority level, creating potential inconsistency in application.
- For non-tiered items, AFPC/DPPSP is the approval authority "with concurrence" of AF/A1P (or AF/REP/NGB/A1P "as applicable")—the conditions under which Reserve or Guard concurrence is required versus AF/A1P concurrence are not explicitly delineated in the extracted text.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed in extracted text:**
- The title encompasses the full evaluation system, but the extracted text does not include substantive content from Chapters 5–9 (e.g., promotion-related evaluations, specific enlisted EPB procedures beyond Chapter 4 headings, or detailed appeal procedures beyond the ERAB authority statement in §10.2.4).
- The updated feedback system and AF Form 717 are prescribed in the Summary of Changes, but the detailed guidance on use (presumably in Chapter 2) is only partially visible.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- Multiple general officers are now authorized to sign evaluations (Summary of Changes), but the extracted text does not clarify how this interacts with existing single-evaluator or rating chain deviation rules (§1.7), which could create ambiguity in practice.
- The CSAF is the sole exception authority for certain policies, yet the HAF Deputy Chief of Staff, Personnel, "may exercise similar authority" for general officer cases—the boundary between these authorities is not precisely defined, risking overlapping jurisdiction.
