# AFI 36-2122: Interservice Officers on Exchange Duty with United States Air Force

**OPR:** AFPC/DPM | **Effective:** 30 June 2025 | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-21 | **Supersedes:** DAFI 36-2122, 3 August 2018

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction governs the Interservice Officer Exchange Program, which enables officers from other Military Services to serve on exchange duty with the United States Air Force and vice versa. The program's primary objective is to provide participating officers hands-on experience and understanding of the mission, policies, and procedures of other Military Services in areas of shared interest and mutual cooperation (§2.1). It applies to officers in the Regular Air Force and does not apply to members of the United States Space Force, Air National Guard, or Air Force Reserve. It is not applicable to officers serving in Joint Staff, joint activities, or liaison roles (§2.1).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR:** Approves new and changed guidance pertaining to this publication (§1.1).
- **AF/A1P:** Oversees the Interservice Officer Exchange Program as primary POC; provides guidance to participating agencies; maintains key contacts; collaborates with exchange representatives from other Services; supports functional managers in developing MOAs (§1.2).
- **AF/A1M:** Establishes manpower accounts for exchange program administrators when joint offsets are identified or the program is approved by AF/A1P (§1.3).
- **AFPC/DPMO:** Initiates assignment processes and selects officers to fill exchange requirements; coordinates with AFDW Manpower for PASCODEs; obtains position descriptions from functional managers (§1.4).
- **AFDW/A1:** Retrieves necessary documents and assists functional managers; manages Officer Evaluation System actions; provides MAJCOM-level administrative support for PME nominations, awards/decorations, assignment entitlements, separation, and general courts martial actions; updates PASCODEs after validating senior rater identification codes (§1.5).
- **AFDW/A1M:** Reviews, evaluates, and coordinates or recommends revisions to program documents; implements unit manpower document changes; establishes PASCODEs for interservice exchange officers (§1.6).
- **AFDW/FM:** Establishes financial accounts; determines funding requirements including TDY and professional gear; coordinates funding transfers between MAJCOMs (§1.7).
- **AFDW/XP:** Reviews skills, experiences, and perspectives of interservice officers to strategically leverage expertise in planning, readiness, and emergency management (§1.8).
- **AFDW/A1MP:** Facilitates management of manpower inputs for the Strategy, Planning, Programming, Budgeting, and Execution process (§1.9).
- **HQ Air Force Functional Managers:** Coordinate with Service counterparts to establish exchange programs ensuring no increase in end strength or JDAL; prepare MOAs and position descriptions; submit requisitions; revalidate position descriptions; notify agencies of program cancellation (§1.10).

## Key Requirements

1. USAF officers must be identified to offset exchange positions to ensure no increase in end strength or Joint Duty Assignment List (T-0) (§1.10.1).
2. New program proposals must be submitted to the respective MAJCOM Commander with comprehensive justification (T-1) (§2.2.1).
3. New position proposals in existing programs must include a proposed station report (Attachment 2) and detailed position description (Attachment 3) identifying the designated Interservice program POC (T-1) (§2.2.2).
4. Gaining units must use inbound officer information to arrange for arrival and integration (T-2) (§2.3).
5. Interservice exchange officers are reported as attached officers, not assigned; strength accountability remains with the parent Military Service (T-1, T-2) (§2.5).
6. Transfer of exchange officers to a different unit requires prior coordination and approval from AFPC/DPMO (T-1) (§2.6).
7. Standard tour length is 36 months (including travel/training), extendable up to 12 additional months with mutual consent (§2.8.1).
8. Tour length adjustment requests must be submitted in writing through the USAF operational commander (T-1), with wing commander recommendation forwarded to AFPC/DPMO and coordinated with the relevant Service (T-2) (§2.8.2).
9. Parent Military Service funds PCS travel to/from the Air Force duty station and PCS moves during tour (T-1); USAF funds TDY travel/per diem performed on behalf of USAF (T-1) (§2.9.2).
10. Exchange officers receive authority and supervisory responsibilities equivalent to Air Force officers of the same grade but are generally not assigned responsibilities reserved for Air Force officers (e.g., disciplinary actions, financial management) (§2.4).
11. All records must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule (opening paragraph).
12. MAJCOM-level supplements require Human Resource Management Strategic Board approval prior to certification (opening paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Authority for recall during hostilities is ambiguous.** Section 2.7 states recall "should be mutually agreed upon" but does not address what happens if the parent Service and host Service disagree, nor does it identify a final decision authority.
2. **Paragraph 1.10 cross-reference is incomplete.** Sections 1.5.1 and 1.6.1 reference "documents identified in paragraph 1.10" but §1.10 describes functional manager responsibilities broadly; the specific documents (MOAs, position descriptions) are only mentioned in §1.10.2, creating potential confusion about scope.
3. **Exchange officer discipline and accountability.** Section 2.4 excludes exchange officers from disciplinary authority but does not clearly address the process if an exchange officer requires disciplinary action — whether the host unit coordinates with the parent Service or AFDW/A1.
4. **Title implies coverage of USAF officers on exchange to other Services**, yet the extracted text focuses almost exclusively on inbound interservice officers attached to the Air Force; procedures for outbound USAF officers are not substantively addressed in the available text.
5. **Financial responsibility for TDY is partially cut off** (§2.9.2.3 truncated), leaving the full scope of USAF TDY funding obligations unclear.
6. **Waiver authority for non-tiered items** routes to AFPC/DPM, but no explicit delegation memo or threshold criteria are provided for distinguishing non-tiered items from tiered ones beyond the reference to DAFMAN 90-161 Table A10.1.
