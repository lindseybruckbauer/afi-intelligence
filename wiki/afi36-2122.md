# AFI 36-2122: Interservice Officers on Exchange Duty with United States Air Force

**OPR:** AFPC/DPM | **Effective:** 30 June 2025 | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-21 | **Supersedes:** DAFI 36-2122, 3 August 2018

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction governs the Interservice Officer Exchange Program, which enables officers from other Military Services to serve on exchange duty with the United States Air Force—and vice versa—to gain hands-on experience and understanding of other Services' missions, policies, and procedures in areas of shared interest and mutual support (§2.1). It applies to officers in the Regular Air Force and does not apply to members of the United States Space Force, Air National Guard, or Air Force Reserve. It is not applicable to officers serving in Joint Staff, joint activities, or liaison roles as representatives of their parent Military Service (§2.1).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority; approves new and changed guidance pertaining to this publication (§1.1).
- **AF/A1P (Director, Military Force Management Policy):** Oversees the Air Force Interservice Officer Exchange Program; serves as primary POC; maintains key contacts; collaborates with other Services' exchange representatives; supports functional managers in developing memorandums of agreement (§1.2).
- **AF/A1M (Director, Manpower, Organization and Resources):** Establishes manpower accounts for exchange program administrators when joint offsets are identified or the program is approved by AF/A1P (§1.3).
- **AFPC/DPMO (Operations and Special Duty Career Management):** Initiates assignment processes and selects officers; coordinates with AFDW Manpower for PASCODEs; obtains position descriptions from functional managers (§1.4).
- **AFDW/A1 (Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Retrieves required documents; manages Officer Evaluation System actions; provides MAJCOM-level administrative support including PME nominations, awards/decorations, assignment entitlements, separations, and general courts-martial actions; updates PASCODEs (§1.5).
- **AFDW/A1M (Manpower and Resources):** Reviews and coordinates documents; implements unit manpower document changes; establishes PASCODEs for interservice exchange officers (§1.6).
- **AFDW/FM (Financial Analysis):** Establishes financial accounts; determines funding requirements including TDY costs and professional gear; coordinates inter-MAJCOM funding transfers (§1.7).
- **AFDW/XP (Plans and Readiness):** Reviews interservice officers' skills and expertise for strategic leverage in planning, readiness, and emergency management (§1.8).
- **AFDW/A1MP (Programs Development Division):** Facilitates management of manpower inputs for the SPPBE process (§1.9).
- **HQ Air Force Functional Managers:** Coordinate with Service counterparts to establish exchange programs; prepare memorandums of agreement and position descriptions; submit requisitions for replacement officers; revalidate position descriptions at each requisition cycle; notify participating agencies of program cancellations (§1.10).
- **Gaining Unit:** Utilizes inbound officer information to arrange for smooth arrival and integration (§2.3).
- **Wing Commander:** Reviews tour length adjustment requests, provides recommendation, and forwards to AFPC/DPMO (§2.8.2).

## Key Requirements

1. Exchange programs must not result in an increase in USAF end strength or Joint Duty Assignment List; USAF offset officers must be identified in every proposal. (T-0) (§1.10.1)
2. New program proposals must be submitted to the respective MAJCOM Commander with comprehensive justification and detailed explanation of the proposed offset position use. (T-1) (§2.2.1)
3. New position proposals within existing programs must include a proposed station report (Attachment 2) and detailed position description (Attachment 3) identifying the designated Interservice program POC. (T-1) (§2.2.2)
4. Gaining units must use inbound officer notification information to make necessary arrival arrangements. (T-2) (§2.3)
5. Interservice exchange officers are reported as attached—not assigned—to Air Force units; strength accountability remains with the parent Military Service. (T-2/T-1) (§2.5)
6. Transfer of exchange officers to different units requires prior coordination and approval from AFPC/DPMO. (T-1) (§2.6)
7. Standard tour length is 36 months (including travel/training time); extensions of up to 12 months require consent of both USAF and parent Service. Tour adjustment requests must be submitted in writing through the USAF operational commander. (T-1/T-2) (§2.8)
8. Pay and allowances are charged to the parent Military Service's appropriations. (§2.9.1)
9. Parent Military Service publishes orders and funds PCS travel to/from and during Air Force duty; USAF funds TDY travel/per diem performed on behalf of the USAF. (T-1) (§2.9.2)
10. All records must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule.
11. MAJCOM-level supplements require Human Resource Management Strategic Board approval prior to certification and approval.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Incomplete financial responsibilities:** The document text is truncated mid-sentence at §2.9.2.3 (TDY funding by USAF), leaving the full scope of financial responsibilities—and any subsequent sections—unexamined.
2. **Exchange officers' authority limitations unclear:** Section 2.4 states exchange officers generally are not assigned responsibilities "reserved for Air Force officers" (e.g., disciplinary actions, financial management) but uses the qualifier "as a general rule" without specifying the conditions under which exceptions may be granted or who approves them.
3. **Hostilities recall process:** Section 2.7 states recall during hostilities "should be mutually agreed upon" but does not specify a decision authority, timeline, or escalation process if agreement cannot be reached.
4. **AFDW/XP role vagueness:** Section 1.8 directs AFDW/XP to "strategically leverage" interservice officers' expertise but provides no specific deliverables, timelines, or coordination requirements.
5. **Waiver authority for non-tiered items:** The opening paragraph routes non-tiered waiver requests to AFPC/DPM for "final decision," but no criteria or process details are provided for adjudication.
6. **Title implies coverage of USAF officers sent to other Services:** The title and program description suggest bidirectionality, yet the visible text focuses almost exclusively on inbound exchange officers attached to the Air Force, with limited procedural guidance for outbound USAF officers beyond offset identification (§1.10.1).
