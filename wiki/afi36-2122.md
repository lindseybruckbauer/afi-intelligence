# AFI 36-2122: Interservice Officers on Exchange Duty with United States Air Force

**OPR:** AFPC/DPM | **Effective:** 30 June 2025 | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Military Personnel | **Supersedes:** DAFI 36-2122, 3 August 2018

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction governs the administration of the Interservice Officer Exchange Program, under which officers from other U.S. Military Services are attached to the Air Force for exchange duty. It applies to officers in the Regular Air Force and does not apply to members of the United States Space Force, Air National Guard, or Air Force Reserve. The program's primary objective is to enable participating officers to gain hands-on experience and understanding of other Military Services' missions, policies, and procedures in areas of shared interest (§2.1). Compliance is mandatory. This instruction is not applicable to officers serving in Joint Staff, joint activities, or liaison roles (§2.1).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority; approves new and changed guidance pertaining to this publication (§1.1).
- **AF/A1P (Director of Air Force Military Force Management Policy):** Oversees the Air Force Interservice Officer Exchange Program as primary point of contact; provides support and guidance to participating agencies; maintains key contacts list; exercises general administrative oversight; collaborates with other Service exchange representatives; supports functional managers in developing memorandums of agreement (§1.2).
- **AF/A1M (Director of Manpower, Organization and Resources):** Establishes manpower accounts for exchange program administrators when joint offsets are identified or the program is approved by AF/A1P (§1.3).
- **AFPC/DPMO (Operations and Special Duty Career Management):** Initiates assignment processes and selects officers; coordinates with AFDW Manpower for PASCODEs; obtains position descriptions from functional managers (§1.4).
- **AFDW/A1 (Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Retrieves required documents; establishes and manages Officer Evaluation System actions; provides administrative support including PME nominations, awards/decorations processing, assignment entitlements, separation, and courts martial actions; updates PASCODEs after validating senior rater identification codes (§1.5).
- **AFDW/A1M (Manpower and Resources):** Reviews and coordinates or recommends revisions to program documents; implements changes in unit manpower documents; establishes PASCODEs for interservice exchange officers (§1.6).
- **AFDW/FM (Financial Analysis):** Establishes financial accounts for exchange programs; determines funding requirements including TDY costs and professional gear; coordinates inter-MAJCOM funding transfers (§1.7).
- **AFDW/XP (Plans and Readiness):** Reviews skills, experiences, and perspective of interservice officers to strategically leverage their expertise in planning, readiness, and emergency management (§1.8).
- **AFDW/A1MP (Programs Development Division):** Facilitates management of manpower inputs for the Strategy, Planning, Programing, Budgeting, and Execution process (§1.9).
- **Headquarters Air Force Functional Managers:** Coordinate with other Service counterparts to establish exchange programs; prepare memorandums of agreement and position descriptions; submit requisitions for replacement officers; revalidate position descriptions at each requisition cycle; notify participating agencies of program cancellations (§1.10).

## Key Requirements

1. No increase in USAF end strength or Joint Duty Assignment List is permissible when establishing an exchange program; USAF officers must be identified as offsets in the program proposal (§1.10.1). **(T-0)**
2. New program proposals must be submitted to the respective MAJCOM Commander with comprehensive justification and explanation of USAF officer use in the offset position (§2.2.1). **(T-1)**
3. New position proposals in existing programs must include a proposed station report (Attachment 2) and detailed position description (Attachment 3) identifying the designated Interservice program point of contact (§2.2.2). **(T-1)**
4. Gaining units must utilize inbound officer information from AFPC/DPMO to make necessary arrival arrangements (§2.3). **(T-2)**
5. Interservice exchange officers shall not be assigned to Air Force units for duty but reported as attached officers; strength accountability remains with the parent Military Service (§2.5). **(T-2, T-1)**
6. Transfer of exchange officers to a different unit requires prior coordination and approval from AFPC/DPMO (§2.6). **(T-1)**
7. Standard tour length is 36 months (including travel and training time); extensions up to 12 additional months require consent of both USAF and the parent Military Service (§2.8.1).
8. Tour length adjustment requests must be submitted in writing through the USAF operational commander and wing commander to AFPC/DPMO, with parent Military Service approval obtained before requesting a replacement (§2.8.2). **(T-1, T-2)**
9. Pay and allowances are charged to the parent Military Service's appropriations; parent Service funds PCS travel to/from and during the Air Force tour; USAF funds TDY travel/per diem performed on USAF behalf (§2.9). **(T-1)**
10. Exchange officers are generally not assigned responsibilities reserved for Air Force officers (e.g., disciplinary actions, financial management) as mandated by AF directives or public law (§2.4).
11. All records generated must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed of per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (opening paragraph).
12. MAJCOM-level supplements require Human Resource Management Strategic Board approval prior to certification (opening paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignments:**
- The role of AFDW/XP (§1.8) is described in vague terms ("strategically leverage their expertise") without specifying concrete decision authority or actionable responsibilities, making it unclear how this office interfaces with the program operationally.
- Paragraph 1.5.1 references "necessary documents identified in paragraph 1.10" but §1.10 describes functional manager responsibilities rather than enumerating specific documents, creating a circular reference.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed:**
- The title references "exchange duty with United States Air Force" but the extracted text does not address evaluation/rating procedures for attached exchange officers in detail (§1.5.2 only mentions establishing OES actions, not the substantive rating criteria or process).
- The instruction does not address security clearance requirements or access limitations for interservice exchange officers.
- Procedures for use of exchange officers during hostilities (§2.7) lack specificity—no timeline, authority level for recall decisions, or dispute resolution mechanism is provided.
- No guidance is provided on training requirements or orientation processes for inbound exchange officers beyond the gaining unit making "necessary arrangements" (§2.3).

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- The instruction states it does not apply to Space Force members, yet DAFPD 36-21 (the implementing directive) may govern both Air Force and Space Force personnel classification, potentially creating a policy gap for USSF interservice exchanges.
