# DAFI 31-118: Security Forces Standards and Procedures

**OPR:** AF/A4S (Directorate of Security Forces) | **Effective:** 15 January 2026 | **Implements:** DAFI 31-118 (via DAFGM 2026-01) | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This DAF Guidance Memorandum (DAFGM 2026-01) immediately implements changes to DAFI 31-118, which governs Security Forces (SF) standards and procedures across the Department of the Air Force. It applies to all DAF civilian employees, uniformed members of the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, Air National Guard, United States Space Force, and those with contractual obligations to abide by DAF publications. The memorandum principally accomplishes three things: (1) designates DAFI 31-122 as the authoritative source for all GS-0083 Police Series-specific guidance, rendering corresponding DAFI 31-118 content obsolete; (2) corrects the JLLIS email address; and (3) comprehensively updates Security Forces Enterprise Governance (SFEG) processes, structure, and procedures (§3.3 et seq.). The memorandum expires one year from issuance or upon publication of an interim change/rewrite of DAFI 31-118, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretary of the Air Force:** Orders publication and compliance with this DAFGM; overarching authority for the instruction.
- **AF/A4 (DCS Logistics, Engineering, & Force Protection):** Issuing authority for this DAFGM; signed by Lt Gen Kenyon K. Bell (§Memorandum header).
- **AF/A4S (Director, Security Forces):** Designated SFEG Lead and Secretariat (§3.3.4.1.2); chairs the SFEB (§3.3.5.2.2) and SFILT (§3.3.5.4.1); retains final vector and veto authority on SFEB decisions (§3.3.5.2.2.3); retains authority over permanent and non-permanent voting membership of the SFEB (§3.3.5.2.2.2) and SFILT (§3.3.5.4.1.2).
- **A4 Enterprise Council (EC):** GO/SES-level governing body that coordinates and aligns actions across the AF Logistics, Engineering, and Force Protection Enterprise; sets direction and ensures the enterprise is organized, trained, and equipped (§3.3.5.1–3.3.5.1.2).
- **A4 Integration Leadership Team (ILT):** Coordinates across governance structure to ensure EC guidance, decisions, and initiatives are executed and information flows between tiers (§3.3.5.1.2).
- **Security Forces Executive Board (SFEB):** O-6/CMSgt-level body; convenes twice per year (minimum one in person); ratifies SF Enterprise Plans; approves/validates SF capability gaps from the RWG; approves training solutions from the SFTWG; provides annual career field health check (§3.3.5.2.1–3.3.5.2.1.5).
- **SFEB Secretariat:** Single POC for all SFEB issues; establishes agenda; coordinates briefings; maintains action items; tracks/monitors initiatives; produces and distributes meeting minutes within 45 calendar days (§3.3.5.2.3–3.3.5.2.3.6).
- **SFEB Voting Members:** MAJCOM SF Directors/equivalents and SFM Chief Master Sergeants from AFMC, ACC, AETC, AFCENT, AFDW, AFGSC, AMC, AFRC, AFSFC, AFSOC, NGB, PACAF, and USAFE; identify links/dependencies and ensure SF Enterprise priorities are recognized by respective leadership chains (§3.3.5.2.4).
- **SFEB Non-Voting Members:** USSF, AFIMSC/IZP, AFLCMC HBU, and USAFA; participation as advisors subject to SFEB Chair approval (§3.3.5.2.5).
- **IBDSS Tri-Chair Board:** PEO (Digital)/AFLCMC, AF/A4S, and Commander AFIMSC jointly provide direction for IBDSS program of record management, funding, configuration management, and implementation (§3.3.5.3).
- **Security Forces Integrated Leadership Team (SFILT):** Convenes monthly via teleconference; provides inputs to SFEB on strategy; refines top-level metrics; fosters new concepts and projects; ensures implementation of strategic priorities (§3.3.5.4–3.3.5.4.2.4).
- **HQ AF/A4S/CFM, Security (Law Enforcement):** Approval authority for AFCS GS-0083 matters; processes waivers where waiver authority is not otherwise defined (Authority Statements).
- **Security Forces (SF) generally:** Authority to enforce federal law aspects not covered by the UCMJ (§3.1.2.1).
- **Working Group Chairs (SB, SWG, SFTWG, EWG):** Each retains authority over permanent voting membership changes within their respective groups (Authority Statements).

## Key Requirements

1. **Compliance is mandatory.** Where this DAFGM is inconsistent with other DAF publications, it prevails per DAFI 90-160 (§Memorandum para 1).
2. **GS-0083 Police Series guidance transition:** All GS-0083 specific guidance is now governed exclusively by DAFI 31-122; corresponding DAFI 31-118 provisions are obsolete (§Memorandum para 2).
3. **SFEG governance structure:** Organizations must adhere to the multi-tiered SFEG hierarchy: A4 EC → SFEB → SFILT → Standing Working Groups (§3.3.2, §3.3.5.1.2).
4. **SFEB convening requirement:** The SFEB must convene at least twice per year, with a minimum of one in-person meeting (§3.3.5.2.1.1).
5. **SFILT convening requirement:** The SFILT must convene monthly via teleconference (§3.3.5.4).
6. **SFEG charter review:** SFEG guidance and charters must be reviewed annually and updated as necessary (§3.3.4.2.4).
7. **Meeting minutes production:** Draft SFEB minutes must be provided to representatives for comment within 15 working days; approved minutes published and distributed within 45 calendar days (§3.3.5.2.3.6).
8. **Capability gap validation:** SF capability gaps must be identified through the Requirements Working Group and approved/validated by the SFEB (§3.3.5.2.1.3).
9. **Training solutions approval:** SF training solutions identified through the SFTWG require SFEB approval (§3.3.5.2.1.5).
10. **Waiver processing:** Waiver requests for mandatory training must follow Tables 6.1/6.2 procedures, starting with the individual or immediate supervisor; waivers without defined authority are processed through HQ AF/A4S/CFM (Authority Statements).
11. **Civilian Defender PAT retesting:** Civilian Defenders are authorized to take the PAT twice; the second attempt must occur within 90 days of the original test date (Authority Statements).
12. **Disputed SFILT votes:** Any SFILT vote without full voting membership agreement must be elevated to the SFEB for arbitration by the A4S (§3.3.5.4.1.4).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Incomplete section coverage:** The extracted text covers primarily Chapter 3 (SFEG governance). The full 147-page, 87-section document presumably addresses SF operational standards, law enforcement procedures, use of force, and installation security, but these substantive areas are not reflected in the available text, making it impossible to assess compliance obligations beyond governance.
2. **Working group charters referenced but not provided:** Multiple working groups (RWG, SFTWG, EWG, SWG) are mentioned with chairs retaining voting membership authority, but their specific charters, compositions, and operating procedures are not included in the extracted text.
3. **IBDSS Tri-Chair relationship to SFEG unclear:** The IBDSS Tri-Chair is described as a "stand-alone Board" (§3.3.5.3) yet is placed within the SFEG section hierarchy, creating ambiguity about its reporting relationship to the A4 EC and whether SFEG processes (e.g., charter review, minutes requirements) apply to it.
4. **SFILT Chair dual role:** AF/A4S chairs both the SFEB and SFILT (§3.3.5.2.2, §3.3.5.4.1), and disputed SFILT votes are elevated to the SFEB "for arbitration from the A4S" (§3.3.5.4.1.4)—creating a potential conflict where the same individual arbitrates disputes they may have chaired.
5. **Deleted sections without replacement:** Sections 3.3.5.1.2.1–3.3.5.1.2.2 are marked as deleted with no replacement text identified, leaving potential gaps in prior governance structure guidance.
6. **Waiver authority tables:** Tables 6.1 and 6.2 are referenced for waiver approval authority and procedures but are not included in the extracted text, preventing verification of the waiver chain.
