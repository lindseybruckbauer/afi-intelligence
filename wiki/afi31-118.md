# DAFI 31-118: Security Forces Standards and Procedures

**OPR:** AF/A4S (Directorate of Security Forces) | **Effective:** 15 January 2026 | **Implements:** DAFI 31-118 (via DAFGM 2026-01) | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This Department of the Air Force Guidance Memorandum (DAFGM 2026-01) immediately implements changes to DAFI 31-118, Security Forces Standards and Procedures. It applies to all DAF civilian employees, uniformed members of the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, Air National Guard (ANG), United States Space Force (USSF), and those with contractual obligations to abide by DAF publications. The memorandum redesignates DAFI 31-122 as the authoritative source for all GS-0083 Police Series-specific guidance (rendering DAFI 31-118's GS-0083 guidance obsolete), corrects the Joint Lessons Learned Information System (JLLIS) email address, and substantially updates the Security Forces Enterprise Governance (SFEG) processes and procedures (§3.3). The DAFGM becomes void one year from issuance or upon publication of an interim change or rewrite of DAFI 31-118, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretary of the Air Force:** Orders implementation of this DAFGM; ultimate authority over DAF publications.
- **AF/A4 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Logistics, Engineering, & Force Protection):** Issues the DAFGM; oversees the A4 Enterprise Council (EC), the GO/SES-level governing body aligning Logistics, Engineering, and Force Protection actions (§3.3.5.1).
- **AF/A4S (Director, Security Forces):** Designated SFEG Lead and Secretariat (§3.3.4.1.2); chairs the SF Executive Board (SFEB) and SF Integrated Leadership Team (SFILT) (§3.3.5.2.2, §3.3.5.4.1); retains veto authority on SFEB decisions and final vector on all SFEB decisions (§3.3.5.2.2.3); retains authority over permanent and non-permanent voting membership of SFEB and SFILT (§3.3.5.2.2.2, §3.3.5.4.1.2); administers the SFEG (§3.3.4.2.3).
- **A4 Enterprise Council (EC):** Sets direction for the A4 Enterprise; ensures it is organized, trained, and equipped to meet current and future force requirements; governs three Functional Executive Boards (Civil Engineer, Logistics, Security Forces) and the IT Portfolio Board (§3.3.5.1–§3.3.5.1.2).
- **A4 Integration Leadership Team (A4 ILT):** Coordinates across governance structure to ensure Council guidance, decisions, and initiatives are executed and information flows between levels (§3.3.5.1.2).
- **Security Forces Executive Board (SFEB):** O-6/CMSgt-level body; establishes SF strategy; creates unity of effort; makes decisions on organization, training, and equipping; ratifies SF Enterprise Plans; approves/validates SF capability gaps from RWG; approves SF training solutions from SFTWG; provides annual career field health check; convenes twice per year with at least one in-person meeting (§3.3.5.2–§3.3.5.2.1.5).
- **SFEB Secretariat:** Single point of contact for all SFEB issues; establishes and promulgates agendas; coordinates briefings; maintains action items; tracks/monitors/reports on initiatives; produces and distributes meeting minutes within 15 working days (draft) and 45 calendar days (approved) (§3.3.5.2.3.1–§3.3.5.2.3.6).
- **SFEB Voting Members:** MAJCOM SF Directors or civilian equivalents and SFM Chief Master Sergeants from AFMC, ACC, AETC, AFCENT, AFDW, AFGSC, AMC, AFRC, AFSFC, AFSOC, NGB, PACAF, and USAFE; identify links/dependencies and ensure SF Enterprise priorities are recognized by leadership chains (§3.3.5.2.4).
- **SFEB Non-Voting Members:** USSF, AFIMSC/IZP, AFLCMC HBU, and USAFA; additional participants approved by SFEB Chair (§3.3.5.2.5).
- **IBDSS Tri-Chair:** Standalone board comprising PEO (Digital)/AFLCMC, AF/A4S, and Commander AFIMSC; provides direction on IBDSS program of record management, funding, strategic priorities, configuration management, and implementation (§3.3.5.3).
- **Security Forces Integrated Leadership Team (SFILT):** Convenes monthly via teleconference; assesses SF strategy implementation; scopes initiatives; drives execution of action items; provides inputs to and assists the SFEB; fosters new concepts/projects for SFEB consideration; elevates unresolved votes to SFEB (§3.3.5.4–§3.3.5.4.2.4).
- **HQ AF/A4S/CFM, Security (Law Enforcement):** Approval authority for AFCS GS-0083 matters; processes waiver requests where waiver authority is not otherwise defined (§6 tables; authority statements).
- **Security Forces (SF):** Authority to enforce federal law not covered by the UCMJ (§3.1.2.1).
- **Standing Working Group (SWG), SF Training Working Group (SFTWG), Equipment Working Group (EWG), Requirements Working Group (RWG) Chairs:** Each retains authority over their respective permanent voting membership changes (authority statements).

## Key Requirements

1. **Compliance is mandatory.** Where this DAFGM is inconsistent with other DAF publications, its direction prevails per DAFI 90-160 (Memorandum, ¶1).
2. **GS-0083 Guidance Migration.** All GS-0083 Police Series-specific guidance must now be sourced from DAFI 31-122, Civilian Police Officer Program; DAFI 31-118 GS-0083 guidance is obsolete (Memorandum, ¶2).
3. **SFEG Governance Structure.** Organizations must adhere to the multi-tiered SFEG hierarchy: A4 EC → SFEB → SFILT → Standing Working Groups (§3.3.2, §3.3.5.1.2).
4. **SFEB Convening Requirement.** The SFEB must convene at least twice per year, with a minimum of one in-person meeting (§3.3.5.2.1.1).
5. **SFILT Monthly Teleconference.** The SFILT must convene monthly via teleconference to review metrics, assess strategy implementation, and drive action item execution (§3.3.5.4).
6. **SFEG Charter Annual Review.** SFEG guidance and charters must be reviewed annually and updated as necessary (§3.3.4.2.4).
7. **Meeting Minutes Timeline.** SFEB Secretariat must provide draft minutes within 15 working days and publish approved minutes within 45 calendar days of each meeting (§3.3.5.2.3.6).
8. **Capability Gap Validation.** SF capability gaps identified through the RWG must be approved and validated by the SFEB (§3.3.5.2.1.3).
9. **Training Solution Approval.** SF training solutions identified through the SFTWG require SFEB approval (§3.3.5.2.1.5).
10. **Waiver Processing.** Waiver requests for policies where authority is not otherwise defined must be processed and coordinated with HQ AF/A4S/CFM, Security (Law Enforcement); requests must originate from the individual or the individual's immediate supervisor (§6 tables; authority statements).
11. **Civilian Defender Physical Ability Test (PAT).** Civilian Defenders are authorized two attempts at the PAT; the second must be accomplished within 90 days of the original test date (authority statements).
12. **DAFGM Expiration.** This memorandum becomes void after one year (15 January 2027) or upon publication of an interim change or rewrite of DAFI 31-118, whichever is earlier.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear Authority Assignments:**
- The SFILT Chair (AF/A4S) must "elevate any votes without full voting membership agreement to the SFEB for arbitration from the A4S" (§3.3.5.4.1.4), yet the A4S also chairs the SFEB. This creates a situation where the same individual arbitrates disputes they could not resolve at the lower tier, raising questions about independent adjudication.
- Working Group Chairs (SWG, SFTWG, EWG, RWG) are referenced as retaining voting membership authority, but the document extract does not identify who appoints or designates these chairs or their reporting relationships within the SFEG hierarchy.

**(b) Topics Implied but Not Fully Addressed:**
- The title "Security Forces Standards and Procedures" implies comprehensive coverage of SF operational standards, use-of-force procedures, law enforcement protocols, and installation security measures. The extracted text is almost exclusively focused on governance structure (Chapter 3); operational standards and procedures content (beyond governance) is not evident in the parsed sections.
- The Civilian Defender PAT requirement is mentioned in authority statements but the specific standards, failure consequences, and accommodation procedures are not detailed in the extracted text.
