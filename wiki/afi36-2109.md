# AFI 36-2109: Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force, Senior Enlisted Leadership Management and Air Force Enlisted Council

**OPR:** AF/CCC | **Effective:** 7 February 2025 (Administrative Change); Base publication 5 January 2021, Incorporating Change 2, 1 February 2023 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Military Personnel | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2109, 14 July 2014

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction establishes the selection and approval authority for the Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force (CMSAF), Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) positions, and group senior enlisted leader (SEL) positions. It also provides guidance on the Air Force Senior Enlisted Leader Council (AFSELC), Air Force Career Field Manager Council (AFCFMC), Air Force Enlisted Council (AFEC), and Senior Enlisted Statesmen Council (SESC). The publication applies to the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard; it does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory, with tiered waiver authorities (T-0 through T-3) identified throughout; non-tiered waiver requests route through the chain of command to the CMSAF.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Chief of Staff, USAF (CSAF):** Selects a Chief Master Sergeant to serve as CMSAF and determines the CMSAF's tenure (§1.1.1).
- **CMSAF:** Serves as personal advisor to the SecAF and CSAF on enlisted welfare, readiness, morale, utilization, and professional development; travels to engage Airmen and families; testifies before Congress; consults with sister-service senior enlisted advisors; serves as waiver approval authority for non-tiered compliance items (§1.2, opening paragraph).
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Develops guidance and procedures for CMSAF, senior enlisted leadership management, and AFEC programs in collaboration with AF/RE and NGB/CF (opening paragraph).
- **AF/A1LE (Chiefs' Group):** Announces the assignment of the CMSAF position (§1.1.2).
- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority for this publication.
- **AF/RE (Chief of Air Force Reserve) & NGB/CF (Director of ANG):** Collaborate with AF/A1 in developing guidance and procedures applicable to their respective components.
- **CCMs (Command Chief Master Sergeants):** Serve at wing, NAF, MAJCOM, joint base, deployed, and other command levels with roles and responsibilities detailed by echelon (Chapters 2–3).
- **Group SELs:** Serve as senior enlisted leaders at group level with selection and role guidance per Chapters 4–5.
- **AFSELC / AFCFMC / AFEC / SESC Members:** Provide advisory input and execute council functions as outlined in Chapters 7–10.

## Key Requirements

1. The CSAF selects the CMSAF; the position carries distinctive rank insignia and the reporting identifier 9C000 (§1.1.1, §1.1.2.2).
2. The CMSAF is coded Distinguished Visitor Code 4 per DoD Order of Precedence as the senior enlisted advisor to the CSAF (§1.1.3). **(T-0)**
3. Former CMSAFs take precedence before all other retired members of the same grade per DoD Order of Precedence (§1.1.4). **(T-0)**
4. CCM selection, assignment, and tenure processes must follow procedures established for RegAF (§2.2–2.3), ANG (§2.4), and AFR (§2.5) components respectively.
5. CCM nominations to joint and combined commands follow the process in §2.6.
6. Group SEL selection follows the process established in §4.2, with ANG funding for Group SEL billets (9G100) per Table 4.1.
7. Housing designations for CCMs and Group SELs follow Key and Essential criteria (Chapter 6, §6.1).
8. The AFCFMC is formally established and the career field manager (CFM) selection process is codified (Chapter 8, Summary of Changes).
9. Outstanding Airmen of the Year (OAY) honorees are authorized to bring a spouse, family member, or one person of their choosing (§9.3 / authority statement).
10. All records generated under this publication must comply with AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule.
11. This publication may not be supplemented; waiver requests for tiered items go to the appropriate tier authority, and for non-tiered items to the CMSAF.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **CMSAF selection criteria undefined:** While §1.1.1 states the CSAF selects the CMSAF, no eligibility criteria, nomination process, or selection board procedures are described—leaving the process entirely discretionary.
2. **Tenure ambiguity for CMSAF:** Tenure is "determined by the CSAF" (§1.1.1) with no minimum or maximum term, unlike CCM tenure addressed in §2.7. This creates potential inconsistency in how tenure expectations are managed across senior enlisted positions.
3. **Space Force exclusion stated twice:** The opening paragraph redundantly states the publication does not apply to the USSF, yet no cross-reference is made to the USSF equivalent publication, leaving a gap for joint-service contexts involving both DAF components.
4. **Chapter content beyond extracted text:** Chapters 3–10 are referenced in the table of contents but substantive text was not fully available in the extract. Detailed roles for MAJCOM CCMs, NAF CCMs, Joint Base CCMs, Group SELs, and all four councils could not be fully assessed for internal consistency or completeness.
5. **AFCFMC marked as "(Added)":** The AFCFMC chapter (Chapter 8) is newly added via change action. Its integration with the existing AFSELC and AFEC structures—particularly regarding overlapping advisory roles—is not evident from the available text.
