# AFI 36-2109: Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force, Senior Enlisted Leadership Management and Air Force Enlisted Council

**OPR:** AF/CCC | **Effective:** 7 February 2025 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21 | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2109, 14 July 2014

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction establishes the selection and approval authority for the Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force (CMSAF), Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) positions, and Group Senior Enlisted Leader (SEL) positions. It also provides guidance on the Air Force Senior Enlisted Leader Council (AFSELC), Air Force Career Field Manager Council (AFCFMC), Air Force Enlisted Council (AFEC), and Senior Enlisted Statesmen Council (SESC). The publication applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR). It does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory, and the publication may not be supplemented.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Chief of Staff, USAF (CSAF):** Selects a CMSgt to serve as CMSAF and determines CMSAF tenure (§1.1.1).
- **CMSAF:** Serves as personal advisor to SecAF and CSAF on enlisted welfare, readiness, morale, utilization, and professional development; travels to engage Airmen and families; testifies before Congress; consults with sister-service senior enlisted advisors; serves as waiver authority for non-tiered compliance items (§1.2, Summary of Changes).
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services):** Develops guidance and procedures for CMSAF, senior enlisted leadership management, and enlisted council programs in collaboration with AF/RE and NGB/CF (Preamble).
- **AF/A1LE (Chiefs' Group):** Announces assignment of the CMSAF position (§1.1.2).
- **SAF/MR:** Certifies the publication (Cover page).
- **CCMs (Command Chief Master Sergeants):** Fulfill roles and responsibilities at wing, host wing, tenant, deployed, NAF, MAJCOM, joint, and joint base levels as detailed in Chapters 2–3 (§§2.1–3.13).
- **Group Senior Enlisted Leaders:** Serve at group level with selection processes and roles defined in Chapters 4–5 (§§4.1–5.2).
- **AFSELC, AFCFMC, AFEC, and SESC members:** Execute advisory and council responsibilities as established in Chapters 7–10.

## Key Requirements

1. The CMSAF position is afforded Distinguished Visitor Code 4 per DoD Order of Precedence as the senior enlisted advisor to the CSAF. (T-0) (§1.1.3)
2. Former CMSAFs take precedence before all other retired members of the same grade per DoD Order of Precedence. (T-0) (§1.1.4)
3. CCM selection, assignment, and tenure processes must follow chapter-specific procedures for RegAF, ANG, and AFR components (§§2.2–2.7).
4. Group SEL selection processes must comply with the procedures in Chapter 4, including ANG funding guidance (Table 4.1) (§4.2).
5. Housing designations for key and essential senior enlisted positions must follow guidance in Chapter 6 (§6.1).
6. Each Outstanding Airmen of the Year (OAY) honoree is authorized to bring their spouse, family member, or one person of their choosing (§9.3).
7. All records generated under this publication must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule (Preamble).
8. Waiver requests for tiered compliance items must be submitted through the chain of command to the appropriate tier waiver approval authority; non-tiered items route through the chain of command to the CMSAF (Preamble).
9. Recommended changes must be submitted via DAF Form 847 through the appropriate functional chain of command (Preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Incomplete CMSAF responsibilities:** Section 1.2.5 ("Engages with military leadership an…") is truncated in the available text, leaving the full scope of the CMSAF's engagement responsibilities unclear.
2. **USSF exclusion clarity:** The publication states twice it does not apply to the Space Force, but does not address how joint or combined command SEL positions (§2.6, §3.11) interact with Space Force senior enlisted equivalents despite the CMSAF consulting with the Chief Master Sergeant of the Space Force (§1.2.4).
3. **Group SEL vs. CCM authority delineation:** While separate chapters exist for CCM and Group SEL roles, the boundary of authority between a wing CCM and a group SEL under the same wing is not explicitly articulated in the extracted text.
4. **ANG/AFR-specific sections:** The preamble notes "selected sections of this publication apply to only specific components as indicated," but the component-specificity markers are not consistently visible in the extracted text, which could create compliance ambiguity for Reserve and Guard personnel.
