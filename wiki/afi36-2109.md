# AFI 36-2109: Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force, Senior Enlisted Leadership Management and Air Force Enlisted Council

**OPR:** AF/CCC | **Effective:** 7 February 2025 (Administrative Change to 5 January 2021 base, Incorporating Change 2, 1 February 2023) | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, *Utilization and Classification of Military Personnel* | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2109, 14 July 2014

## Purpose & Scope

This instruction establishes the selection and approval authority for the position of Chief Master Sergeant of the Air Force (CMSAF), Command Chief Master Sergeant (CCM) positions, and group senior enlisted leader (SEL) positions. It also provides guidance on the Air Force Senior Enlisted Leader Council (AFSELC), Air Force Career Field Manager Council (AFCFMC), Air Force Enlisted Council (AFEC), and Senior Enlisted Statesmen Council (SESC). The publication applies to the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Force Reserve (AFR). It does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Compliance is mandatory, and the publication may not be supplemented (§ Preamble).

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Chief of Staff, USAF (CSAF):** Selects a CMSgt to serve as CMSAF; determines CMSAF tenure (§ 1.1.1).
- **CMSAF:** Serves as personal advisor to the SecAF and CSAF on welfare, readiness, morale, utilization, and professional development of the enlisted force; travels to engage Airmen and families; testifies before Congress; consults with sister-service senior enlisted leaders; serves as waiver authority for non-tiered compliance items (§§ 1.2, Preamble).
- **AF/A1LE (Chiefs' Group):** Announces the assignment of the CMSAF position (§ 1.1.2).
- **Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel and Services (AF/A1):** Develops guidance and procedures for CMSAF, senior enlisted leadership management, and AFEC programs, in collaboration with AF/RE and NGB/CF (§ Preamble).
- **Chief of Air Force Reserve (AF/RE) & Director of ANG (NGB/CF):** Collaborate with AF/A1 on guidance development for programs covered by this AFI (§ Preamble).
- **SAF/MR:** Certifying authority for this publication.
- **CCMs (various levels — Wing, NAF, MAJCOM, Joint Base, Deployed, Tenant):** Carry out roles and responsibilities as defined in Chapters 3 and associated sections (§§ 3.1–3.13).
- **Group SELs:** Fulfill roles and responsibilities defined in Chapters 4–5 (§§ 4.1–4.2, 5.1–5.2).
- **AFSELC, AFCFMC, AFEC, SESC:** Advisory councils with roles defined in Chapters 7–10.

## Key Requirements

1. The CMSAF is afforded DoD Order of Precedence as Distinguished Visitor Code 4 in the capacity of senior enlisted advisor to the CSAF. (T-0) (§ 1.1.3)
2. Former CMSAFs are accorded precedence before all other retired members of the same grade per DoD Order of Precedence. (T-0) (§ 1.1.4)
3. Waiver requests for tiered compliance items must be submitted through the chain of command to the appropriate Tier waiver approval authority; non-tiered items route through the chain of command to the CMSAF. (§ Preamble)
4. CCM selection, assignment, and tenure processes differ by component (RegAF, ANG, AFR) and are governed by specific procedures in Chapter 2 (§§ 2.2–2.7).
5. Group SEL selection processes, including ANG funding provisions (9G100), are prescribed in Chapter 4 (§§ 4.1–4.2, Table 4.1).
6. Housing designations for key and essential positions are addressed in Chapter 6 (§ 6.1).
7. Career Field Manager (CFM) selection process is codified, and the AFCFMC is formally established as an advisory body (Chapter 8, §§ 8.1–8.3).
8. Outstanding Airmen of the Year (OAY) are each authorized to bring their spouse, family member, or one person of their choosing to recognition events (§ 9.3).
9. All records generated under this publication must comply with AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule (§ Preamble).
10. This publication may not be supplemented (§ Preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

- **(a) CMSAF selection criteria:** While § 1.1.1 states the CSAF selects the CMSAF, no selection criteria, eligibility requirements, or formal nomination process are articulated in the available text—only that tenure is at the CSAF's determination.
- **(b) USSF exclusion without cross-reference:** The publication twice states it does not apply to the Space Force, but does not reference the equivalent Space Force instruction (if any), which could create confusion for joint or cross-component scenarios.
- **(c) Waiver authority scope for CMSAF:** The CMSAF is designated waiver authority for "non-tiered compliance items," but the text does not clearly define what constitutes a non-tiered item versus an untiered administrative provision, leaving room for interpretation.
- **(d) Incomplete visibility into Chapters 2–10:** The extracted text truncates after § 1.2.5, so the detailed CCM/Group SEL selection procedures, council charters, and housing provisions (Chapters 2–10) could not be fully evaluated for internal conflicts or omissions.
