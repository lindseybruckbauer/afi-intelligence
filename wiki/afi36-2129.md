# AFI 36-2129: Logistics Plans Management

**OPR:** AF/A4LX | **Effective:** 9 July 2019 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2129, 20 December 2011

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 36-2129 provides guidance and procedures related to the professional development of Logistics Plans (2G0X1) personnel, addressing functional area core functions, personnel management, career progression, and training. It applies to all civilian employees and uniformed members of the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard. Compliance is mandatory, and supplements must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (§1, Preamble).

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **AF/A4 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Logistics, Engineering and Force Protection):** Ensures agile combat support, sustainment, and readiness through planning, programming, budgeting, and developing policy/guidance IAW HAFMD 1-38 (§2.1).
- **AF/A4L (Director of Logistics):** Organizes, trains, and equips logistics personnel; develops logistics plans policy and guidance (§2.2).
- **AF/A4LX (Logistics Operations, Plans, and Programs Division):** Develops, coordinates, and publishes logistics plans policy for WRM, Support Agreements, ACSA, Deployment Planning & Execution, and BaS&E Site Planning; acts as Logistics Module and BaS&E Site Planning system program manager; ensures subordinate organizations assess and report readiness IAW AFI 90-201 (§2.3).
- **Air Force Career Field Manager:** Establishes career field entry requirements, manages trained personnel requirements, develops career-long training plans, constructs career paths, evaluates training effectiveness, monitors career field health/manpower, and publishes the Logistics Plans CFETP (§2.4).
- **MAJCOMs/DRUs/FOAs/Commanders:** Validate, prioritize, and advocate for mission-driven requirements; perform Lead MAJCOM responsibilities per AFPD 10-9; work with AFIMSC on requirements and integrated planning; monitor compliance with common output level standards (§2.5).
- **MAJCOM Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Compiles and submits enlisted development team vectoring packages; makes recommendations on vector forms; provides feedback to installation senior enlisted logistics planners and members (§1.6.6.1.2).
- **Installation Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Reviews enlisted development team submissions for correctness (T-1), provides vectoring recommendations (T-1), and forwards completed packages to MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner (T-1) (§1.6.6.1.1).
- **Supervisors:** Assess new logistics plans personnel's competency levels upon arrival (T-3); re-certify new logistics planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if not assigned to duty position within last two years (T-3) (§1.4.3).

## Key Requirements
1. Logistics plans personnel **will not** be appointed to act as unit deployment managers (T-1) (§1.4.2).
2. Supervisors must assess new logistics plans personnel's competency levels upon arrival (T-3) (§1.4.3.1).
3. Supervisors must re-certify new logistics planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if not assigned to their duty position within the last two years (T-3); exceptions apply for short tours (≤12 months) and organizations with two or fewer personnel assigned (§1.4.3.2).
4. Logistics plans personnel should only be used to accomplish core functions: adaptive planning, deployment planning/execution, BaS&E site planning, WRM, support agreements, and ACSA (§1.3, §1.4.2).
5. All applicable SMSgts, MSgts, and MSgt selects must complete the vector worksheet and provide it to the installation's senior enlisted logistics planner for the Enlisted Development Team process IAW AFI 36-2640 (§1.6.6.1).
6. Installation senior enlisted logistics planners must review, recommend, and forward vectoring packages (T-1) (§1.6.6.1.1).
7. Civilian logisticians should receive training commensurate with grade and duty assignment, including at minimum the basic Logistics Plans Specialist Course (§1.9).
8. All supplements directly implementing this publication must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (Preamble).
9. Records created under this publication must be maintained IAW AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per the AF Records Disposition Schedule (Preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities
- **(a) Unclear authority for MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner actions:** The vectoring responsibilities at §1.6.6.1.2 lack tier designations, unlike the installation-level counterparts at §1.6.6.1.1 (all T-1), creating ambiguity about enforcement and waiver authority for MAJCOM-level vectoring tasks.
- **(b) Incomplete text at §2.5.5:** The document text is truncated mid-sentence ("Air component commands will provide logistics plans' personnel to the Air Force Forces…"), leaving the full scope of air component command responsibilities unaddressed.
- **(c) Civilian workforce development:** While §1.9 states civilians "should" receive training commensurate with grade and duty, no tier compliance level is assigned and no detailed civilian career progression framework is provided, despite the publication's stated applicability to all civilian employees.
- **(d) AFIMSC roles:** The Summary of Changes states AFIMSC roles and responsibilities were reiterated, but the extracted text only references AFIMSC indirectly (§2.5.3); the full AFIMSC section may exist beyond the extracted portion, but its scope is not visible for review.
