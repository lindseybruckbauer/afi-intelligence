# AFI 36-2129: Logistics Plans Management

**OPR:** AF/A4LX (Logistics Operations, Plans, and Programs Division) | **Effective:** 9 July 2019 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2129, 20 December 2011

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 36-2129 provides guidance and procedures related to the professional development of Logistics Plans (2G0X1) personnel, addressing functional area core functions, personnel management, career progression, and personnel training (§1.1). This instruction applies to all civilian employees and uniformed members of the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard. Compliance is mandatory, and supplements must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **AF/A4 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Logistics, Engineering and Force Protection):** Ensures agile combat support, sustainment, and readiness through planning, programming, budgeting, and developing policy/guidance IAW HAFMD 1-38 (§2.1).
- **AF/A4L (Director of Logistics):** Organizes, trains, and equips logistics personnel; develops logistics plans policy and guidance (§2.2).
- **AF/A4LX (Logistics Operations, Plans, and Programs Division):** Develops, coordinates, and publishes logistics plans policy for WRM, Support Agreements, ACSA, Deployment Planning & Execution, and BaS&E Site Planning; acts as Logistics Module and BaS&E Site Planning system program manager; ensures subordinate organizations assess and report readiness IAW AFI 90-201 (§2.3).
- **Air Force Career Field Manager:** Establishes career field entry requirements, manages trained personnel requirements, develops career-long training plans, constructs career paths, evaluates training effectiveness, monitors career field health/manpower, publishes the Logistics Plans CFETP (§2.4).
- **MAJCOMs/DRUs/FOAs/Commanders:** Validate, prioritize, and advocate for mission-driven requirements; perform Lead MAJCOM responsibilities per AFPD 10-9; work with AFIMSC on requirements and integrated planning; monitor compliance with common output level standards (§2.5).
- **MAJCOM Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Compiles and submits vectoring forms to the enlisted development team; provides vector recommendations; delivers feedback to installation senior enlisted logistics planners and members (§1.6.6.1.2).
- **Installation Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Reviews enlisted development submissions for accuracy (T-1), provides vectoring recommendations (T-1), forwards completed vectoring packages to MAJCOM (T-1) (§1.6.6.1.1).
- **Supervisors:** Assess new logistics plans personnel competency upon arrival (T-3); re-certify new planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if not assigned to their new duty position within the last two years (T-3) (§1.4.3).

## Key Requirements
1. Logistics plans personnel **will not** be appointed to act as unit deployment managers (T-1) (§1.4.2).
2. Supervisors must assess new logistics plans personnel competency levels upon arrival (T-3) (§1.4.3.1).
3. Supervisors must re-certify new planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days when the member has not been in their new duty position within the last two years (T-3); exceptions apply for short tours (≤12 months) and organizations with two or fewer personnel assigned (§1.4.3.2).
4. All applicable SMSgts, MSgts, and MSgt-selects must complete and provide the vector worksheet to the installation senior enlisted logistics planner per AFI 36-2640 (§1.6.6.1).
5. Installation senior enlisted logistics planners must review, recommend, and forward vectoring packages (T-1) (§1.6.6.1.1).
6. Civilian logisticians should receive training commensurate with grade and duty assignment, including at minimum the basic Logistics Plans Specialist Course (§1.9).
7. Logistics plans personnel should only perform core functions identified in §1.3 (adaptive planning, deployment planning/execution, BaS&E site planning, WRM, support agreements, ACSA) (§1.4.2).
8. All supplements directly implementing this publication must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (Preamble).
9. Waiver requests must be submitted through the chain of command to the appropriate tier waiver approval authority (Preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities
- **(a) Unclear authority assignment for MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner:** The vectoring responsibilities at §1.6.6.1.2 lack tier designations, unlike the parallel installation-level tasks at §1.6.6.1.1 (all T-1), leaving the enforceability of MAJCOM-level vectoring actions ambiguous.
- **(b) Incomplete AFIMSC responsibilities:** The Summary of Changes highlights reiterating AFIMSC roles, yet the extracted text references AFIMSC only tangentially at §2.5.3 in the context of MAJCOMs working with AFIMSC; dedicated AFIMSC responsibilities appear to fall beyond the extracted portion, leaving a potential gap.
- **(c) Civilian career progression:** While §1.9 notes civilians should receive training commensurate with their grade, no structured career progression framework analogous to the enlisted skill-level system (§1.6) is provided for civilian logisticians.
- **(d) §2.5.5 truncated:** The text cuts off mid-sentence regarding air component commands providing logistics plans personnel to AFFOR, leaving that requirement incomplete in the available text.
- **(e) ARC/ANG rotation guidance:** The exception at §1.4.3.2 states ARC and ANG units "should" rotate personnel every 24–36 months, using permissive rather than mandatory language, creating potential inconsistency in application.
