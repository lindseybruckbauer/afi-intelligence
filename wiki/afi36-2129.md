# AFI 36-2129: Logistics Plans Management

**OPR:** AF/A4LX | **Effective:** 9 July 2019 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2129, 20 December 2011

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 36-2129 provides guidance and procedures related to the professional development of Logistics Plans (2G0X1) personnel, addressing functional area core functions, personnel management, career progression, and training. It applies to all civilian employees and uniformed members of the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard. Compliance is mandatory, and supplements at any level must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (Section 1, introductory paragraph).

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **AF/A4 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Logistics, Engineering and Force Protection):** Ensures agile combat support, sustainment, and readiness through planning, programming, budgeting, and developing written policy and guidance IAW HAFMD 1-38 (§2.1).
- **AF/A4L (Director of Logistics):** Organizes, trains, and equips logistics personnel; develops logistics plans policy and guidance (§2.2).
- **AF/A4LX (Logistics Operations, Plans, and Programs Division):** Develops, coordinates, and publishes logistics plans policy and guidance for WRM, support agreements, ACSA, deployment planning/execution, and BaS&E site planning; acts as system program manager for Logistics Module and BaS&E Site Planning; ensures subordinate organizations assess and report readiness IAW AFI 90-201 (§2.3).
- **Air Force Career Field Manager:** Establishes career field entry requirements; manages trained personnel requirements; develops career-long training plans; constructs career paths; evaluates training effectiveness; monitors career field health and manpower; publishes the Logistics Plans CFETP (§2.4).
- **MAJCOMs/DRUs/FOAs/Commanders:** Validate, prioritize, and advocate for mission-driven requirements; perform Lead MAJCOM responsibilities per AFPD 10-9; work with AFIMSC on requirements and integrated planning; monitor compliance with common output level standards; air component commands provide logistics plans personnel to AFFOR staffs (§2.5).
- **MAJCOM Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Compiles and submits Enlisted Development Team vectoring forms; makes vectoring recommendations; provides feedback to installation senior enlisted logistics planners and members (§1.6.6.1.2).
- **Installation Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Reviews vectoring submissions for correctness; provides vectoring recommendations to submitters; forwards completed packages to MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner (§1.6.6.1.1; T-1).
- **Supervisors:** Assess new logistics plans personnel competency levels upon arrival (T-3); re-certify new logistics planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if not assigned to their duty position within the last two years (T-3) (§1.4.3).

## Key Requirements
1. Logistics plans personnel **will not** be appointed to act as unit deployment managers (§1.4.2; T-1).
2. Supervisors must assess new logistics plans personnel's competency levels upon arrival (§1.4.3.1; T-3).
3. Supervisors must re-certify new logistics planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if the member has not been in their new duty position within the last two years (§1.4.3.2; T-3). Exceptions: short tour assignments (12 months or less) and organizations with two or fewer personnel assigned (§1.4.3.2.1–1.4.3.2.2). ARC/ANG units should rotate personnel within duty sections every 24–36 months.
4. Logistics plans personnel should only accomplish core functions identified in §1.3 (adaptive planning, deployment planning/execution, BaS&E site planning, WRM, support agreements, ACSA) (§1.4.2).
5. All applicable SMSgts, MSgts, and MSgt-selects must complete vector worksheets and provide them to the installation senior enlisted logistics planner per AFI 36-2640 (§1.6.6.1).
6. Installation senior enlisted logistics planners must review, recommend, and forward vectoring packages to MAJCOM (§1.6.6.1.1; T-1).
7. Civilian logisticians should receive training commensurate with grade and duty assignment, including at minimum the basic Logistics Plans Specialist Course (§1.9).
8. All supplements directly implementing this publication must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (introductory paragraph).
9. Waiver requests for tiered compliance items must be submitted through the chain of command to the appropriate tier waiver approval authority (introductory paragraph).

## Gaps & Ambiguities
- **(a) MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner responsibilities lack tier designations.** Unlike the installation-level counterpart (T-1), the MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner's vectoring responsibilities in §1.6.6.1.2 carry no tier compliance statements, making enforcement authority unclear.
- **(b) AFIMSC roles and responsibilities are mentioned in the Summary of Changes as reiterated, but the extracted text only briefly references AFIMSC in §2.5.3 in the context of MAJCOM coordination.** The specific AFIMSC responsibilities are not fully visible in the available text, creating ambiguity about AFIMSC's distinct authorities versus MAJCOM authorities.
- **(c) Civilian training requirements (§1.9) use "should" language** rather than mandatory compliance language with tier designations, leaving the obligation for civilian logistician training largely advisory despite the instruction applying to civilian employees.
- **(d) Section 2.5.5 text is truncated,** cutting off the requirement for air component commands to provide logistics plans personnel to AFFOR staffs; the full scope of this requirement is indeterminate from the available text.
- **(e) Recruiting guidance (§1.10)** is limited to a single sentence with no tier compliance or specific procedural requirements, despite being set apart in its own section.
