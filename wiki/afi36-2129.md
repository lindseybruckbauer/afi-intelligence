# AFI 36-2129: Logistics Plans Management

**OPR:** AF/A4LX | **Effective:** 9 July 2019 | **Implements:** AFPD 36-21, Utilization and Classification of Air Force Military Personnel | **Supersedes:** AFI 36-2129, 20 December 2011

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 36-2129 provides guidance and procedures related to the professional development, career progression, training, personnel management, and manpower utilization of Logistics Plans (2G0X1) personnel. It applies to all civilian employees and uniformed members of the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard. The publication defines core functions of the logistics plans functional area, establishes training requirements, and delineates roles and responsibilities from Headquarters Air Force through MAJCOM and installation levels. Compliance is mandatory; supplements at any level must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (§1, Preamble).

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **AF/A4 (Deputy Chief of Staff, Logistics, Engineering and Force Protection):** Ensures agile combat support, sustainment, and readiness through planning, programming, budgeting, and developing policy and guidance IAW HAFMD 1-38 (§2.1).
- **AF/A4L (Director of Logistics):** Organizes, trains, and equips logistics personnel; develops logistics plans policy and guidance (§2.2).
- **AF/A4LX (Logistics Operations, Plans, and Programs Division):** Develops, coordinates, and publishes logistics plans policy for WRM, support agreements, ACSA, deployment planning, and BaS&E site planning; acts as Logistics Module and BaS&E site planning system program manager; ensures subordinate organizations assess and report readiness IAW AFI 90-201 (§2.3).
- **Air Force Career Field Manager:** Establishes career field entry requirements, manages trained personnel requirements, develops career-long training plans, constructs career paths, evaluates training effectiveness, monitors career field health and manpower, publishes the Logistics Plans CFETP (§2.4).
- **MAJCOMs/DRUs/FOAs/Commanders:** Validate and prioritize mission-driven requirements, perform Lead MAJCOM responsibilities per AFPD 10-9, work with AFIMSC to identify requirements and improve enterprise visibility, monitor compliance with standard levels of service (§2.5).
- **MAJCOM Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Compiles and submits completed vectoring forms to the enlisted development team, makes vectoring recommendations, and provides feedback to installation senior enlisted logistics planners and members (§1.6.6.1.2).
- **Installation Senior Enlisted Logistics Planner:** Reviews enlisted development vectoring submissions for accuracy (T-1), provides vectoring recommendations (T-1), and forwards completed packages to MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner (T-1) (§1.6.6.1.1).
- **Supervisors:** Assess new logistics plans personnel competency levels upon arrival (T-3); re-certify new logistics planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if not assigned to the duty position within the last two years (T-3) (§1.4.3).

## Key Requirements
1. Logistics plans personnel will **not** be appointed to act as unit deployment managers (T-1) (§1.4.2).
2. Supervisors must assess new logistics plans personnel competency levels upon arrival (T-3) (§1.4.3.1).
3. Supervisors must re-certify new logistics planners (E-6 and below) within 120 days if the member has not been assigned to the duty position within the last two years (T-3), with exceptions for short tours (≤12 months) and organizations with two or fewer personnel (§1.4.3.2).
4. Installation senior enlisted logistics planners must review vectoring submissions for accuracy, provide recommendations, and forward packages to the MAJCOM level (T-1) (§1.6.6.1.1).
5. All applicable SMSgts, MSgts, and MSgt-selects must complete and provide the vector worksheet to the installation's senior enlisted logistics planner per AFI 36-2640 (§1.6.6.1).
6. Logistics plans personnel should only perform core functions identified in §1.3 (adaptive planning, deployment planning/execution, BaS&E site planning, WRM, support agreements, ACSA) (§1.4.2).
7. Civilian logisticians should receive training and education commensurate with grade and duty assignment, including at minimum the basic Logistics Plans Specialist Course (§1.9).
8. All supplements directly implementing this publication must be routed to AF/A4LX for coordination prior to certification and approval (Preamble).
9. Records created under this publication must be maintained IAW AFMAN 33-363 and disposed of per the AF Records Disposition Schedule (Preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities
- **(a) Incomplete text for §2.5.5:** The extracted text cuts off mid-sentence regarding air component commands providing logistics plans personnel to Air Force Forces, leaving this responsibility undefined.
- **(b) MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner responsibilities lack tier designations:** Unlike the installation-level counterpart whose tasks are designated T-1, the MAJCOM senior enlisted logistics planner's parallel responsibilities in §1.6.6.1.2 carry no tier waiver authority, creating ambiguity about enforceability.
- **(c) Civilian training requirements are aspirational, not mandatory:** Section 1.9 uses "should" rather than directive language and carries no tier designation, leaving civilian training compliance effectively unenforceable.
- **(d) AFIMSC roles referenced but not fully detailed:** Section 2.5.3 notes that MAJCOMs work with AFIMSC, and the Summary of Changes highlights AFIMSC roles were reiterated, but the extracted text does not contain a dedicated AFIMSC responsibilities section—suggesting either truncation or minimal treatment relative to the stated revision purpose.
- **(e) Recruiting guidance is minimal:** Section 1.10 states logistics plans personnel "should actively recruit and conduct interviews" of cross-trainees but provides no procedures, criteria, or accountability framework.
