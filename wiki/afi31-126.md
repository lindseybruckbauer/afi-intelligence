# DAFI 31-126: DoD Military Working Dog (MWD) Program

**OPR:** AF/A4 (DCS/Logistics, Engineering & Force Protection) | **Effective:** 15 April 2026 | **Implements:** DAFPD 31-1 | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This DAFGM updates DAFI 31-126 to implement military working dog (MWD) health and welfare policies and the FY2023 NDAA (Section 382) mandate prohibiting abandonment of MWDs overseas. It applies to all DoD Components—including OSD, Military Departments, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands, Defense Agencies, DoD Field Activities, and any entity with a military or contract working dog (CWD) requirement or supporting role—within the United States, its territories, and foreign countries. Where inconsistent with other DAF publications, this guidance prevails per DAFI 90-160 and DAFMAN 90-161.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretary of the Air Force:** Serves as the DoD Executive Agent (EA) for the DoD MWD Program.
- **AF/A4 (Lt Gen Kenyon K. Bell, signatory):** Issues the DAFGM on behalf of the Secretary; AF/A4S houses the DoD MWD Program Manager (§2.4.5.3).
- **Joint Service MWD Working Dog Committee (JSMWDC):** Determines annual Trained Dog Requirements (TDR); convenes in Q3 of each FY to review and confirm the three-year TDR; ratifies manpower calculation results (§1.2, 1.2.2).
- **DoD MWD Program Manager (PM):** Reviews CWD contract requests and provides recommendations (§2.4.5.2); approves out-of-pipeline dog procurements (§2.4.3); certifies completion of CWD disposition (§2.4.5.4.6); coordinates prior to awarding any CWD contract (§2.4.5.1).
- **Service MWD Program Managers:** May request out-of-cycle TDR increases in writing to the DoD MWD PM (§1.2.1); participate in CWD contract review panels (§2.4.5.2); submit TDR changes (§1.2.2.2).
- **341st Training Squadron (341 TRS):** Procures and certifies trained working dogs with DoD MWD EA Program Office approval (§2.4.3); manages MWD disposition processes for CWDs (§2.4.5.4.5); submits TDR changes (§1.2.2.2).
- **341 TRS/CC and Director, Holland Veterinary Hospital (HVH):** Recommend closure of CWD disposition to the DoD MWD PM; not responsible for costs/resourcing of CWDs at contract conclusion (§2.4.5.4.6).
- **Agents (COCOMs, Installation Commands, Services, NGB, DSCA, DoD Agencies):** Responsible for CWD health and welfare, including during contract termination; must coordinate statements of work prior to submission (§2.4.1, 2.4.5.4, 2.4.5.4.5).
- **Attending Veterinarian:** Authorized to euthanize an MWD experiencing undue suffering with poor prognosis without prior consultation with DoD MWD VS personnel (Authority Statements).
- **CCB Chairperson (or designated representative):** Sole authority to approve or disapprove CCB actions (Authority Statements).
- **US Army Veterinary Services:** May provide CWD care on reimbursable, space-available basis if contractually agreed; may provide emergency care before civilian referral (§2.4.4.1, 2.4.4.2).
- **Contractors:** Responsible for reimbursement of all military and civilian veterinary care for CWDs (§2.4.4.3); dogs failing agent requirements remain the contractor's responsibility (§2.4).

## Key Requirements

1. **Prohibition on Abandonment:** MWDs and CWDs used in military operations outside CONUS shall not be abandoned, per 10 USC §995 and PL 117-263 §382. All programs and contracts must adhere to this prohibition (§2.4.6). **(T-0)**
2. **Tri-Annual Needs Assessment:** DoD and service components must perform a needs assessment of MWD requirements every three years, addressing current/future capabilities, continuity of operations, and projected COCOM/DSCA/inter-agency mission support (§1.2.2.1).
3. **CWD Use as Last Resort:** CWDs are limited to situations where a permanent or rotational MWD Team is not feasible; CWDs are restricted to defensive roles and strictly prohibited from offensive operations (§2.4.5.1).
4. **CWD Contract Coordination:** Use of CWDs requires coordination with the DoD MWD PM prior to contract award; all new and recurring CWD contracts must be reviewed by a panel including the DoD MWD PM, AVS Director, Service MWD PMs, and 341 TRS/CC (§2.4.5.1, 2.4.5.2).
5. **CWD Tracking:** All CWDs must be entered by assigned tattoo in the Working Dog Management System (or replacement) and tracked from acceptance through disposition (§2.4.5.4.4).
6. **18 Required Health and Welfare Items:** All federally managed and contractor managed working dog programs must address 18 health and welfare areas (abuse/neglect, emergency medical care, euthanasia, exercise, food/water, grooming, and others per Figure A3.1) in their policies, practices, and statements of work (Attachment 3, §A3.1).
7. **CWD Medical Standards:** CWDs must meet the supporting Agent's MWD requirements including training, certification, and veterinary medical standards no less restrictive than current AVS standards (§2.4.5.4.1).
8. **Statement of Work Compliance:** CWD statements of work must address all 18 health and welfare areas identified in Attachment 3 and include all veterinary documentation requirements supporting future disposition (§2.4.5.4.2, 2.4.5.4.3).
9. **TDR Revision Controls:** Out-of-cycle TDR increases must be requested in writing, are reviewed case-by-case, and kept to a minimum for unexpected urgent mission changes only (§1.2.1). **(T-1)**
10. **Agent Disposition Responsibility:** Agents must fund and support CWD health and welfare in the event of early, planned, or unexpected contract termination; CWD transfer to 341 TRS is administrative only (§2.4.5.4.5).
11. **Records Management:** All records generated must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the AF Records Disposition Schedule (Memorandum preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Incomplete Health and Welfare Table:** The 18 required health and welfare items in Figure A3.1 are only partially reproduced in the extracted text (cut off after "Grooming" and beginning of "Health and welfare"), leaving at least 12 of the 18 areas unspecified in the available text. Full compliance guidance for all 18 items is unclear from the document as parsed.
2. **CCB Undefined:** The authority statement references a "CCB Chairperson" with approval/disapproval authority, but the extracted text does not define what the CCB is, its membership, or when it convenes—likely addressed in the base DAFI 31-126 but not in this DAFGM.
3. **Tier Waiver Authority Unspecified:** The waiver process references "appropriate Tier waiver approval authority" but does not specify which tier applies to which requirement beyond the two tiering markers noted (T-0, T-1), nor does it identify specific waiver approval authorities.
4. **Attachment 4 Not Addressed:** The preamble references additions of both Attachments 3 and 4, but no content from Attachment 4 appears in the extracted text, leaving its subject matter and requirements unknown.
5. **Supersession Ambiguity:** The DAFGM does not specify what prior version of DAFI 31-126 it modifies, nor does it list a formal supersession, making version control tracking difficult.
6. **Certifying Official Missing:** The "Certified by" field is blank in the document metadata, which is unusual for DAF publications and may indicate an administrative gap.
