# DAFI 31-126: DoD Military Working Dog (MWD) Program

**OPR:** AF/A4 (DCS/Logistics, Engineering & Force Protection) | **Effective:** 15 April 2026 | **Implements:** 10 USC § 2387; 10 USC § 995; Public Law 117-263, Section 382 (FY2023 NDAA) | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This DAFGM updates DAFI 31-126 to implement MWD health and welfare policies, establish requirements for Contract Working Dogs (CWDs), and codify the Section 382 mandate prohibiting abandonment of military working dogs overseas. It applies to all DoD Components—including OSD, Military Departments, Joint Staff, Combatant Commands, Defense Agencies, DoD Field Activities, and any entity with a military or contract working dog requirement or supporting role—within the United States, its territories, and foreign countries. The DAFGM prevails over inconsistent DAF publications per DAFI 90-160 and DAFMAN 90-161, and becomes void one year from issuance or upon rewrite/replacement of DAFI 31-126, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretary of the Air Force:** Serves as DoD Executive Agent (EA) for the DoD MWD Program.
- **AF/A4 (Lt Gen Kenyon K. Bell, signatory):** Issues guidance on behalf of the Secretary; the DoD MWD Program Manager is assigned to AF/A4S.
- **Joint Services Military Working Dog Committee (JSMWDC):** Determines annual Trained Dog Requirements (TDR); convenes in the third quarter of each FY to review and confirm the three-year TDR; ratifies manpower calculation results (¶1.2).
- **DoD MWD Program Manager (PM):** Reviews and approves TDR changes (¶1.2.2.2); coordinates CWD contract requests (¶2.4.5.1); certifies completion of CWD disposition (¶2.4.5.4.6); provides recommendation on all new and recurring CWD contracts (¶2.4.5.2).
- **Service MWD PMs:** May request out-of-cycle TDR revisions in writing to the DoD MWD PM (¶1.2.1); participate in CWD contract review panels (¶2.4.5.2); submit TDR changes (¶1.2.2.2).
- **341st Training Squadron (TRS):** Procures and certifies working dogs as part of annual TDR with written DoD MWD EA Program Office approval (¶2.4.3); manages CWD disposition processes (¶2.4.5.4.5–2.4.5.4.6); submits TDR changes (¶1.2.2.2).
- **341 TRS/CC and Director, Holland MWD Veterinary Hospital (HVH):** Recommend closure of CWD dispositions to the DoD MWD PM; not responsible for costs of CWD support post-contract (¶2.4.5.4.6).
- **Requesting Agents (COCOMs, Installation Commands, Services, NGB, DSCA, DoD Agencies):** Responsible for coordinating CWD statements of work (¶2.4.5.4); funding and supporting CWD health and welfare through disposition, including upon contract termination (¶2.4.5.4.5); entering CWDs into the Working Dog Management System (¶2.4.5.4.4).
- **CWD Contract Review Panel:** Composed of DoD MWD PM, Director Army Veterinary Services (AVS) or designee, Service MWD PMs, and 341 TRS/CC or designee; reviews all new and recurring CWD contracts (¶2.4.5.2).
- **US Army Veterinary Services:** May provide care to CWDs on a reimbursable, space-available basis if contractually agreed (¶2.4.4.1); may perform emergency veterinary procedures to prevent suffering or save life/limb/eyesight on a reimbursable basis (¶2.4.4.2).
- **Attending Veterinarian:** Authorized to euthanize an MWD experiencing undue suffering with poor prognosis without prior DoD MWD VS consultation (T-0) (Authority Statements).
- **Contractors:** Responsible for reimbursement of all military and civilian veterinary care for CWDs (¶2.4.4.3); dogs failing to complete agent requirements remain contractor responsibility (¶2.4).
- **CCB Chairperson (or designated representative):** Sole authority to approve or disapprove Configuration Control Board actions (Authority Statements).
- **Air Education and Training Command (AETC):** Works in concert with the EA and JSMWDC to compute projected 341 TRS MWD training requirements 18–24 months prior to each FY (¶1.2.2).

## Key Requirements

1. **Prohibition on Overseas Abandonment (T-0):** All DoD MWD and CWD programs and contracts must adhere to the prohibition on abandoning MWDs and CWDs used outside CONUS, per 10 USC § 995 and PL 117-263, Section 382 (¶2.4.6).
2. **TDR Out-of-Cycle Requests:** Must be in writing to the DoD MWD PM, limited to unexpected urgent mission changes, and reviewed case-by-case (T-1) (¶1.2.1).
3. **Tri-Annual Needs Assessment:** DoD and service components must perform a needs assessment evaluating MWD requirements every three years, addressing current/future capabilities, continuity of operations, and projected COCOM/DSCA/inter-agency missions (¶1.2.2.1).
4. **CWD Usage Restricted to Last Resort:** CWDs are limited to use only when a permanent or rotational MWD team is not feasible; CWDs are restricted to defensive roles and strictly prohibited from offensive operations (¶2.4.5.1).
5. **CWD Contract Coordination:** All CWD contracts require coordination with the DoD MWD PM prior to award and review by the designated panel (¶2.4.5.1–2.4.5.2).
6. **18 Required Health and Welfare Items:** All federally managed and contractor-managed working dog programs must address all 18 health and welfare areas identified in Figure A3.1, including abuse/neglect, emergency medical care, euthanasia, exercise, food/water, and grooming (Attachment 3, ¶A3.1).
7. **CWD Tracking:** All CWDs must be entered by assigned tattoo into the Working Dog Management System (or replacement) and tracked from acceptance through disposition (¶2.4.5.4.4).
8. **CWD Medical Standards:** CWDs must meet the supporting Agent's MWD requirements for training, certification, and veterinary standards; medical standards must be no less restrictive than current AVS standards (¶2.4.5.4.1).
9. **CWD Veterinary Documentation:** Statements of work must include all veterinary documentation requirements to support future disposition actions (¶2.4.5.4.2).
10. **Agent Funding Obligation for CWD Disposition:** Agents must fund and support CWD health and welfare through early, planned, or unexpected contract termination; disposition may take months to over a year (¶2.4.5.4.5).
11. **Records Management:** All records generated under this publication must adhere to AFI 33-322 and be disposed per the AF Records Disposition Schedule (Memorandum preamble).
12. **Working Dog Procurement Restriction:** Procurement of working dogs outside the MWD pipeline is restricted; 341 TRS may procure already-trained dogs only with written DoD MWD EA Program Office approval (¶2.4.3).
13. **Manpower Changes Ratification Timeline:** Manpower equation results must be presented and ratified by JSMWDC no earlier than 18–24 months prior to proposed manning changes (¶1.2).
