# AFGM 2026-36-2033: Air Force Guidance Memorandum Reimplementing Warrant Officers

**OPR:** AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services) | **Effective:** 4 February 2026 | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-20, 36-21, 36-24, 36-25, 36-26, 36-29, 36-30, 36-32 | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This AFGM immediately establishes warrant officers in the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve (AFR), and Air National Guard (ANG). It promulgates personnel policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for managing warrant officers throughout the entire personnel life cycle (Ch. 1). The initial cadre of warrant officers will serve in two career fields—Warfighter Communications & Information Technology Systems Operations (17W) and Cyber Effects & Warfare Operations (17Y)—to maintain technical leadership in specialties with highly perishable skills (Ch. 2). Compliance is mandatory across all AF/DAF policies, instructions, manuals, and associated forms. Where inconsistent with other AF/DAF publications, this AFGM prevails per DAFMAN 90-161. The memorandum becomes void one year from its date or upon publication of a permanent interim change or rewrite, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR (Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary for Manpower and Reserve Affairs):** Issued the AFGM by order of the Secretary of the Air Force; signatory authority (Cover memo).
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Serves as OPR for policy questions regarding warrant officer personnel management (Ch. 1).
- **AF/RE (Chief of Air Force Reserve):** Addresses policy questions for AFR warrant officer matters (Ch. 1).
- **NGB/CF (Director, Air National Guard):** Addresses policy questions for ANG warrant officer matters; may further restrict maximum accession age (§4.1.5).
- **The President / Secretary of War (delegated):** Authority for warrant and commission appointments across all WO grades (§3.2).
- **AFRS Recruiting Operations Director / 367th Recruiting Group Recruiting Operations Director:** May further restrict maximum accession age based on component needs (§4.1.5).
- **AFRC/A6:** Conducts selection boards for AFR warrant officer applicants (§4.1.11).
- **AETC/SG (Command Surgeon General):** Provides concurrence for postpartum members seeking early return to training physical requirements (§4.1.7).
- **AFRS/RSG (Accession Medical Waiver Division):** Processes pregnancy-related medical waivers per DAFMAN 48-123 (§4.1.7).
- **Unit Commanders:** Approve DAF Form 357 (Family Care Certification) for applicants with dependents (§4.1.9); endorse AF IMT 1288 for AFR applicants (§4.1.11).
- **Tier Waiver Approval Authorities:** Approve waiver requests for tiered compliance items routed through the chain of command (Cover memo).

## Key Requirements

1. All warrant officer applicants must be United States citizens (§4.1.1). (T-1 implied by accession requirement)
2. Applicants must be at least 18 years of age for an original appointment (§4.1.5). **(T-1)**
3. Applicants unable to qualify for retirement before removal from active status must acknowledge this in writing and request a waiver through command channels (§4.1.5). **(T-1)**
4. Applicants must be medically qualified or hold an approved medical waiver per DAFMAN 48-123 and provide an AF Form 422 (valid 12 months) (§4.1.6).
5. All applicants must meet fitness requirements of DAFMAN 36-2905 and height/weight standards per DoDI 1308.03 (§4.1.6.1). **(T-0)**
6. Applicants must meet dress and appearance standards per DAFI 36-2903 (§4.1.6.2).
7. Members may apply for Warrant Officer Training School (WOTS) while pregnant but may not complete training while pregnant or within 6 months postpartum (20+ weeks) without physician approval and AETC/SG concurrence (§4.1.7).
8. Applicants must meet minimum education requirements: high school graduate, covered graduate, or alternate credential holder (including GED or 12 semester hours / 22.5 quarter hours of college credit) (§4.1.8).
9. Single, divorced, or separated applicants with dependents (or those married to a military/common-law spouse) must have a commander-approved DAF Form 357 per DoDI 1342.19/DAFI 36-2908 (§4.1.9). **(T-3)**
10. All accession applicants must complete AF Form 2030 (Drug and Alcohol Abuse Certificate), except those currently holding status in an air component (§4.1.10). **(T-1)**
11. Applicants must hold or initiate procedures for a Top Secret/SCI clearance; certain specialties may require a favorable CI polygraph adjudication (§4.1.13).
12. Inter-service transfer warrant officers must not be released from existing ADSCs or financial obligations of the losing service (§4.1.14.1–4.1.14.2). **(T-0 for IST per §4.1.14)**
13. All records must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule (Cover memo).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignments:**
- §3.2 states appointment authority was delegated by the President to the "Secretary of War"—a title that has not existed since 1947 (replaced by Secretary of the Army / Secretary of Defense). It is unclear whether this refers to the Secretary of the Air Force or is an archaic statutory citation that needs updating for DAF applicability.
- The OPR is listed as "Unknown" in publication metadata, though the body text identifies AF/A1; formal OPR designation is not explicitly stated on the cover memorandum.
- §4.1.14.5 (AFR conditional release authority) is cut off mid-sentence in the available text, leaving the designated authority incomplete.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed:**
- The title and implementing DAFPDs span evaluations (36-24), promotions (36-25), military standards (36-29), entitlements (36-30), and retirements/separations (36-32), yet the available text covers only grade structure and accession policy. Guidance on WO evaluation systems, promotion board procedures/criteria, professional military education requirements, retention/separation policy, and entitlements is not present in the extracted text (may reside in unparsed portions of the 14-page document).
- No duty position descriptions, manning authorizations, or force structure allocation guidance is provided for the two affected career fields (17W/17Y).
- WOTS curriculum, duration, location, and graduation standards are referenced but not described.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- The five-tier WO grade structure (W-1 through W-5) is consistent with other services, but no crosswalk is provided for how WO grades interact with existing AF enlisted (E-1 through E-9) or commissioned officer (O-1 through O-10) chains of command, potentially creating ambiguity in authority and precedence at the unit level.
