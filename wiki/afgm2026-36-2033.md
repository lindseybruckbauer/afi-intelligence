# AFGM 2026-36-2033: Air Force Guidance Memorandum Reimplementing Warrant Officers

**OPR:** AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services) | **Effective:** 4 February 2026 | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-20, 36-21, 36-24, 36-25, 36-26, 36-29, 36-30, 36-32 | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This AFGM immediately reestablishes warrant officers in the Regular Air Force (RegAF), Air Force Reserve (AFR), and Air National Guard (ANG). It promulgates personnel policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for managing warrant officers throughout the entire personnel life cycle—from accessions through grade structure (Chapter 1). Compliance is mandatory, and where this memorandum conflicts with other AF/DAF publications, its provisions prevail per DAFMAN 90-161. The initial cadre is limited to two career fields: Warfighter Communications & Information Technology Systems Operations (17W) and Cyber Effects & Warfare Operations (17Y) (Chapter 2). The memorandum becomes void one year from its date or upon incorporation into a permanent publication, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretary of the Air Force (SecAF):** Orders the reimplementation of warrant officers across all AF components.
- **SAF/MR (Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary for Manpower and Reserve Affairs):** Signs and issues the AFGM (memorandum header).
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Serves as policy OPR; addresses questions regarding policies herein (Chapter 1).
- **AF/RE (Chief of Air Force Reserve):** Addresses policy questions for AFR warrant officer matters (Chapter 1).
- **NGB/CF (Director of the Air National Guard):** Addresses policy questions for ANG warrant officer matters; may further restrict maximum accession age (§4.1.5).
- **The President:** Appoints warrant officers (W-1 by warrant, W-2 through W-5 by commission), with authority further delegated to the Secretary of War (§3.2).
- **AFRS Recruiting Operations Director / 367th Recruiting Group Recruiting Operations Director:** May further restrict maximum accession age based on component needs (§4.1.5).
- **AFRC/A6:** Conducts AFR warrant officer selection boards (§4.1.11).
- **AETC/SG (AETC Office of the Command Surgeon General):** Provides concurrence for postpartum members seeking early participation in WOTS physical requirements (§4.1.7).
- **AFRS/RSG (Accession Medical Waiver Division):** Processes pregnancy-related medical waivers per DAFMAN 48-123 (§4.1.7).
- **Unit Commanders:** Approve DAF Form 357 (Family Care Certification) for applicable applicants (§4.1.9); endorse AF IMT 1288 for AFR applicants (§4.1.11).

## Key Requirements

1. **Citizenship:** All WO applicants must be United States citizens (§4.1.1).
2. **Age:** Applicants must be at least 18 years of age for an original appointment. (T-1) (§4.1.5)
3. **Physical Fitness:** Applicants must meet fitness requirements of DAFMAN 36-2905 and height/weight standards per DoDI 1308.03. (T-0) (§4.1.6.1)
4. **Medical Qualification:** Applicants must be medically qualified or hold an approved waiver per DAFMAN 48-123; AF Form 422 required and valid for 12 months for WOTS applications (§4.1.6).
5. **Education:** Applicants must be a high school graduate, covered graduate, or alternate credential holder (including GED or minimum 12 semester hours / 22.5 quarter hours of college) (§4.1.8).
6. **Security Clearance:** Applicants may apply with a Secret clearance but must initiate procedures to obtain Top Secret/SCI access; certain specialties may require a CI polygraph (§4.1.13).
7. **Drug & Alcohol Certification:** All accession applicants must complete AF Form 2030, except those currently holding status in an air component. (T-1) (§4.1.10)
8. **Family Care Plan:** Single, divorced, separated applicants with dependents, or those married to a military/common-law spouse, must have a commander-approved DAF Form 357 prior to accession. (T-3) (§4.1.9)
9. **Pregnancy:** Members may apply while pregnant but may not complete WOTS training while pregnant or for 6 months postpartum (≥20 weeks pregnancy); early participation requires attending physician and AETC/SG written approval (§4.1.7).
10. **Inter-Service Transfer:** WOs transferring from other services are separated from their losing service and originally appointed in the AF without interrupting total service; existing ADSCs and financial obligations carry over. (T-0) (§4.1.14–4.1.14.2)
11. **Conscientious Objector:** Applicants must not be conscientious objectors under 50 USC § 3806(j) and DoDI 1300.06 (§4.1.2).
12. **Records Management:** All records created under this AFGM must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule (preamble).

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear authority assignments:**
- **Appointment authority anachronism:** Section 3.2 states appointment authority was delegated by the President to the "Secretary of War"—a title abolished in 1947. The current delegatee (presumably the Secretary of the Air Force or Secretary of Defense) is not clarified.
- **OPR identity:** The cover page lists OPR as "Unknown," and while Chapter 1 directs questions to AF/A1, AF/RE, or NGB/CF, no single OPR is formally designated for waiver processing of non-tiered items.
- **AFR conditional release authority:** Section 4.1.14.5 is truncated mid-sentence, leaving the conditional release authority for AFR warrant officers undefined.

**(b) Topics implied but not fully addressed:**
- **Evaluations (DAFPD 36-24):** Listed as implemented but no evaluation policy for warrant officers appears in the extracted text.
- **Promotions and Demotions (DAFPD 36-25):** Listed as implemented; promotion board procedures, time-in-grade requirements, and demotion criteria are absent beyond a brief IST promotion list integration note (§4.1.14.3).
- **Military Standards (DAFPD 36-29):** Referenced but no WO-specific standards articulated.
- **Military Entitlements (DAFPD 36-30):** Referenced but no WO pay, allowances, or entitlement details provided.
- **Retirements and Separations (DAFPD 36-32):** Referenced but no WO-specific retirement/separation policy beyond an age-related acknowledgment (§4.1.5).
- **Utilization and Classification (DAFPD 36-21):** Beyond naming two AFSCs (17W, 17Y), no duty position descriptions, skill-level progression, or utilization policies are defined.
- **WOTS curriculum, duration, and location** are not described despite multiple references to Warrant Officer Training School.
- **Non-prior service pathway:** Section 4.1.12 opens selection boards to non-prior service applicants but provides no distinct accession pipeline, training prerequisites, or selection criteria for this population.

**(c) Potential conflicts:**
- The one-year expiration clause (February 2027) creates a risk of a policy vacuum if a permanent publication is not fielded in time, potentially leaving warrant officers in service without governing policy.
