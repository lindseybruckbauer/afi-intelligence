# AFGM 2026-36-2033: Air Force Guidance Memorandum Reimplementing Warrant Officers

**OPR:** AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services) | **Effective:** 4 February 2026 | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-20, DAFPD 36-21, DAFPD 36-24, DAFPD 36-25, DAFPD 36-26, DAFPD 36-29, DAFPD 36-30, DAFPD 36-32 | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope

This AFGM immediately re-establishes warrant officers (WO1–CW5) in the Regular Air Force, Air Force Reserve, and Air National Guard. It promulgates personnel policy, assigns responsibilities, and prescribes procedures for managing warrant officers throughout the entire personnel life cycle—including accession, classification, evaluation, promotion, and separation (Chapter 1). The initial warrant officer cadre is limited to two career fields: Warfighter Communications & Information Technology Systems Operations (17W) and Cyber Effects & Warfare Operations (17Y) (Chapter 2). Compliance is mandatory for all AF and DAF organizations, and this AFGM supersedes conflicting guidance in other AF/DAF publications per DAFMAN 90-161. The memorandum becomes void one year from its effective date or upon publication of a permanent rewrite, whichever is earlier.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **Secretary of the Air Force (SecAF):** Orders the reimplementation of the warrant officer corps across all three AF components.
- **SAF/MR (Principal Deputy Assistant Secretary for Manpower and Reserve Affairs):** Signs and issues this AFGM on behalf of SecAF; serves as certifying authority.
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Serves as functional OPR; addresses policy questions regarding this guidance (Chapter 1).
- **AF/RE (Chief of Air Force Reserve):** Addresses policy questions for AFR warrant officer management (Chapter 1).
- **NGB/CF (Director of the Air National Guard):** Addresses policy questions for ANG warrant officer management; may further restrict maximum accession age (§4.1.5).
- **The President:** Appoints warrant officers (W-1 by warrant; W-2 through W-5 by commission), with authority further delegated to the Secretary of War (§3.2).
- **AFRC/A6:** Conducts AFR warrant officer selection boards (§4.1.11).
- **AFRS Recruiting Operations Director / 367th Recruiting Group Recruiting Operations Director:** May further restrict maximum accession age based on component needs (§4.1.5).
- **AETC/SG (AETC Command Surgeon General):** Provides concurrence for post-pregnancy early participation in WOTS physical requirements (§4.1.7).
- **Unit Commanders:** Approve DAF Form 357 (Family Care Certification) for applicable applicants (§4.1.9); endorse AF IMT 1288 for AFR applicants (§4.1.11).
- **Tier Waiver Approval Authorities:** Approve waiver requests for tiered compliance items routed through the chain of command; non-tiered items routed to the AFGM OPR.

## Key Requirements

1. **Citizenship:** All WO applicants must be United States citizens (§4.1.1). (T-1)
2. **Minimum Age:** Applicants must be at least 18 years of age for original appointment (§4.1.5). (T-1)
3. **Retirement Eligibility Acknowledgment:** Applicants who cannot qualify for retirement before removal from active status must acknowledge this in writing and request a waiver through command channels (§4.1.5). (T-1)
4. **Medical Qualification:** Applicants must be medically qualified or hold an approved waiver per DAFMAN 48-123; AF Form 422 required and valid for 12 months (§4.1.6).
5. **Physical Fitness & Body Composition:** Applicants must meet fitness requirements of DAFMAN 36-2905 and height/weight standards per DoDI 1308.03 (§4.1.6.1). (T-0)
6. **Dress and Appearance:** Applicants must comply with DAFI 36-2903 (§4.1.6.2).
7. **Pregnancy Restrictions:** Members may apply while pregnant but cannot complete WOTS training while pregnant or within 6 months postpartum (20+ weeks) without physician approval and AETC/SG concurrence (§4.1.7).
8. **Education:** Applicants must be high school graduates, covered graduates, or hold alternate credentials (GED or minimum 12 semester hours / 22.5 quarter hours of college credit) (§4.1.8).
9. **Family Care Plan:** Single, divorced, separated applicants with dependents or those married to a military/common-law spouse must have a commander-approved DAF Form 357 per DoDI 1342.19_DAFI 36-2908 (§4.1.9). (T-3)
10. **Drug & Alcohol Certification:** All accession applicants must complete AF Form 2030 unless currently holding status in an air component (§4.1.10). (T-1)
11. **Security Clearance:** Applicants may apply with a Secret clearance but must initiate procedures for Top Secret/SCI access; certain specialties may require a CI polygraph (§4.1.13).
12. **Inter-Service Transfer:** IST applicants are separated from their losing service and originally appointed in the AF without interrupting total service; ADSCs and financial obligations from the losing service must be honored (§4.1.14–4.1.14.2). (T-0)
13. **Records Management:** All records created under this AFGM must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

**(a) Unclear Authority Assignments:**
- **§3.2** states WO appointments are made by the President "who further delegated authority to the Secretary of War"—an obsolete title last used in 1947. It is unclear whether this refers to the Secretary of Defense, the Secretary of the Air Force, or is simply an uncorrected historical reference carried from Army regulations. This creates ambiguity about current appointment authority.
- The OPR is listed as "Unknown" in publication metadata yet Chapter 1 references AF/A1, AF/RE, and NGB/CF for policy questions without explicitly designating a single OPR for the AFGM itself.

**(b) Topics Implied but Not Fully Addressed:**
- The AFGM implements DAFPDs on **evaluations (36-24), promotions (36-25), military standards (36-29), entitlements (36-30), and retirements/separations (36-32)**, yet the available text covers only accessions and grade structure. Promotion criteria, evaluation forms/processes, WO-specific conduct standards, entitlement details, and retirement/separation procedures are not addressed in the extracted text—these may appear in unextracted portions but represent significant lifecycle gaps if absent.
- **WOTS curriculum, duration, and location** are referenced but not described.
- **Duty positions, billet structure, and force management** for the 17W/17Y career fields are not detailed.
- **Non-prior-service applicant pipeline** (§4.1.12) is stated as open but no specific accession pathway, training sequence, or prerequisite military experience is defined.

**(c) Potential Conflicts:**
- The one-year expiration clause creates a risk that if no permanent publication is issued by February 2027, the entire WO program loses its policy basis, potentially stranding personnel mid-career cycle with no governing instruction.
- IST provisions (§4.1.14.3) regarding promotion list integration by date of rank may conflict with AF-specific promotion board sequencing if WO promotion timelines differ across services.
