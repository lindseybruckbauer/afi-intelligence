# AFI 31-213: Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards and Off-Installation Liaison and Operations

**OPR:** Unknown | **Effective:** 27 July 2006 (Certified Current: 23 April 2026) | **Implements:** Not specified | **Supersedes:** Not specified

## Purpose & Scope
AFI 31-213 is a joint-service publication (also designated AR 190-24, OPNAVINST 1620.2A, MCO 1620.2D, and COMDTINST 1620.1E) that governs the establishment and operation of Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards (AFDCBs) and provides policy for off-installation liaison and operations conducted by military police and law enforcement personnel. It applies to the Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, and Coast Guard, addressing coordination with civilian authorities and the designation of off-limits establishments or areas that pose a threat to the health, safety, morale, or welfare of military personnel.

## Authority & Responsibilities
- **Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards (AFDCBs):** Responsible for advising installation commanders and coordinating among the military services regarding off-installation conditions that may adversely affect military personnel.
- **Installation Commanders:** Authorized to act on AFDCB recommendations, including placing establishments or areas off-limits to military personnel.
- **Military Police / Security Forces:** Responsible for off-installation liaison, coordination with civilian law enforcement, and enforcement of off-limits designations.

## Key Requirements
1. Establishment and operation of Armed Forces Disciplinary Control Boards in areas with significant military populations, involving multi-service coordination.
2. Coordination with civilian law enforcement and local authorities for off-installation liaison and operations.
3. Procedures for designating establishments or areas as off-limits to military personnel when conditions threaten health, safety, morale, or welfare.
4. Compliance with joint-service policy across all five branches (Army, Navy, Air Force, Marine Corps, Coast Guard).
