# AFH 36-2647: COMPETENCY MODELING

**OPR:** AETC/A3J | **Effective:** 8 February 2022 (Incorporating Change 1, 8 July 2025) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-26, Total Force Development and Management; DAFMAN 36-2689, Training Program | **Supersedes:** AFH 36-2647, 19 April 2019

## Purpose & Scope

This handbook provides reference material and instructional guidance on how and when competency models are planned, built, and executed in support of the USAF mission (§1.1). It covers both foundational competencies (applicable to all Airmen) and occupational competencies (career-field-specific). The publication applies to Air Force military and civilian personnel, Air National Guard, and Air Force Reserve. It does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. As a handbook (AFH), it is informational/instructional rather than directive.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR (Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Manpower and Reserve Affairs):** Functions as the Air Force's lead proponent for competency modeling and serves as the approving official for DAF-level competency modeling policy (§2.1). Also serves as certifying authority for this publication.
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Facilitates the Air Force's ability to identify, develop, measure, and track competencies across the service; serves as certifying authority for departmental-level competency modeling policy (§2.2).
- **AETC/CC (Commander, Air Education and Training Command):** Develops and executes competency-modeling policies, plans, programs, processes, and activities necessary to guide, measure, implement, assess, and oversee all aspects of competency modeling for the USAF (§2.3).
- **AETC/A3J (Force Development Competencies Division):** Serves as the OPR and collaborates with functional authorities, career field managers, and functional managers to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4).
- **Functional authorities, career field managers, and functional managers:** Collaborate with AETC/A3J to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4).
- **SECAF, CSAF, and CMSAF:** Have full authority to make changes to foundational competencies (§3.2.2.1).
- **AF Futures:** Has delegated authority to identify competencies for inclusion within foundational competencies based on future needs of the USAF (§3.2.2.2).

## Key Requirements

1. Competency models must describe observable and measurable patterns of knowledge, skills, abilities, and other characteristics (KSAOs) required for mission success (§1.1).
2. Occupational competency models are applicable to all components (RegAF, AFR, ANG) within a functional community (§1.2).
3. Foundational competencies are universally applicable to all Airmen (officer, enlisted, civilian) and are organized into four categories: Developing Self, Developing Others, Developing Ideas, and Developing Organizations (§3.2.3).
4. Occupational competency model development follows a distinct process requiring continued involvement of career field subject matter experts (§3.2.4.1).
5. Foundational competency development followed a four-phase scientific validation approach including literature review, behavioral event interviews, elite-performer surveys, and representative-sample validation (§3.2.1.1–3.2.1.4).
6. Supplements to this publication at any level must be reviewed by the OPR prior to certification, approval, and implementation.
7. All records generated from processes in this publication must adhere to AFI 33-322, Records Management and Information Governance Program.
8. Change 1 (8 July 2025) revised content to comply with Executive Orders 14151, 14148, and 14168, resulting in deletion of Attachments 3–7.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

- **(a) Unclear authority boundaries:** The relationship between SAF/MR as "approving official" for DAF-level policy (§2.1) and AF/A1 as "certifying authority" for departmental-level policy (§2.2) uses similar terminology without clear delineation of where one authority ends and the other begins.
- **(b) Deleted attachments without replacement:** Attachments 3–7 were deleted per Change 1 (EO compliance), but no replacement guidance or redirect is provided, leaving potential gaps in content those attachments previously covered.
- **(c) Enforcement/compliance ambiguity:** As a handbook (AFH) rather than an instruction (AFI), the document is informational. It is unclear which elements, if any, carry mandatory compliance weight versus serving purely as guidance, particularly regarding the occupational competency model development process.
- **(d) Space Force exclusion noted but not explained:** The publication explicitly excludes USSF but does not indicate whether a separate USSF competency modeling publication exists or is planned.
