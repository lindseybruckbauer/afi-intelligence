# AFH 36-2647: Competency Modeling

**OPR:** AETC/A3J | **Effective:** 8 February 2022 (Incorporating Change 1, 8 July 2025) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-26, DAFMAN 36-2689 | **Supersedes:** AFH 36-2647, 19 April 2019

## Purpose & Scope

This handbook provides reference material and instructional guidance on how and when competency models are planned, built, and executed in support of the USAF mission (§1.1). Competency models are organized collections of competencies describing observable and measurable patterns of knowledge, skills, abilities, and other characteristics (KSAOs) required for mission success. The publication applies to Air Force military and civilian personnel, Air National Guard, and Air Force Reserve. It does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Change 1 (8 July 2025) revises the handbook to comply with Executive Orders 14151, 14148, and 14168, and updates references for currency.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR (Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Manpower and Reserve Affairs):** Functions as the Air Force's lead proponent for competency modeling and approving official for DAF-level competency modeling policy (§2.1). Also serves as certifying authority for this publication.
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Facilitates the Air Force's ability to identify, develop, measure, and track competencies across the service; serves as certifying authority for departmental-level competency modeling policy (§2.2).
- **AETC/CC (Commander, Air Education and Training Command):** Develops and executes competency-modeling policies, plans, programs, processes, and other activities necessary to guide, measure, implement, assess, and oversee all aspects of competency modeling for the USAF (§2.3).
- **AETC/A3J (Force Development Competencies Division):** Collaborates with functional authorities, career field managers, and functional managers to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4). Serves as OPR for this publication.
- **Functional Authorities, Career Field Managers, and Functional Managers:** Collaborate with AETC/A3J to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4).
- **SECAF, CSAF, and CMSAF:** Have full authority to make changes to foundational competencies (§3.2.2.1).
- **AF Futures:** Has delegated authority to identify competencies for inclusion within foundational competencies based on future needs of the USAF (§3.2.2.2).

## Key Requirements

1. Competency models must describe observable and measurable patterns of KSAOs required for successful mission performance (§1.1).
2. Foundational competencies are universally applicable to all Airmen (officer, enlisted, and civilian) and serve as the underpinning construct of force development (§3.1.2.1).
3. Occupational competency models are applicable to all components (RegAF, AFR, ANG) within a functional community and must describe competencies for a specific workforce category (§1.2, §3.1.2.2).
4. Occupational competency model development must follow a distinct process with continued involvement of career field subject matter experts (§3.2.4.1).
5. Foundational competencies are organized into four categories: Developing Self, Developing Others, Developing Ideas, and Developing Organizations, each identified by label, definition, proficiency level/criteria, and observable behaviors (§3.2.3).
6. Foundational competency development follows a four-phased scientific approach including literature review, behavioral event interviews, validated surveys, and population-wide validation (§3.2.1.1–3.2.1.4).
7. Supplements to this publication at any level must be reviewed by the OPR prior to certification, approval, and implementation.
8. All records generated from processes in this publication must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the AF Records Disposition Schedule.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Deleted Attachments (3–7):** Attachments 3 through 7 are marked "(DELETED)" with no explanation of what content was removed or where it was relocated, creating potential gaps in guidance that previously existed.
2. **AF Futures delegation unclear:** Section 3.2.2.2 states AF Futures has "delegated authority" to identify foundational competencies based on future needs but does not specify who delegated this authority, the scope of that delegation, or the approval process for proposed additions.
3. **Measurement and tracking mechanisms:** While AF/A1 is tasked to facilitate the ability to "measure and track" competencies (§2.2), the available text does not detail specific systems, metrics, or reporting requirements for doing so.
4. **Handbook vs. directive authority:** As a handbook (AFH), this publication provides guidance and reference material but does not impose mandatory compliance obligations, which may limit enforceability of the competency modeling processes described.
