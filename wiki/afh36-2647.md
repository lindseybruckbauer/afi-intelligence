# AFH 36-2647: COMPETENCY MODELING

**OPR:** AETC/A3J | **Effective:** 8 February 2022 (Incorporating Change 1, 8 July 2025) | **Implements:** DAFPD 36-26, DAFMAN 36-2689 | **Supersedes:** AFH 36-2647, 19 April 2019

## Purpose & Scope

This handbook provides reference material and instructional guidance on how and when competency models are planned, built, and executed in support of the USAF mission (§1.1). It covers both foundational competencies (applicable to all Airmen—officer, enlisted, and civilian) and occupational competencies (specific to career fields or workforce categories). The publication applies to Air Force military and civilian personnel, Air National Guard, and Air Force Reserve; it does **not** apply to the United States Space Force. Change 1 (8 July 2025) revised the handbook to comply with Executive Orders 14151, 14148, and 14168 and updated references for currency.

## Authority & Responsibilities

- **SAF/MR (Assistant Secretary of the Air Force for Manpower and Reserve Affairs):** Functions as the Air Force's lead proponent for competency modeling and is the approving official for DAF-level competency modeling policy (§2.1). Also serves as the certifying authority for this publication.
- **AF/A1 (Deputy Chief of Staff for Manpower, Personnel, and Services):** Facilitates the Air Force's ability to identify, develop, measure, and track competencies across the service; serves as certifying authority for departmental-level competency modeling policy (§2.2).
- **AETC/CC (Commander, Air Education and Training Command):** Develops and executes competency-modeling policies, plans, programs, processes, and other activities necessary to guide, measure, implement, assess, and oversee all aspects of competency modeling for the USAF (§2.3).
- **AETC/A3J (Force Development Competencies Division):** Collaborates with functional authorities, career field managers, and functional managers to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4); serves as OPR for this publication.
- **Functional Authorities, Career Field Managers, and Functional Managers:** Collaborate with AETC/A3J to establish and modify occupational competency models (§2.4).
- **SECAF, CSAF, and CMSAF:** Have full authority to make changes to foundational competencies (§3.2.2.1).
- **AF Futures:** Has delegated authority to identify competencies for inclusion within foundational competencies based on future needs of the USAF (§3.2.2.2).

## Key Requirements

1. Competency models must describe observable and measurable patterns of Knowledge, Skills, Abilities, and Other characteristics (KSAOs) required for successful mission performance (§1.1).
2. Occupational competency models are applicable to all components (RegAF, AFR, ANG) within a functional community and must be developed in collaboration with AETC/A3J and career field SMEs (§1.2, §2.4, §3.2.4.1).
3. Foundational competencies are organized into four categories: Developing Self, Developing Others, Developing Ideas, and Developing Organizations—each identified by label, definition, proficiency level/criteria, and observable behaviors (§3.2.3).
4. Foundational competency development must follow a validated scientific methodology (four-phased approach including literature review, behavioral event interviews, survey validation, and psychometric reduction) (§3.2.1.1–3.2.1.4).
5. Competency models must be used within the context of total force development to inform assignment, classification, learning and development, recruitment, retention, and other talent management policies (§1.1.1).
6. Competency models must enable Airmen to identify competency gaps and determine education, training, and experience needs (§1.1.2).
7. All records generated from processes in this publication must adhere to AFI 33-322 and the Air Force Records Disposition Schedule.
8. Supplements to this publication at any level must be reviewed by the OPR prior to certification, approval, and implementation.

## Gaps & Ambiguities

1. **Deleted attachments without explanation:** Attachments 3–7 are all marked "(DELETED)" with no indication of whether their content was relocated, superseded, or rendered obsolete. This creates potential gaps in guidance that previously existed in the 2019 version.
2. **Unclear delineation between AF/A1 and SAF/MR policy authority:** SAF/MR is described as the "approving official" for DAF-level policy (§2.1), while AF/A1 is the "certifying authority" for "departmental-level" policy (§2.2). The distinction between "DAF-level" and "departmental-level" competency modeling policy is not defined, creating potential overlap.
3. **AF Futures' delegated authority scope undefined:** Section 3.2.2.2 states AF Futures has "delegated authority to identify competencies" for foundational models but does not specify who delegated this authority, what approval process applies, or how conflicts with SECAF/CSAF/CMSAF authority (§3.2.2.1) are resolved.
4. **AETC crowdsourcing mechanism unspecified:** Section 3.2.2.3 references AETC's authority to address items for foundational competencies "through crowdsourcing" but provides no procedural framework, validation criteria, or governance for this process.
5. **No compliance/enforcement mechanisms:** As a handbook (AFH) rather than an instruction (AFI), the publication provides guidance but the enforceability of its procedures relative to the implementing DAFPD and DAFMAN is not clearly articulated.
